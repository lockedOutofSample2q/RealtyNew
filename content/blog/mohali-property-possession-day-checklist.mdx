When considering **mohali property possession day checklist**, it is essential to understand the underlying market dynamics. Property possession in Mohali represents the final transition from a contractual agreement to physical ownership, but it is also the buyer’s last point of significant leverage. On possession day, your primary checklist must include the verification of the Occupation Certificate (OC), a comprehensive snagging inspection of all electrical and plumbing systems, and a detailed audit of common area amenities. You must refuse to sign any "Satisfaction Letter" or "No-Dues Certificate" that waives your right to claim repairs for construction defects identified during the walkthrough. Under RERA Punjab guidelines, developers are liable for structural defects for five years, yet accepting possession without a recorded snag list can complicate future legal claims. This guide outlines the exact technical and legal steps required to safeguard your investment in the Mohali real estate market, ensuring that the asset you receive matches the promise made at the time of booking.

## The Significance of the Occupation Certificate in Mohali

The most critical document in your possession journey is the Occupation Certificate (OC). In Mohali, whether you are dealing with a project in Aerocity, IT City, or a private developer in Sector 82A, the OC is the only legal proof that the building is habitable and complies with the sanctioned building plans of the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority (GMADA) or the Punjab Urban Planning and Development Authority (PUDA).

According to reports from The Tribune and Economic Times, many projects in the New Chandigarh and Mullanpur regions have attempted "paper possession" without obtaining a valid OC. Taking possession of a unit in a building that lacks this certificate is a legal risk that can lead to several complications. Without an OC, you cannot obtain a permanent electricity connection from the Punjab State Power Corporation Limited (PSPCL). You will be forced to rely on temporary construction-phase electricity, which is significantly more expensive and prone to fluctuations. Furthermore, registering the property in your name at the Tehsil office requires the OC as a prerequisite in most cases. Always insist on seeing the original, stamped OC from the relevant Municipal Committee or GMADA before proceeding with the handover.

## The Physical Inspection: Creating a Professional Snag List

A snag list is a formal document that records every construction defect, minor or major, within your unit. In Mohali's competitive market, builders often rush the possession process to avoid the "delay compensation" penalties mandated by RERA Punjab. As a buyer, your duty is to slow the process down and conduct a meticulous inspection.

### Electrical and Safety Audit
Carry a digital multimeter or a simple mobile charger to test every socket in the apartment. Ensure that the distribution board (DB) is clearly labeled and that the Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB) is functional. Check the wiring for any loose connections, particularly in the balcony and kitchen areas where moisture is prevalent. On our YouTube channel @Amritrealty, we often demonstrate how faulty wiring in new constructions can lead to long term maintenance headaches if not caught during the initial walkthrough.

### Plumbing and Seepage Verification
Turn on all taps and flush all toilets simultaneously to check the water pressure. Observe the drainage speed in the kitchen and bathrooms: slow drainage often indicates construction debris stuck in the pipes. Inspect the walls adjacent to bathrooms for any signs of dampness or "shora" (efflorescence). In the Mohali climate, seepage is a recurring issue, especially in projects where the waterproofing was hurried during the monsoon season. Use a dry tissue to wipe under the kitchen sink and bathroom vanities to detect slow leaks that might not be visible to the naked eye.

### Flooring and Joinery
Tap on every floor tile with a small wooden stick or a marble. A hollow sound indicates a lack of proper cement-sand mortar underneath, which will eventually lead to the tile cracking or lifting. Check the alignment of all doors and windows. In many luxury projects in Mohali, UPVC windows are standard: ensure that the weather-stripping is intact to prevent dust and noise pollution from entering the home. If the doors do not shut smoothly or if the locks are misaligned, these must be recorded as snags for the builder to rectify before you move in.

## Documentation: What You Must Carry and What You Must Collect

Possession day involves a heavy exchange of paperwork. You should arrive prepared with copies of your allotment letter, all payment receipts, and your Aadhaar or PAN card. However, the more important aspect is what you walk away with.

### The Handover Folder
Ensure you receive the following documents in their original form:
1.  **The Possession Letter:** This is the formal transfer of the property.
2.  **The Handover Certificate:** A document where you acknowledge the receipt of keys.
3.  **The Maintenance Agreement:** A contract with the agency responsible for the upkeep of the project.
4.  **Warranties for Fixtures:** Original warranty cards and manuals for geysers, air conditioners, kitchen chimneys, or any other appliances provided by the developer.
5.  **Utility Transfer Documents:** Papers required to transfer the electricity meter and water connection to your name.

### Meter Readings and Utility IDs
Take timestamped photographs of the electricity and water meters at the exact moment of handover. This prevents the developer from billing you for electricity consumed during the final stages of construction or cleaning. In multi-floor buildings in Mohali, ensure that your unit has a separate Property Tax ID (UID). Without a separate ID, you will face difficulties in obtaining a No-Dues Certificate (NDC) from the Municipal Committee during future resale. For more on this, refer to our pillar post on [property registration in Mohali](/blog/property-registration-mohali-2026-stamp-duty).

## Common Areas and Infrastructure Audit

Your investment is not limited to the four walls of your apartment; it extends to the shared infrastructure of the society. Before signing the final papers, walk through the common areas.

### Lifts and Fire Safety
Check if the lifts have a valid "License to Operate" from the Punjab Electrical Inspectorate. Test the intercom system between the lift car and the security desk. Inspect the fire extinguishers and hydrants on your floor to ensure they are pressurized and have not exceeded their expiry dates. Safety is a non-negotiable aspect of any [buyer protection strategy](/blog/mohali-property-buyer-protection-guide).

### Parking and Storage
Locate your designated parking spot as mentioned in your allotment letter. Measure the dimensions to ensure it can comfortably accommodate your vehicle. In many Mohali sectors, parking disputes are common because developers often reconfigure parking layouts after the initial sales phase. If you have been promised a dedicated storage space or a servant room, verify its condition and location as well.

## What to Refuse to Sign: Protecting Your Legal Rights

This is the most critical part of the day. Developers often present a "Satisfaction Letter" or a "No-Dues Certificate" and insist that you sign it before they hand over the keys. 

### The Trap of the "Satisfaction Letter"
A Satisfaction Letter usually contains a clause stating that you have inspected the property and found it to be in perfect condition, waiving your right to future claims. **Do not sign this if you have found snags.** If the developer's staff refuses to hand over the keys without this signature, you should write "Possession taken under protest, subject to the attached snag list" on the document itself. This creates a legal record that the possession was conditional upon the rectification of the defects you identified.

### Indemnity Bonds
Some developers ask buyers to sign indemnity bonds that absolve the builder of any liability regarding future structural issues. This is a direct violation of Section 14(3) of the Real Estate (Regulation and Development) Act, 2016 (RERA), which mandates a five-year structural defect liability period. You are not legally obligated to sign any document that contradicts the protections provided by RERA Punjab. If you encounter such pressure, it is advisable to consult a [real estate advisor](/blog/real-estate-broker-vs-consultant-mohali-difference) who understands the local legal landscape.

## The Post-Possession Transition

Once you have the keys, the work does not stop. You must initiate the process of "Mutation" or "Intkal" in the revenue records. While the possession letter gives you physical control, the mutation ensures that your name is reflected as the owner in the government records (Jamabandi). This is a separate process from the registration of the sale deed and is essential for the legal completeness of your title. You can read more about this in our guide on [property mutation in Mohali](/blog/property-mutation-intkal-mohali-process).

Furthermore, join the Resident Welfare Association (RWA) or the Apartment Owners Association (AOA) as soon as it is formed. In projects like those in Aerocity or Mullanpur, the AOA plays a vital role in holding the developer accountable for the maintenance of common services like the sewage treatment plant (STP) and the backup power generators.

## Summary Checklist for Possession Day in Mohali

To ensure you don't miss anything in the heat of the moment, here is a condensed checklist:

*   **Verify OC/CC:** Check original documents from GMADA/PUDA/MC.
*   **Snagging:** Carry a charger, tape, marble, and camera.
*   **Plumbing:** Check pressure, drainage, and leakage under sinks.
*   **Electrical:** Test every socket, ELCB, and DB labeling.
*   **Joinery:** Open/close all doors and windows; check locks.
*   **Documentation:** Collect Possession Letter, Maintenance Agreement, and Warranties.
*   **Meter Readings:** Take photos of electricity and water meters.
*   **Legal:** Refuse to sign unconditional "Satisfaction Letters" if snags exist.
*   **Utility IDs:** Ensure separate UID for property tax and separate PSPCL meter.
