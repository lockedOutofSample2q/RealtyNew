# Property Possession in Mohali: The Checklist to Carry on Possession Day and What to Refuse to Sign

Possession day is the final transition from buyer to owner, but in Mohali’s real estate market, it is also your last point of significant leverage. Before accepting the keys, you must verify the Occupation Certificate (OC) or Completion Certificate (CC) to ensure the building is legally habitable. Your primary task is creating a detailed snag list: documenting every construction defect, from hollow floor tiles to plumbing leaks, with timestamped photos and videos. Crucially, refuse to sign any "no-objection" or "full-satisfaction" letter that waives your right to future repairs if the developer has not yet resolved these snags. Under RERA Punjab, you have a 5-year structural defect liability period, but accepting possession without a recorded snag list makes enforcement much harder. Having navigated project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and various Municipal Committees, I have seen where the gaps are: and this guide is built on those gaps to protect your investment.

## Why is the Occupation Certificate (OC) your most important document?

In Mohali, whether you are in a GMADA sector or a private licensed colony, the Occupation Certificate (OC) is the only proof that the building has been constructed according to the sanctioned plans and is safe for occupancy. Without an OC, your building is technically unauthorized. You might face issues with permanent electricity connections from PSPCL or, worse, challenges during resale.

Always ask to see the original OC issued by the relevant authority: be it GMADA or the local Municipal Committee. Do not settle for "we have applied for it." Taking possession of a unit in a building without an OC is a risk I rarely advise my clients to take. It leaves you vulnerable to regulatory penalties and infrastructure gaps that the builder may never fix once the majority of buyers have moved in. This is a core part of any [buyer protection strategy](/blog/mohali-property-buyer-protection-guide).

## How to create a professional snag list on possession day?

A "snag" is any defect or unfinished work in your property. Builders are often in a rush to hand over possession to stop the delay penalties they might be paying under RERA. You must be the one to slow things down. Carry a notebook, a measuring tape, a mobile charger (to test sockets), and a marble (to test floor slope). Document everything with photos and video.

### The Interior Checklist:
*   **Flooring and Skirting:** Tap on every single tile, especially in the center and corners, to check for a hollow sound. This indicates poor cement-sand bonding.
*   **Walls and Paint:** Look for dampness (seepage) near bathrooms and external walls. Use your hand to feel for cold spots or bubbling paint.
*   **Electrical Points:** Plug your charger into every socket and check if the indicator light comes on. Test the two-way switches for bedrooms.
*   **Plumbing:** Turn on every tap simultaneously to check for water pressure drops. Inspect the area under the kitchen sink with a dry tissue for slow leaks.
*   **Doors and Windows:** Open and close every door and window multiple times. Check the weather-stripping on UPVC windows to ensure air conditioning efficiency.

### The External and Common Area Checklist:
Do not ignore the areas outside your front door. Your property value is tied to the common infrastructure. Check the lift's functioning and inspect the state of the lobby and the fire-fighting equipment on your floor. Ensure the parking spot assigned to you matches the dimensions in your allotment letter. If you are in a multi-floor building, refer to our [process and paperwork guide](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026) to understand how shared spaces are managed.

## What documents must you collect before leaving the site?

You should leave the site with a physical or digital folder containing the following:
1.  **Possession Letter:** The formal document stating that the developer has handed over the property.
2.  **Occupation Certificate (OC):** A copy of the OC issued by GMADA or the Municipal Committee.
3.  **No-Dues Certificate (NOC):** A document from the builder’s accounts department confirming you have no outstanding dues.
4.  **Maintenance Agreement:** A signed copy of the agreement with the Maintenance Agency.
5.  **Keys:** All sets, including master keys and spares for all internal doors.
6.  **Warranty Documents:** For every fixture provided, such as geysers, ACs, and kitchen chimneys.
7.  **Meter Readings:** Take timestamped photos of the electricity and water meters.

## What should you refuse to sign on possession day?

This is where the real negotiation happens. Builders often present a stack of papers and ask you to sign them in a hurry. One of these will likely be a "Satisfaction Letter" or a "No-Objection Certificate" in favor of the developer. Never sign a document that says you are "fully satisfied with the quality of construction" if you have found even one snag.

Builders use these letters in RERA courts to prove that the buyer accepted the property without any complaints. If they refuse to hand over the keys without this signature, write "Possession taken under protest subject to the attached snag list" in bold and initial every page of the snag list. This creates a legal record that the possession was conditional. This paper trail is what wins cases in [real estate disputes in Punjab](/blog/real-estate-disputes-legal-rights-punjab-2026).

## The Importance of Separate Utility IDs

In my experience resolving property disputes, many headaches stem from shared utility IDs. In the "Sandeep Case," we dealt with a multi-floor building where the property tax and electricity were not properly separated at the time of possession. It took three months of coordination with the Municipal Committee and PSPCL to generate separate IDs and meters. 

If you are buying a floor in a 3-storey building, ensure that your electricity meter is in the process of being transferred to your name and that you have a separate property tax ID. Without this, your resale value will be severely impacted, and you will find it nearly impossible to get an [NDC from the Municipal Committee](/blog/municipal-committee-property-tax-mohali-ndc) later. Always insist on seeing the [PSPCL load sanction letter](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-pspcl) for your specific unit.

### FAQ: Common Questions on Possession Day

### Can I take possession if the lift is not working?
Technically yes, but you should document it as a major snag. Ensure the builder gives a written timeline for the lift's certification and operation before you sign the handover documents.

### What if the electricity meter is still in the builder's name?
This is common during the initial handover phase. However, you must ensure that you are not being billed at construction-phase rates. Demand a separate [PSPCL connection for your floor](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-pspcl) as soon as possible.
