Prelaunch pricing in Mohali represents a genuine discount only when the all-in acquisition cost is at least 15 percent lower than the current registered transaction prices of comparable, RERA-approved inventory in the same micro-market. To determine if an "Early Bird" offer is a mathematical discount or a marketing markup, you must calculate the total cost including External Development Charges (EDC), Infrastructure Development Charges (IDC), Interest Free Maintenance Security (IFMS), and Preferential Location Charges (PLC) against the base selling price. In the current 2026 market, many prelaunch offers on Airport Road or PR7 are marketed at "base prices" that appear low but equalize with ready-to-move rates once statutory costs and GST are factored in. A true prelaunch discount must compensate the investor for the risk of capital lock-in and the absence of an immediate RERA registration number, which is legally required for any public marketing or sale.

## The Anatomy of a Mohali Prelaunch Offer

The term "prelaunch" in the Punjab real estate context often refers to the period before a developer has obtained the final RERA registration number but has secured the Change of Land Use (CLU) and initial layout approvals. From a regulatory standpoint under RERA Punjab Section 3, no developer can advertise or sell without a registration number. However, the market continues to operate through "expression of interest" (EOI) or "soft launch" mechanisms.

For a High Net Worth Individual (HNI), the appeal is the entry price. If a project in Sector 82A or IT City is expected to launch at Rs 8,500 per square foot, a prelaunch offer at Rs 7,200 looks like a clear win. Yet, the price on the brochure is rarely the price on the cheque. To perform a real cost calculation, you must peel back the layers of the developer payment plan.

## The Real Cost Calculation: Moving Beyond Base Price

In Mohali, the gap between the marketed "Base Selling Price" (BSP) and the "All-in Cost" can range from 18 percent to 25 percent. When evaluating an early bird offer, use this framework to find the actual investment figure:

1. **Statutory Charges (EDC and IDC):** These are passed from the government to the developer and then to the buyer. In premium Mohali sectors, these can add Rs 400 to Rs 600 per square foot. Ensure you ask if the prelaunch price is "inclusive" or "exclusive" of these.
2. **Preferential Location Charges (PLC):** For corner units, park-facing views, or higher floors (floor rise), PLCs can add 5 to 10 percent to the base price. Many prelaunch "discounts" only apply to non-PLC units.
3. **Possession Linked Charges:** IFMS, club membership, and power backup installation are often billed at the time of possession. These are capital outflows that must be discounted back to today's value to understand your real ROI.
4. **GST Implications:** Under current norms, under-construction property attracts 5 percent GST (without Input Tax Credit) for non-affordable housing. This is a pure cost that does not exist in the same way for secondary market transactions where the Occupation Certificate (OC) is already issued.

## Benchmarking Against Registered Transactions

The most effective way to verify a discount is to stop looking at developer brochures and start looking at the RERA Punjab portal. Every registered project is required to file quarterly updates that include the number of units sold and, crucially, the price points.

Compare the proposed prelaunch price with the last three months of registered sales in the same sector. If a neighboring project with a similar delivery timeline is registering sales at Rs 7,800 per square foot and your "prelaunch" offer is Rs 7,500, the 4 percent delta is insufficient. You are effectively taking 100 percent of the development risk for a 4 percent gain, which is a poor trade for an HNI investor.

As we often discuss on our YouTube channel @Amritrealty, the market price is not what is quoted on the billboard: it is what is recorded at the Tehsil office. A professional advisor looks at the circle rate versus the market rate to identify where the "fat" in the pricing lies.

## The "Soft Launch" Risk Premium

Investing in a prelaunch project in Punjab involves two distinct types of risk: execution risk and regulatory risk. The risk premium is the "extra" discount you should demand for taking these risks.

In Mohali, the Forest Department approvals or PSPCL load sanctions can sometimes delay a project by 12 to 18 months. If your capital is locked in a project that is delayed, your "discounted" entry price is being eroded by the opportunity cost of that capital. If you could have earned 7 to 8 percent in a liquid debt instrument, a project delayed by two years effectively costs you 15 percent more than you planned.

Therefore, a genuine prelaunch discount should be at least 15 to 20 percent below the anticipated launch price to provide a safety buffer. Anything less is simply the developer outsourcing their financing costs to you at a rate cheaper than a bank loan.

## Red Flags: When the Early Bird Price is an Illusion

Not all price increases are organic. Developers often use "artificial appreciation" to create a sense of urgency. You might see a price hike of Rs 200 per square foot announced every 30 days. This is a common tactic in the New Chandigarh and Aerocity expansion zones.

To tell if this is real:
* **Check Inventory Levels:** Is the developer actually selling out, or are the "sold" units being held by investors for resale? If there is high "under-construction resale" inventory, the developer's price hike is unsustainable.
* **Verify the Construction Link:** A real price increase usually follows a construction milestone, such as the completion of the basement or the casting of the first slab. If the price is rising while the site remains a vacant lot, the "appreciation" is purely on paper.
* **Developer Track Record:** Research if the developer has a history of "stalling" after the initial prelaunch collection. Use our [Mohali developer due diligence checklist](/blog/mohali-developer-project-due-diligence-checklist) to vet the entity behind the offer.

## The Role of Location in Price Justification

A prelaunch price in a stagnant sector is a liability. However, in high-growth corridors like the Airport Road or the upcoming Bharatmala connectivity zones, the pricing logic changes. For instance, commercial units in Sector 82 area that were available at Rs 3-4 crore during their early phases now trade upwards of Rs 12-16 crore. In these cases, the "prelaunch" was not just a discount; it was an entry into a supply-constrained micro-market.

When we advise HNI clients at RHMC, we look for "Vision Investing." As I often say: "Je aapaan vision ton bina challaange taan aapaan mehngi cheez khareeddaange." (If we move without vision, we will buy at the wrong price). A prelaunch price is only "cheap" if the location has the infrastructure legs to run for the next five years.

## Summary Checklist for Early Bird Offers

Before signing an EOI in Mohali, run this three-point audit:
1. **The RERA Comparison:** Is the price at least 15 percent lower than the nearest registered, RERA-approved project of similar grade?
2. **The All-in Math:** Does the price include EDC, IDC, and PLC, or are these "hidden" in the fine print?
3. **The Escrow Question:** Is the developer committing to placing the prelaunch funds into a dedicated account that will be converted to a RERA escrow account once the registration is received?

If the answer to any of these is "No," the early bird price is likely a marketing tool rather than a financial advantage. Professional real estate investment is about the clinical calculation of the "entry-to-exit" spread. In a market like Mohali, where supply is expanding rapidly toward Rajpura and Banur, price discipline is your only protection against stagnation.
