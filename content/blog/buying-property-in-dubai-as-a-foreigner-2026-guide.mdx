Dubai remains one of the most accessible property markets for international buyers, but accessibility is not the same as simplicity.

If you are purchasing remotely or for the first time, the process is straightforward only when your due diligence is structured.

## 1) Confirm the Property Is in a Freehold Area

Foreign nationals can buy in designated freehold zones such as:

- Dubai Marina
- Downtown Dubai
- Palm Jumeirah
- Jumeirah Village Circle
- Business Bay
- Dubai Hills Estate

Before paying a reservation amount, verify the exact project status and title eligibility through official documents and the developer's registration records.

## 2) Budget the Full Transaction Cost, Not Just the Price

Most first-time buyers underestimate closing costs. A typical acquisition includes:

- Dubai Land Department transfer fee: **4%**
- Trustee office/admin costs
- Agency fee (often 2% + VAT in resale)
- Mortgage arrangement and valuation fees (if financed)

Rule of thumb: reserve **6% to 8%** on top of purchase price for a safer budget.

## 3) Understand the Difference Between Off-Plan and Resale

**Off-plan** can reduce upfront capital through staged payments, but you accept delivery risk and timeline uncertainty.

**Resale** gives immediate title transfer and rental visibility, but requires larger upfront cash in most cases.

Neither is universally better. Your choice should match your timeline, risk tolerance, and liquidity profile.

## 4) Run Legal and Developer Checks Before Signing

At minimum, complete these checks:

1. RERA registration and project status
2. Sales and Purchase Agreement terms (payment schedule, delay clauses, penalties)
3. Building service charge history
4. Existing encumbrances or outstanding obligations

A polished brochure is marketing. The contract is reality.

## 5) Prepare the Payment Path Early

Cross-border transfers can delay deals, especially when compliance teams request additional source-of-funds documentation.

Prepare these in advance:

- Passport and address documents
- Proof of funds and bank letters
- Power of attorney structure if buying remotely

Deals are often lost in the final 72 hours because buyers treat operations as an afterthought.

## 6) Plan the Asset Strategy Before Completion

Decide before signing whether the property is for:

- Long-term rental income
- Medium-term hold and resale
- Personal use with optional rental fallback

Your strategy affects unit type, layout priority, furnishing budget, and expected return.
