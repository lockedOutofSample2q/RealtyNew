When considering **mohali property advisor NRI trust questions**, it is essential to understand the underlying market dynamics. To trust a Mohali property advisor with an NRI investment, you must ask 10 specific questions: How do you earn your fee? Can you show a project you recommended against? What is your post-sale support for MC tax and NDC? How do you verify RERA filings beyond the registration number? Do you provide live video site visits? What is your experience with GMADA and PSPCL liaisoning? How many NRI transactions have you closed this year? Can you provide anonymised case studies of dispute resolution? What is your checklist for Jamabandi and CLU verification? And finally, what is your communication frequency after the sale? An advisor who cannot provide data-backed answers to these is a broker for the developer, not a consultant for you. True advisory in Mohali requires 10+ years of ground experience across regulatory bodies to protect remote capital.

The Mohali real estate market in 2026 is no longer just about buying four walls. It is about navigating a complex web of infrastructure corridors, regulatory shifts, and developer liquidity. For a Non-Resident Indian (NRI) living in Brampton, Birmingham, or Melbourne, the distance creates a vulnerability that many local brokers are eager to exploit. According to reports in The Tribune and Economic Times, the Tricity area (Chandigarh, Mohali, Panchkula) remains a top destination for NRI capital, yet the gap between marketing brochures and ground reality has never been wider. 

As someone who has stood on both sides of the table: as a developer obtaining approvals from five different Punjab government bodies and as an independent consultant protecting buyer interests: I know that trust is built on transparency, not on glossy PDFs. Before you remit your hard-earned dollars or pounds, you must put your prospective advisor through this rigorous 10-question checklist.

## 1. How is your revenue model structured: Commission, Fee, or both?

This is the most critical question for any [Mohali property advisor NRI trust questions](/blog/mohali-property-advisor-nri-trust-questions) search. You need to know whose interests are being served. If an advisor is purely commission-based, they are legally and financially an agent of the developer. Their goal is the transaction, not your long-term ROI. 

In my practice at Realty Holding and Management Consultants, I emphasize that while commissions are a standard part of the industry, a true consultant is transparent about them. Ask if they are willing to work on a flat advisory fee. A fee-based model ensures that if the right advice is "do not buy," the advisor still gets paid for their expertise, removing the conflict of interest. As the market expands towards the Rajpura and Banga corridors, as noted by local news portals, the temptation for brokers to push high-commission pre-launch projects is high. Always verify if the recommendation is based on project merit or the payout percentage.

## 2. Can you name a project you recently recommended against?

If an advisor says every project in Mohali is a "great buy," they are a salesman, not an advisor. In a market where [appreciation vs stagnation zones](/blog/mohali-property-appreciation-vs-stagnation-zones) can be separated by a single 200-foot road, everything cannot be a winner. 

I often use the phrase: "Je aapaan vision ton bina challaange taan aapaan mehngi cheez khareeddaange." This means if we move without vision, we will buy at the wrong price. A professional advisor should be able to show you a project they walked away from because of poor construction quality, slow delivery track records, or hidden loading factors. If they cannot name a project they rejected, they are not filtering the market for you. You can see examples of how we analyze these risks on our YouTube channel @Amritrealty.

## 3. What is your post-sale support system for MC Tax and NDC?

For an NRI, the real "headache" starts after the payment is made. Most brokers vanish once the commission is cleared. However, the legal health of your property depends on post-transaction work. In Mohali, this involves obtaining a No Dues Certificate (NDC), managing Municipal Committee (MC) property tax, and ensuring a separate property ID is generated.

We recently handled a case where a buyer of a ground floor unit found that the previous owner had unpaid taxes across the entire building. It took us three months of drafting MOUs between three different floor owners and coordinating with the MC to resolve the liability and generate a separate ID. Ask your advisor: Will you be there to handle the [property tax dispute](/blog/property-tax-dispute-multi-floor-building-mohali) or the [GMADA transfer process](/blog/gmada-plot-transfer-ownership-process) three months after the sale? If the answer is vague, keep looking.

## 4. How do you verify RERA Punjab technicalities beyond the registration number?

Simply having a RERA number is the bare minimum. A deep-dive advisor looks at the quarterly progress reports (QPRs) on the RERA Punjab portal. They check if the promoter has uploaded the encumbrance certificate and if the construction milestones match the declared timelines. 

Having served as a director of a RERA-approved company, I know how these filings are managed from the inside. I look for red flags like a sudden change in the architect or a mismatch between the "booked" units and the funds in the escrow account. If your advisor doesn't know [how to check RERA Punjab property](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property) details beyond the landing page, they aren't protecting your investment.

## 5. Do you provide live video site visits or just developer-sourced photos?

As an NRI, you cannot fly down for every milestone. You need eyes on the ground. A glossy brochure or a "drone shot" provided by the developer's marketing team is not evidence. You need an advisor who will go to the site at 11 AM on a Wednesday and show you the actual quality of the brickwork, the waterproofing in the basement, and the number of laborers on the floor.

At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we provide unfiltered video walkthroughs for our clients. We show the "ugly" parts: the narrow staircases, the loading factors, and the surrounding neighborhood: so you can make an informed decision. Transparency is the only way to bridge the distance between Mohali and your home abroad. This is a core part of our [NRI Property Guide for Mohali 2026](/blog/nri-property-guide-mohali-2026).

## 6. What is your direct experience with GMADA, PUDA, and PSPCL?

Property in Mohali is governed by multiple regulatory bodies. GMADA handles allotments, PUDA handles colony licensing, PSPCL manages electricity loads, and the Municipal Committee handles building sanctions. An advisor must understand the procedural "DNA" of these offices.

Ask them: Have you ever resolved a [plot cancellation recovery](/blog/mohali-plot-cancelled-booking-recovery-process)? Do you know the difference between a shared and a separate electricity meter connection? If they use words like "management" or "setting" instead of "procedure" and "documentation," be careful. True expertise is in knowing the laws, not just the people. My experience in obtaining approvals for my own projects across all five bodies ensures that I know exactly where a file might get stuck.

## 7. How many NRI transactions have you handled involving FEMA and TDS?

The financial rules for NRIs are different. From NRO/NRE account compliance to the [22.66% TDS rate on property sales](/blog/nri-tax-property-sale-mohali-tds-capital-gains), the tax implications can wipe out your gains if handled incorrectly. 

Your advisor should be familiar with the "Section 197" lower TDS certificate process and the repatriation rules under FEMA. If they treat an NRI deal like a local cash-and-carry transaction, you are at risk of heavy penalties from the Income Tax department. Experience matters. We have closed over 180 transactions, many of which required complex cross-border financial coordination.

## 8. Can you provide anonymised case studies of dispute resolution?

Ask for "battle stories." Every property advisor has a list of "happy clients," but very few have stories of where things went wrong and they fixed it. For example, we once spent eight months chasing a builder to reinstate two cancelled plots for a client who had already paid 65% of the value. The seller had defaulted, and the builder had re-allocated the plots. We fought for the client, got a re-allocation to a better location, and completed the transfer.

If your advisor doesn't have such stories, they likely haven't handled enough complex transactions to know what to do when the developer stops picking up your calls. Real expertise is proven in the [dispute resolution process](/blog/real-estate-disputes-legal-rights-punjab-2026).

## 9. What is your legal document checklist: Jamabandi, CLU, and NOCs?

A brochure is not a legal document. Before you sign anything, your advisor must review the "Jamabandi" or "Fard" (land records), the Change of Land Use (CLU) certificate from PUDA, and the various No Objection Certificates (NOCs). 

We have seen projects in Mohali face [Forest Department approval cancellations](/blog/forest-department-approval-cancellation-lesson) because they were too close to a green belt. If your advisor hasn't checked the specific forest clearance for a project near the hills or the airport, your capital is at risk. You should also check our [Mohali Real Estate FAQ 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-faq-2026) for a list of mandatory documents.

## 10. What is your communication commitment after the registry?

The biggest complaint NRIs have is the "silence" that follows the final payment. You need a commitment on how often you will receive construction updates, photos, and news about the surrounding infrastructure.

Will the advisor help you find a tenant? Will they help you with the resale value assessment three years down the line? At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we believe the sale is the beginning of the relationship. We provide a structured follow-up system that keeps you informed about your investment long after the registry is done. We understand that for an NRI, property is often a "legacy" or a "retirement" asset, and it needs continuous management.

### The Verdict on Choosing an Advisor

Choosing the right partner in Mohali is about identifying the difference between a broker and a consultant. A broker works for the developer to "sell" a unit. A consultant works for you to "secure" an asset. The difference can cost you crores in lost appreciation or legal fees.

By asking these ten questions, you force the advisor to demonstrate their technical knowledge, their ethical stance, and their commitment to your success.
