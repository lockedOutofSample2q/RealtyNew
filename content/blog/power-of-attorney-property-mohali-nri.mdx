# Power of Attorney for Property in Mohali: What It Should Say, What It Shouldn't, and Who to Trust With It

For an NRI buying or selling property in Mohali, a Power of Attorney (POA) is not just a convenience, it is often a necessity. To answer the core question: a property POA in Punjab must be a 'Special Power of Attorney' (SPA) rather than a General one to be safest, it absolutely must be registered with the Sub-Registrar if it involves the sale or transfer of immovable property, and if executed abroad, it requires an Apostille or Embassy attestation followed by adjudication in India. You should never grant a POA that allows the holder to receive sale proceeds into their personal account: all financial transactions must flow through your own NRE/NRO accounts.

In my decade of navigating Mohali𠏋 real estate landscape, I have seen the POA be both a bridge to a successful transaction and a trap for the unwary. When you are sitting in Toronto, London, or Dubai, and your property is in Sector 82A or Aerocity, the person holding your POA is essentially "you" in the eyes of the law. This guide breaks down how to structure that power so it protects your interests rather than compromising them.

## What is the difference between a General and Specific Power of Attorney in Mohali?

The first decision you will face is whether to grant a General Power of Attorney (GPA) or a Special Power of Attorney (SPA). In the context of our [complete Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), understanding this distinction is the foundation of your security.

A **General Power of Attorney (GPA)** gives the holder wide-ranging powers to act on your behalf across multiple domains. While it sounds efficient, it is unnecessarily risky for a single property transaction. In Punjab, a GPA is often viewed with extra scrutiny by authorities because of historical misuse.

A **Special Power of Attorney (SPA)**, on the other hand, is restricted to a specific task or property. For instance, if you are buying a luxury flat in Mullanpur, the SPA will specify the exact unit number, the developer𠏋 name, and the specific actions allowed. For NRIs, I always recommend the SPA. It limits your exposure. 

## Does a Power of Attorney have to be registered in Punjab?

This is where many NRIs make a fatal mistake. Under the Registration Act, 1908, any Power of Attorney that authorizes the holder to sell, gift, exchange, or lease immovable property for a period exceeding one year **must be registered**.

If you send a signed piece of paper from Canada to your cousin in Mohali, and he takes it to the Sub-Registrar𠏋 office to sign a Sale Deed, it will be rejected. Even worse, if a transaction is somehow pushed through on an unregistered POA, it remains legally vulnerable. I have dealt with [real estate disputes in Punjab](/blog/real-estate-disputes-legal-rights-punjab-2026) where the entire basis of the lawsuit was a "defective POA."

For an NRI, the registration process involves two steps:
1. **Execution Abroad:** You sign the document in the presence of a Notary Public or a Consular Officer at the Indian Embassy/Consulate.
2. **Adjudication in India:** Once the document arrives in India, it must be 'adjudicated' or stamped by the Divisional Commissioner within three months of its receipt in India.

## What should a safe NRI Power of Attorney actually say?

Specificity is your best defense. A well-drafted POA for Mohali property should be surgical. It should list specific permissions:

1.  **The 'To Represent' Clause:** Permission to appear before GMADA, PUDA, or the Municipal Committee for signatures and document collection.
2.  **The 'Registration' Clause:** Specific authority to present documents for registration at the Sub-Registrar𠏋 office.
3.  **The 'Financial' Limit:** This is critical. The POA should explicitly state that any payments made or received must be via the NRI𠏋 own NRE/NRO accounts. 
4.  **The 'No-Substitution' Clause:** Ensure the holder cannot "delegate" this power to a third party you don't know.
5.  **The 'Expiry' Date:** If the POA is for a specific purchase, have it expire once the Registry is complete and Mutation is filed.

## Who should you trust with your Power of Attorney in Mohali?

This is a question of "skin in the game." While family is the default choice, I often advise NRIs to consider the following:
- **Professional Advisors:** Not for the 'Sale' power, but for the 'Representation' power. An advisor can handle the legwork at GMADA or the Municipal Committee, while you keep the 'Sale' power with a trusted family member.
- **Limited Scope Holders:** Give the power to sign utilities or maintenance to one person, and the power to register property to another.
