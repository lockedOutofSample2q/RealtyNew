# Selling a Mohali Property: The Complete Checklist — NOC, Loan Closure, Tax, and What Sellers Miss

Selling a property in Mohali in 2026 requires a precise sequence of legal and financial steps to ensure a clean exit and full realization of value. To successfully close a sale, you must secure four critical clearances: an updated Jamabandi (revenue record), a Permission to Transfer (PTT) from GMADA or the Municipal Committee, a loan foreclosure letter if the property is mortgaged, and a No Dues Certificate (NDC) for property tax. While buyers in Punjab typically bear the 7% stamp duty and registration costs, sellers must account for brokerage, legal drafting, and capital gains tax: which for NRIs can involve a mandatory 22.66% TDS. Having personally closed over 180 transactions across all property categories in Punjab, I know that the difference between a smooth registry and a stalled deal lies in the preparation of these documents 30 days before the buyer arrives.

## What are the mandatory documents required to sell a property in Mohali?

In my years of navigating project approvals and closing transactions, I have seen many deals collapse at the last minute because the seller assumed their paperwork was complete. It is not enough to just have the original allotment letter or sale deed. You need a set of supporting documents that prove the property is liquid and free of liabilities.

The first thing a sophisticated buyer or a bank will ask for is the Fard or Jamabandi. This is the record of rights maintained by the revenue department. It tells the world who actually owns the land. If you are selling a plot in a GMADA sector, you need the Permission to Transfer (PTT) from the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority. This is non-negotiable. Without it, the sub-registrar will not record the transaction.

![Checklist of mandatory documents for selling residential property in Mohali](mohali-seller-checklist.jpg)

Next is the Encumbrance Certificate (EC). This document proves that the property is free from any legal dues or mortgages. If you have ever taken a loan against the property, the EC must show that the lien has been removed. I often tell my HNI clients: do not wait for a buyer to ask for these. Get them ready as soon as you decide to sell. It shows you are a serious seller and prevents unnecessary price negotiations based on paperwork risk.

## How do you handle an outstanding home loan during a sale?

If your property has an active mortgage, the bank holds your original documents. You cannot sell what you do not technically possess on paper. The process here is specific. You must first request a Foreclosure Letter or a List of Documents (LOD) from your bank. This letter states exactly how much money is needed to close the loan as of a specific date.

In a typical Mohali transaction, the buyer’s payment is often used to close this loan. This requires a three-way coordination between you, the buyer, and the bank. The buyer pays the bank directly to clear the dues: the bank then releases the original documents and an NOC stating the loan is closed. Only after this can the property be registered in the buyer’s name. I have managed these closures for several clients where the timing was tight. Always factor in 10 to 15 working days for the bank to return original documents after the payment is made.

## The Capital Gains Trap: What is your actual net realization?

Most sellers calculate their profit by subtracting the purchase price from the sale price. As someone who is AMFI and NCFM certified, I look at the math differently. Your real profit is what stays in your pocket after the government takes its share.

Under current Indian tax laws, if you have held the property for more than 24 months, it qualifies for Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG). The tax rate is 20% with indexation benefits. Indexation is a tool that allows you to adjust the purchase price for inflation, which reduces your taxable profit. However, if you sell within 24 months, the profit is added to your income and taxed at your applicable slab: which for most HNIs in Mohali is 30% plus surcharge. To save on this tax, you can reinvest the capital gains into another residential property within two years or buy specific 54EC capital gains bonds. ## What hidden costs do Mohali sellers often forget to budget for?

Sellers often focus on the big number, the sale price, but there are several smaller leaks that add up:

1. **Brokerage:** The standard in Mohali is 1% to 2% of the total transaction value.
2. **Legal Fees:** You will need a professional document writer to draft the Agreement to Sale and the Sale Deed. While the buyer pays for the stamp duty, the seller usually bears the cost of ensuring their own legal interests are protected.
3. **Property Tax Clearance:** You cannot sell without a No Dues Certificate (NDC) from the Municipal Committee or GMADA. If you have unpaid property tax from three years ago, it will surface now with interest. I handled a case for a client named Sandeep where a property tax dispute took three months of persistent coordination to resolve because the seller had not separated the property IDs. You can find more on this in our [guide to property registration](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026).
4. **Maintenance Dues:** Most luxury societies in Sector 82A or Aerocity will not give you a clearance certificate unless all maintenance bills and sinking fund contributions are paid up to date.

## Selling as an NRI: Navigating the 22.66% TDS

If you are an NRI selling property in Mohali, the rules change significantly. The buyer is legally required to deduct TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) at a rate of 22.66% on the total sale value, not just the profit. This is a huge chunk of capital to have locked up with the tax department.

To avoid this, you can apply for a Lower Deduction Certificate (LDC) from the Income Tax department. This certificate allows the buyer to deduct TDS only on the actual capital gains rather than the total sale price. Getting this certificate takes 45 to 60 days, so you must start the process early. Without it, you will be waiting until the next financial year to claim a refund for the excess tax deducted. For more details, refer to our [capital gains tax guide for property](/blog/capital-gains-tax-agricultural-land-punjab-2026).

## The Timeline: How long does it actually take?

From the day you decide to sell to the day you walk out of the sub-registrar's office with the money, expect a timeline of 45 to 90 days.

*   **Week 1-2:** Documentation gathering and finding a buyer.
*   **Week 3:** Price negotiation and signing the Agreement to Sale (usually with a 10% token amount).
*   **Week 4-6:** Buyer secures a home loan if needed. You secure the loan closure NOC from your bank.
*   **Week 7:** Final payment and Registry.

The biggest delays usually happen in the paperwork phase. I have seen transactions stalled for eight months because of cancelled bookings that the seller was not even aware of. These are the things that a standard checklist misses but an experienced advisor catches.
