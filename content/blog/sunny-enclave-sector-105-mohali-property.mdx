# Sunny Enclave Sector 105–109 Mohali: What Buyers in This Belt Are Actually Getting in 2026

In 2026, the Sunny Enclave belt, specifically Sectors 105 to 109, stands as the most practical entry point for buyers who want the Mohali address without the ₹2–4 crore price tag of the core city. For a Land Seller reinvesting an acquisition payout, this area offers a balance of immediate livability and high resale liquidity. Currently, residential plots here trade between ₹45,000 and ₹75,000 per sq. yard, while 3BHK independent floors range from ₹65 Lakh to ₹95 Lakh. It is an established, bustling township that provides a soft landing for families moving from rural Punjab. While it may not offer the high-end aesthetic of the [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), its functionality as a residential hub is unmatched in the mid-budget segment. The belt offers a genuine community feel, high density of essential services, and a price point that allows for diversification of capital, which is critical for families managing life-changing acquisition wealth.

## Why is Sector 105-109 the "First Choice" for Land Sellers?

When a family sells agricultural land in areas like Banur, Rajpura, or along the Ludhiana highway, the sudden influx of ₹1 crore to ₹5 crore can be overwhelming. In my experience advising these families, the first instinct is often to buy where they feel at home. They are not just looking for an asset. They are looking for a transition.

Sunny Enclave Sector 105 feels like a natural extension of the Punjab heartland. Unlike the corporate-heavy environment of IT City or the high-rise culture of Sector 82A, this belt is built for horizontal living. You see wide porches, community parks filled with familiar faces, and local markets that cater to every household need. From a financial perspective, it is a safe harbour. If you have received a payout and are following the [land seller reinvestment guide punjab 2026](/blog/land-seller-reinvestment-guide-punjab-2026), Sector 105 offers immediate utility. You can buy a plot, build a floor for yourself, and rent out the others to generate a steady monthly income that beats any FD return.

Most buyers here are not looking to flip a property in six months. They are looking to park capital for 10 years. They value the fact that their neighbor is likely another family from a similar background, perhaps a retired officer or a business owner from a nearby town like Kharar or Kurali. This social cohesion makes the area resilient to market volatility. When core Mohali prices stagnate, Sunny Enclave often keeps moving because its demand is driven by people who need to live there, not just speculators.

## Is the Connectivity to Core Mohali and Chandigarh Actually Good?

For years, the "Sunny Enclave bottleneck" was a real deterrent. The single entry-exit point near the Kharar flyover caused massive delays. In 2026, that narrative has changed. The PR-7 (Airport Road) extension has bypassed the old Kharar congestion, linking Sector 105 directly to the IT corridor and the airport.

However, be realistic. If your office is in Chandigarh Sector 17 or the Phase 8B Industrial Area, your commute during peak hours (9:00 AM and 6:00 PM) will still involve some traffic. The advantage here is the highway access. If you have family back in Ludhiana or Jalandhar, being on this side of Mohali saves you 30 minutes of cross-city driving every single trip. For a Land Seller who still has interests in rural Punjab, this edge-of-the-city location is often a feature, not a bug.

The PR-7 extension has essentially turned Sunny Enclave from a suburb of Kharar into a sector of Mohali. You are now 20 minutes from the airport and 15 minutes from the PCA Stadium. This infrastructure shift is the primary reason why resale prices in Sector 105 have held firm while other outskirts have struggled.

## The Resale Factor: How Quickly Can You Exit?

The biggest strength of Sector 105 is its liquidity. In the real estate business, we say a property is only worth what someone will pay for it today. 

Because the ticket size in Sunny Enclave, especially for floors and small plots, fits the budget of 70% of Mohali's home buyers, these properties sell fast. In core Mohali sectors where a plot costs ₹4 crore, your pool of buyers is tiny. You might wait six months for a serious offer. In Sector 105, where a floor costs ₹75 Lakh, your pool of buyers is massive. 

During my time on the developer side, I noticed that the most successful small-scale investors were the ones who bought 3-4 properties in this belt rather than one massive unit elsewhere. They could sell one unit whenever they needed cash, perhaps for a wedding or a business expansion, without disturbing their entire portfolio. This is the definition of "smart money" in the Punjab market.

## What is the Rental Yield Reality in Sector 105-109?

For investors, Sunny Enclave offers one of the best rental yields in the Tricity. While a luxury flat in Sector 82A might give you a 2.5% yield, a 3BHK floor in Sector 105 can easily touch 3.5% to 4%. 

The demand comes from young professionals working in the nearby IT parks who want more space than a studio but cannot afford Chandigarh rents. It also comes from students enrolled in the various universities and colleges along the Kharar-Landran belt. A typical 3BHK floor that costs ₹75 Lakh can rent for ₹22,000 to ₹28,000 per month. If you are a Land Seller looking for pension income, this is a much more robust strategy than placing the entire payout in an FD.

## What Should You Verify Before Signing the Agreement?

Even in an established area like Sector 105, due diligence is non-negotiable. I have seen too many buyers get blinded by a "good deal" only to find out the paperwork is a mess. 

One case involved aconstructed building where the seller had not paid Municipal Committee property tax for several years. When the buyer tried to clear it, it appeared as a collective liability across all floors. We had to draft an MOU between all three floor owners to allocate liability and generate separate property IDs. It took 3 months of coordination to resolve. 

Before you buy, verify:
1. **Mutation (Intkal) Status:** Ensure the seller's name is updated in the revenue records.
2. **NOC from Developer:** You need a clear No Objection Certificate from the developer to ensure all dues are paid.
3. **Electricity and Water Connections:** Check if individual meters are installed. Do not rely on shared meters.
4. **Road Width and Parking:** Measure the road width yourself. A 10ft difference can affect your resale value significantly.
