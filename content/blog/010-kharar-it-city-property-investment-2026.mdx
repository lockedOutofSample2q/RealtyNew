# Kharar IT City Belt: Is This Mohali's Best Undervalued Investment Corridor in 2026?

Is Kharar IT City the best undervalued investment in Mohali for 2026? Yes. Currently, the Kharar IT City belt offers a 35-40% price entry gap compared to established sectors like 82A and Aerocity, despite sharing the same corporate demand drivers and IT corridor connectivity. With the expansion of the IT corridor and the increasing influx of corporate tenants, we project a 15-18% annual appreciation over the next three years.

The investment case for Kharar IT City rests on the "Vision Investing" principle: buying where infrastructure is already planned and partially built, but before it is fully priced in. While Sector 82A has already seen its massive jump (from Rs 3-4 crore to Rs 12-16 crore for commercial units), Kharar is still in the early-to-mid stage of its growth curve. For CXO-level investors seeking both capital growth and future rental yields from the growing tech workforce, this corridor represents the most rational entry point in the current market.

## The Price Gap Analysis: Why Sector 82A's Success is Kharar's Opportunity?

In the [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), I often talk about the expansion principle. When a sector like 82A or the JLPL corridor hits a price ceiling, the smart money moves to the next available expansion zone. Sector 82A was once the "frontier." I remember when commercial units there were available at Rs 3-4 crore. Today, those same units trade at Rs 12-16 crore. That ship has sailed for the investor looking for 3x or 4x returns.

Kharar IT City is the direct beneficiary of this saturation. If you look at the price per square foot (psf) data, Sector 82A is trading at a premium that reflects its maturity. Kharar, however, is providing a similar proximity to the IT hub but at a significantly lower entry cost. This price gap isn't because the land is "worse"—it's because the "brand" of the IT City belt is still maturing in the minds of the general public. 

As someone who has managed project documentation for approvals across PUDA and PSPCL, I've seen how these corridors develop. The paperwork moves first, then the infrastructure, and finally the people. Kharar is currently in the late infrastructure-early people stage. That is where the money is made.

The IT sector in Mohali is no longer just a "promise." It is a massive, growing reality. The demand for corporate office space and the subsequent housing demand for IT professionals is shifting the weight of the city toward the west. In our [Mohali real estate investment analysis 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-investment-analysis-2026), we’ve tracked how rental yields in the IT City belt have started to outperform older sectors.

Corporate tenants don't want to deal with the congestion of older Mohali phases. They want the wide roads, the connectivity to the airport, and the proximity to their offices that the Kharar IT corridor provides. For a CXO looking to invest, this means higher occupancy rates and a better tenant profile. You aren't just buying a plot or a flat; you are buying into the growth of the region's white-collar economy.

## FAQ: Common Questions About Kharar IT City Investment

### What is the current price gap between Kharar IT City and Sector 82A?
Currently, the price gap stands at approximately 35-40%. While Sector 82A has reached its peak maturity as a premium commercial and residential hub, Kharar is still in the expansion phase, offering a more accessible entry point for similar connectivity and tenant demand.

### Which projects in the Kharar IT belt are the safest for NRIs or CXOs?
The safest projects are those with clear RERA Punjab registration, completed OC/CC for earlier phases, and direct connectivity to the main Airport Road or IT City arteries. Avoid projects that only have "applied for" status or lack clear utility sanctions from PSPCL and GMADA.

### How does the IT corridor expansion impact rental yields?
As more IT companies move their operations to the Mohali-Kharar belt, the demand for high-quality rental housing for professionals increases. This drive for "convenience housing" leads to higher rental yields (3-5%) compared to the 2-3% typically seen in purely residential or stagnant sectors.

### Is the Kharar IT City belt suitable for end-users or just investors?
It is suitable for both. While the investment case is driven by appreciation and yield, the lifestyle infrastructure — including schools, healthcare, and retail — is developing rapidly, making it a viable relocation option for families who want to be near the city's growth center.
