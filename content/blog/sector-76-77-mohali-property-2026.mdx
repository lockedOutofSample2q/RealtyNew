# Sector 76 and 77 Mohali: Current Prices, Possession Status, and the Honest Buyer's Assessment

In 2026, Sector 76 and 77 in Mohali have established themselves as premium, high-liquidity residential hubs with residential plot prices ranging from ₹90,000 to ₹1,35,000 per square yard, and independent floors trading between ₹7,000 and ₹9,000 per square foot. These sectors are fully developed GMADA pockets offering immediate connectivity to the IT Park (Sector 82/83) and the Airport Road (PR-7). While GMADA-allotted inventory is 100% possession-ready, certain private group housing projects in the immediate vicinity still encounter 8–14 month delivery delays. For HNI investors, these sectors offer stability and strong rental demand from corporate professionals, though the "low-entry" appreciation window of 2021 has officially closed. If you are looking for a secure asset with high resale liquidity and established infrastructure, Sector 76 and 77 are top-tier choices, provided you verify the specific maintenance and mutation status of the property in question.

## Why is Sector 76 and 77 considered the "Administrative Heart" of Mohali?

When you look at the map of Mohali, Sector 76 and 77 sit at a strategic crossroads. Having managed project approvals across the Municipal Committee and GMADA, I’ve seen how these sectors were prioritized for infrastructure. Sector 76 houses the District Administrative Complex (DAC) and Vikas Bhawan, making it the seat of power in Mohali. 

For a buyer, this isn't just about being near government offices; it's about the quality of the roads, the reliability of the PSPCL power grid, and the speed of the sewerage system. In these sectors, the "basic" infrastructure that plagues newer corridors is already solved. You aren't buying a promise here; you are buying a paved reality.

The proximity to the IT Park in Sector 82 and 83 is the primary driver for rental yields. Senior corporate executives who work at MNCs often prefer 76 and 77 because they can reach their office in under 5 minutes without having to deal with the heavy transit traffic of the main Airport Road.

## What is the actual possession status of projects in this belt?

If you are buying a GMADA-allotted plot, possession is not an issue. These are established sectors where most owners have already obtained their Completion Certificates (CC) and Occupation Certificates (OC). However, the situation changes when you look at private "builder floors" or group housing projects on the periphery.

In my years on the developer side, I’ve seen how "delivery" is often a flexible term in marketing brochures. In the Sector 76-77 belt:
1. **GMADA Plots:** 100% ready. Mutation and transfer processes are standard.
2. **Independent Floors:** Most are ready, but newer 4-storey configurations (Stilt + 4) sometimes face delays in PSPCL load sanctions for separate meters.
3. **Private Group Housing:** There are 2-3 major projects nearby that are currently 12 months behind their RERA-declared timelines.

Before you sign an agreement, you must check the RERA Punjab portal. I’ve detailed exactly how to do this in my guide on [how to check RERA Punjab property](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property). Don't take a broker's word for "possession in 3 months" when the RERA filing shows a 2027 completion date.

## Is the connectivity to the Airport and IT Park a genuine advantage?

Connectivity is often a buzzword used to sell poor locations, but in Sector 76 and 77, it is a physical reality. The PR-7 Airport Road is a straight shot from the boundary of Sector 77. 

But here is the "honest" part: during peak office hours (9:00 AM and 6:00 PM), the entry points to the IT Park can get congested. The advantage of living in 76/77 is that you have multiple internal access roads that allow you to bypass the main highway bottlenecks. 

For an NRI buyer looking for a [managed property remotely](/blog/manage-mohali-property-remotely-nri-abroad), this connectivity ensures a steady stream of high-quality corporate tenants. Engineers and managers working at nearby IT giants aren't looking for the cheapest rent; they are looking for the shortest commute and the best security—both of which these sectors provide.

## What does the Sector 76-77 buyer's assessment reveal? (The Honest Verdict)

Every sector has its flaws. Even these "A-grade" sectors have issues that most brokers will hide from you.

**What the sector offers (The Pros):**
- **Liquidity:** These are the "Blue Chip" stocks of Mohali real estate. You can exit quickly if you need capital.
- **Infrastructure:** Wide roads (minimum 40ft internal), functional street lighting, and dedicated green belts.
- **Governance:** Being near the Administrative Complex means issues like waste management and road repairs are addressed faster than in outlying sectors.

**What the sector lacks (The Cons):**
- **Price Saturation:** If you are looking for a property that will double in 2 years, this isn't it. This is a wealth-preservation and steady-growth play.
- **Parking:** In areas with high density of builder floors, street parking is becoming a nightmare. If the floor you are buying doesn't have a dedicated stilt parking spot, walk away.
- **Old Inventory:** Some of the older constructions in Sector 76 are showing their age. Be careful not to pay "new floor" prices for a 10-year-old renovation.

## How to navigate the mutation and paperwork in these sectors?

Because Sector 76 and 77 are GMADA-controlled, the transfer process is very structured but requires precise documentation. I've personally resolved disputes where a seller's mutation (Intkal) was not updated in the records, stalling the sale for months.

You must ensure that the property tax is cleared and a "No Dues Certificate" (NDC) is obtained from the Municipal Committee. In many multi-floor buildings here, the property tax was historically collective. As I mentioned in my [property tax dispute multi-floor building mohali](/blog/property-tax-dispute-multi-floor-building-mohali) case study, separating these IDs is crucial before you complete your registry.

If you are buying a plot, verify the GMADA transfer records directly. Don't rely on a "photo" of the allotment letter. Ask for a certified copy. My [mohali property registration process guide 2026](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026) covers the exact sequence you should follow.

## Are there any "Red Zones" or areas to avoid in Sector 76-77?

While most of the sector is clean, pay attention to the boundaries near the village "abadi" (original village settlements). Properties directly abutting the old village boundaries sometimes face issues with unorganized parking, stray cattle, or unclear title histories.

Also, be wary of properties near the designated "High Tension" power lines. While GMADA usually plans around them, some private builders might push the limits of the buffer zones. Always check the site plan against the physical reality.
