## The Five Regulatory Pillars: Navigating Punjab's Governance

You cannot safely buy property in Mohali without understanding the five regulatory bodies that govern the land. A "RERA number" is just the start: the real safety lies in the liaisoning trail.

### 1. GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority)
GMADA is the gold standard. It controls plot allotments through draws and auctions, manages colony licensing within its jurisdiction, and maintains the ultimate record of property transfers (mutations). 

In 2026, a critical technical detail often overlooked is the transfer process. A property transfer in a GMADA sector requires a certified copy with an official Tehsildar stamp. We have seen buyers attempt to "verify" deals using WhatsApp photos of allotment letters. This is a recipe for disaster. The official process involves OTP-based verification sent to the last registered buyer and typically takes 4 to 8 weeks. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we ensure the Tehsildar's trail is clean before a single rupee moves. 

One of the most common disputes we handle involves "cancelled plots." If a seller fails to pay installments to the builder or GMADA, the plot can be cancelled without immediate public notice. Verification at the source, rather than trusting a resale agreement, is the only way to protect your capital. Our team regularly visits the GMADA office in Sector 62 to verify the ledger status of properties before advising a closure.

### 2. PUDA (Punjab Urban Development Authority)
While GMADA handles the core sectors, PUDA oversees licensing for areas outside those limits. It is vital to distinguish between a project that has "applied for PUDA approval" and one that is "PUDA approved." 

The technicality lies in the CLU (Change of Land Use) and layout plan approvals. These are separate milestones. A developer might have a CLU but lack layout approval: selling in this phase is technically unauthorized. We have filed RTIs (Right to Information) to verify the status of these approvals for our HNI clients, ensuring that the "upcoming project" is not a legal trap.

### 3. PSPCL (Punjab State Power Corporation Limited)
Electricity is often an afterthought until it becomes a deal-breaker. For multi-floor buildings (independent floors), separate metering is critical. 

If a building has a single shared connection, the liability is collective. Getting separate PSPCL connections for each floor requires a specific application, load sanction, and connection process that can take weeks. This is not just about convenience: you cannot get a No Dues Certificate (NDC) for resale without separate metering and a cleared bill in the owner's name. We have managed dozens of such cases where the lack of a separate meter stalled a high-value transaction for months.

### 4. The Municipal Committee (MC)
The MC governs building plan sanctions, Occupation Certificates (OC), and property tax IDs. 

A major issue in 2026 is the shared property tax ID. In many multi-floor constructions, the MC creates a single ID for the entire building. If the owner of the top floor defaults on tax, the owner of the ground floor cannot get an NDC to sell their property. Resolving this requires a legal MOU (Memorandum of Understanding) between floor owners and a persistent application to the MC to generate separate property IDs. 

Furthermore, the recent increase in Floor Area Ratio (FAR) across Mohali has led to many owners adding illegal floors. Any construction beyond the sanctioned map requires a revised building plan from the MC and a potential compounding fee. Buying a property with unapproved construction is a legal liability that will come back to haunt you during resale. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we review the sanctioned map against the physical structure before every high-ticket closure.

### 5. Forest and Conservation Authorities
In the peripheral zones of Mohali, particularly near the hills or designated green belts, Forest Department clearance is mandatory. We have seen projects where approvals were cancelled due to environmental non-compliance. 

The most striking example from my own career involved a project where forest department approval was revoked mid-construction. Navigating the "Green Zone" vs. "Construction Zone" maps requires more than a broker's word: it requires a study of the latest forest department notifications and local revenue records.

## Industrial Zone Classification: Red, Orange, and Green

For HNI investors, industrial land is the high-yield frontier of 2026. However, you must understand the "Zone Math."

1. **Red Zone:** These are designated for high-pollution industries such as chemicals, pharma, and heavy manufacturing. These plots require proximity to a natural drainage (nala) and significant setbacks from residential zones. Because they are restricted, Red Zone plots often command a premium from genuine industrial buyers.
2. **Orange Zone:** Medium-impact industries. These are more flexible but still require specific pollution control board clearances.
3. **Green Zone:** Low-impact, non-polluting industries like electronics assembly or warehousing.

The appreciation we saw in the Rajpura belt was driven by the scarcity of Red Zone land combined with the Bharatmala connectivity. If you buy a Green Zone plot thinking you can set up a pharma unit, your capital will be locked in a useless asset.

## Property Categories and Technical Benchmarks

Success in 2026 requires moving beyond the "flat vs. plot" debate. You must understand the technical benchmarks of each asset class.

### Residential Flats: The Loading Factor Trap
In the luxury 3BHK and 4BHK segment, the market has standardized a reserve price starting at Rs 1.15 crore for premium units. However, the "price per square foot" is a deceptive metric.

The technical benchmark you must watch is the **Loading Factor**. Standard loading in Mohali is 25-30%. If a developer claims a 2,000 sq. ft. flat but the carpet area is only 1,200 sq. ft., the loading is 40%. This means you are paying for common areas that offer no utility. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we calculate the effective usable price per square foot before recommending any project.

## Technical Breakdown: GST and Maintenance Charges

Buying in 2026 involves significant post-purchase costs that are often hidden in the fine print.

### GST on Real Estate
- **Under-Construction:** 5% for non-affordable housing (no Input Tax Credit).
- **Affordable Housing:** 1%.
- **Ready-to-Move (with OC):** 0%.
This creates a massive price delta. A 2 crore under-construction flat has an immediate 10 lakh GST burden that a ready-to-move flat avoids. We advise our CXO clients to factor this into their ROI calculations.

### Maintenance Charges (The Rs 3-8 psf Spectrum)
- **Rs 3-4 psf:** Basic security, cleaning, and common area lighting.
- **Rs 5-6 psf:** Adds power backup, swimming pool maintenance, and basic gym.
- **Rs 7-8 psf:** Full-service luxury including concierge, temperature-controlled pools, and premium landscaping.
For a 2,500 sq. ft. flat, the difference between Rs 3 and Rs 8 psf is 12,500 per month. Over 10 years, this is a 15 lakh commitment.

## The Mutation and Intkal Process: A Step-by-Step Guide

Even after the registry is done, the property is not fully yours in the eyes of the revenue department until the **Mutation (Intkal)** is complete.

1. **Registry Completion:** The Sale Deed is signed at the Sub-Registrar's office.
2. **Application for Mutation:** A copy of the deed is submitted to the Patwari of the area.
3. **Verification:** The Patwari verifies the record against the current Jamabandi (land records).
4. **Public Notice:** A 15-day notice period is observed for any objections.
5. **Approval:** The Kanungo or Tehsildar approves the entry in the Mutation Register.
6. **Jamabandi Update:** The new owner's name is reflected in the bi-annual Jamabandi.
Without mutation, you cannot get a bank loan or a No Dues Certificate (NDC) for resale. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we track this process until the new Jamabandi is issued.

## The NRI Investor's Guide: FEMA, TDS, and Remote Safety

For the NRI diaspora, Mohali is a top destination, but the technical legalities are often the stumbling block. 

### FEMA Compliance
NRIs can purchase residential and commercial property without RBI approval. However, agricultural land, farmhouses, and plantation properties are restricted. All payments must route through NRE or NRO accounts. Using a local relative's bank account to pay for property is a violation of FEMA that can lead to severe penalties.

### The Tax Hurdle: 22.66% TDS
The biggest shock for NRIs is the Tax Deducted at Source (TDS). On long-term capital gains, the TDS rate is 22.66% (20% base + surcharge + cess). On short-term gains (less than 24 months), it is 30% plus surcharge. 

Repatriation of sale proceeds is limited to USD 1 million per year through the NRO account. This requires the submission of Form 15CA and 15CB, certified by a Chartered Accountant. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we coordinate with tax professionals to ensure our NRI clients' capital is mobile and compliant.

### Power of Attorney (POA)
Buying remotely requires a robust POA framework. In 2026, a POA must be properly adjudicated in India to be valid for property registration. We provide video walkthroughs and digital due diligence folders for our NRI clients, allowing them to close deals from London, Toronto, or Dubai with the same confidence as if they were standing on-site in Mohali.

## The Land Seller's Strategy: Avoiding the Reinvestment Trap

Many land sellers in Punjab, particularly those receiving government acquisition payouts for roads or canals, fall into the "FD Trap." 

### The Math of Stagnation
A typical payout parked in a Fixed Deposit (FD) offers roughly 7% gross interest. After a 30% tax slab and a 6% inflation rate, the real net return is near zero or negative. Your wealth is not growing: it is evaporating.

### Section 10(37) and Section 54F
Under Section 10(37), capital gains on compulsorily acquired agricultural land are often exempt for individuals. To maintain other exemptions, particularly under Section 54F, sellers must reinvest the proceeds in residential property within a 2-year window (for purchase) or a 3-year window (for construction).

The 24-month window is the most critical timeframe for wealth preservation. If you do not reinvest, the money should be parked in the Capital Gains Account Scheme, not a standard savings account. We help land sellers transition from agricultural payouts into high-yield commercial SCOs or premium residential assets in Mohali.

## Technical FAQ: 10 Questions Real Buyers Ask

**1. Can I buy property in Mohali with a GPA (General Power of Attorney)?**
A GPA is not a title of ownership. You can use a GPA to *execute* a sale deed, but the registry itself is the only proof of ownership. Buying on "GPA basis" without a registry is illegal and offers no title protection.

**2. What is the difference between carpet area and super area in 2026?**
RERA Punjab mandates disclosure of carpet area (the net usable floor area). Super area includes common areas like corridors, lift shafts, and stairs. Always calculate your price based on the carpet area.

**3. Is maintenance taxable?**
Yes, GST is applicable on maintenance charges if they exceed 7,500 per month per member.

**4. How do I verify a GMADA allotment?**
Visit the Estate Officer at the GMADA office in Sector 62. Provide the property number and request a verification of the ledger status. Digital records are often delayed: physical verification is mandatory for safety.

**5. What is the penalty for delayed possession in 2026?**
Under RERA, the developer is liable to pay interest (usually SBI MCLR + 2%) for every month of delay. You can also file for a full refund with interest if the delay exceeds the promised date significantly.

**6. Can I build an extra floor on my GMADA plot?**
Recent FAR increases allow for additional construction, but you must get a revised map sanctioned by the Municipal Committee and pay the requisite compounding or FAR charges.

**7. Is registration possible on Saturday?**
No, registration offices in Mohali operate Monday to Friday. Appointments must be booked online.

**8. What are Floor Rise Charges (FRC)?**
Developers charge a premium for higher floors (usually Rs 50-100 psf per floor). We often negotiate these for our clients in high-rise projects.

**9. Do I need a No Objection Certificate (NOC) for resale?**
For GMADA sectors, you need a No Dues Certificate (NDC) and a transfer permission. For private projects, a simple NDC from the builder is usually sufficient for registry.

**10. What is the NRI TDS on agricultural land?**
NRIs cannot purchase agricultural land. If they are *selling* inherited agricultural land, the TDS is 22.66% on long-term gains.

## Legal Safeguards: Section 138 and RTI

The real estate system in Punjab has built-in safeguards that most buyers never use.

### Section 138 of the NI Act (Cheque Bouncing)
A dishonored cheque is a criminal offense. If a developer or seller issues a refund cheque that bounces, you have a powerful legal tool. The outcome is often a settlement, but it can lead to imprisonment. Having an advisor who knows how to trigger this process is a critical layer of protection.

### RTI for Due Diligence
The Right to Information Act 2005 is the "secret weapon" of the professional investor. You can file an RTI with GMADA or the Municipal Committee to verify the actual status of project approvals, the history of property allotments, and the status of environmental clearances.
