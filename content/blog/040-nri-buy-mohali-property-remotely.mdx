# NRI Buying Mohali Property Without Visiting: What Remote Due Diligence Actually Looks Like

Buying Mohali property from abroad without a physical visit is entirely possible and safe if you follow a rigorous remote due diligence protocol. The process involves five mandatory steps: a live video site walkthrough to verify ground reality, digital verification of RERA and title documents, a legal review of the agreement to sale, payment exclusively through NRO/NRE accounts for FEMA compliance, and registration via a specific Power of Attorney (POA). By insisting on seeing "live" evidence of approvals and construction progress rather than relying on glossy brochures, an NRI can secure an investment in Mohali with the same level of certainty as a local buyer. This remote-first approach removes the need for expensive travel while ensuring that every regulatory check — from GMADA transfer eligibility to RERA compliance — is completed before a single rupee leaves your account.

## Step 1: The Video Site Walkthrough Protocol (What to ask your advisor to show)

When I do a video walkthrough for a client in the UK or UAE, I am not showing them the "experience center" or the air-conditioned model flat. Those are built to sell, not to inform. A proper remote site visit must focus on the "bones" of the project. This is the "Video Protocol" I use to protect my clients' capital.

Ask your advisor to show you:
1. **The Actual Tower and Floor:** Don't settle for "it will look like this." See the slab, the brickwork, and the distance from the neighbouring tower. I use a high-definition gimbal to ensure the client can see the quality of the RCC casting and the thickness of the walls.
2. **Construction Pace:** Are there workers on-site? Is the equipment moving? I've seen projects that look great in photos but haven't moved a brick in three months. I often visit sites unannounced to give my clients a "surprise check" on the actual workforce deployment.
3. **The Surrounding Infrastructure:** Show me the approach road. Show me the nearest transformer. Show me if the "green belt" promised in the brochure is actually a dumping ground or a forest. In Mohali, connectivity is everything. I verify if the proposed 200ft road is actually under construction or just a line on a map.
4. **Site Hygiene:** A developer who keeps a clean site usually builds a clean project. RCC quality and finish are visible even on a high-definition video call if you know where to point the camera. I check for honeycombing in the concrete and the quality of the plumbing pipes being used.

## Step 2: Document Verification on Video Call (Seeing the originals)

A PDF scan can be edited, but a live document on a video call is harder to fake. Before you commit, I always recommend a "Document Review Call." This isn't just a quick glance: it's a line-by-line verification of the project's legal standing.

We go through:
* **The Original Allotment Letter:** Especially for GMADA plots, seeing the original stamp and the serial number is critical. I've handled 180+ transactions, and I've seen how "duplicate" allotment letters can hide underlying liens.
* **RERA Registration:** We open the RERA Punjab website together on a screen share and verify the project's quarterly progress reports. If a developer hasn't updated their RERA filings, it is a massive red flag. We check the "Escrow Account" details to ensure your money is going where it should.
* **Approved Building Maps:** I make sure the client sees the seal of the Municipal Committee or GMADA on the maps. We verify that the floor you are buying actually exists on the approved plan.
* **NOCs:** Whether it is the Forest Department clearance or the PSPCL load sanction, we verify the document dates. I've personally seen project approvals cancelled by the Forest Department because of buffer zone violations. Verification is the only cure for uncertainty.

This is where my experience in government liaisoning across PUDA and the Municipal Committee comes in. I know what an authentic approval looks like and, more importantly, I know what a "conditional" approval that might get cancelled looks like.

## Step 3: The Agreement to Sale Review (Before you sign anything)

The agreement to sale is the most important document you will ever sign in your property journey. As an advisor, I review these from the perspective of a former developer. I know exactly where the "one-sided" clauses are hidden because I used to manage these operations for a RERA-approved firm.

We look for the "Possession Date" and the "Penalty Clause." Does the developer pay you the same interest for their delay that you pay them for your payment delay? If not, we negotiate. We also check for the "Loading Factor." In Mohali, 25-30% is standard. If I see 35% or more, I tell my clients they are being overcharged for unusable space. 

For example, if you are buying a 1500 sqft flat at Rs 10,000 psf, a 5% difference in loading is a Rs 7.5 lakh hidden cost. Most brokers won't tell you this: I will. ## Step 4: The Correct Payment Route (FEMA compliance is non-negotiable)

I've seen NRIs get into trouble because they used a resident relative's account to make payments. This is a violation of FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) rules and can lead to severe penalties when you attempt to repatriate the sale proceeds later.

The protocol is simple:
* All payments must originate from your **NRE or NRO account**.
* Never pay in "cash" or via unofficial channels. Every rupee must be on the record.
* Ensure the developer provides a receipt that mentions the payment came from an NRI account. This is a crucial document for your future tax filings.

This transparency is not just for the government, it is for your own protection. As someone who has worked in capital markets with AMFI and NCFM certifications, I view property as a financial asset. If the entry route is wrong, your ROI calculation is irrelevant because your liquidity is frozen. I discuss the full tax and repatriation implications in our [NRI property guide](/blog/nri-property-guide-mohali-2026).

## Step 5: Registration via Power of Attorney (Structuring the POA safely)

You do not need to fly to India for the registry. You can execute a "Specific Power of Attorney" (POA) in your country of residence, get it apostilled or attested by the Indian Embassy, and send it to your trusted representative or advisor in Mohali.

However, I always advise: **Never give a General Power of Attorney.** Your POA should be limited only to the specific property and the specific task (like signing the registry or GMADA transfer documents). It should not give anyone the power to sell the property without your further consent. 

In Mohali, the POA must be "adjudicated" at the divisional commissioner's office before it can be used for a property registry. This is a procedural step I handle for my NRI clients regularly. We ensure the POA holder is someone you trust, and we keep the scope narrow to protect your ownership rights.

## What are the red flags to watch for in a remote deal?

If an advisor or broker says any of the following, walk away:
1. "Don't worry about RERA, it's a big brand." (Even big brands fail. Always verify).
2. "This offer is only for today, just transfer the booking amount now." (Manufactured urgency is the enemy of due diligence).
3. "The documents are with the bank, we will show them later." (If they can't show them now, they don't have them).

My time in capital markets taught me that the best investors are the ones who are comfortable saying "No" to a deal that feels rushed. Real estate is a long-term asset, 24 hours of extra due diligence will not cost you the deal, but it might save your capital. If you don't have a vision for the next 5-10 years, you are just gambling, not investing.

## Frequently Asked Questions about Buying Mohali Property Remotely

### Can I get a home loan for a Mohali property if I buy remotely?
Yes, most major banks like SBI, HDFC, and ICICI have dedicated NRI wings. You can apply and get a sanction remotely. The final disbursement usually requires your POA holder to sign the documents on your behalf in India. We help clients choose banks based on their country of residence, as some banks are faster with UK or Canada-based applicants.

### How do I verify if a property is under GMADA or a Municipal Committee?
This is a common point of confusion. GMADA controls the larger master plan and specific sectors like Aerocity and IT City. Older phases and some newer extensions fall under the Municipal Committee for building sanctions and property tax. We verify this by looking at the "Change of Land Use" (CLU) documents and the building plan sanction seal. This distinction changes your property tax liability and your resale process. For more on this, see our [guide on property advisor trust questions](/blog/mohali-property-advisor-nri-trust-questions).

### Is the registry process different for an NRI?
The process is the same, but the documentation includes your POA and your PAN card (or Form 60). If you are selling as an NRI, the TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) is significantly higher (around 22.66% for long-term gains), but for buying, the stamp duty and registration fees are identical to those for residents.

### What happens if the developer changes the layout after I book remotely?
This is why RERA is your best friend. Under RERA, a developer cannot make major changes to the sanctioned plan without the consent of two-thirds of the allottees. During our remote due diligence, we save a copy of the RERA-approved plan. If they deviate, we have the legal grounds to demand a refund with interest.

### How do I manage the property after the purchase is complete?
Remote management involves finding a verified tenant, drafting a digital rental agreement, and ensuring rent is credited to your NRO account. We provide post-sale support for all these steps, including getting your separate electricity connection and property tax ID.
