# How to Check a Mohali Developer's Delivery Track Record: The Data That Actually Matters

To check a Mohali developer’s delivery track record accurately, you must verify three specific data points: the RERA Punjab completion certificate (CC) dates against the original promised timelines, the volume of litigation in the RERA portal, and the physical state of past projects. Do not rely on marketing brochures that claim decades of excellence. Instead, visit the RERA Punjab portal, search for the developer's previous Special Purpose Vehicles (SPVs), and calculate the possession gap: the delta between the date on the allotment letter and the date the Occupancy Certificate (OC) was actually granted. A gap of more than 12 months across multiple projects is a systemic red flag. Finally, speak with residents at delivered sites to confirm if the possession offered was a legal handover with utilities or merely a fit-out possession used to stop penalty clocks. Having served as a director and sales head for a RERA-approved developer, I know that the truth of a builder’s reliability is found in their past files, not their current banners.

## What does RERA Punjab actually tell you about a developer's past?

Many buyers think that if a project is registered with RERA, it is safe. That is a misunderstanding. RERA is a transparency tool, not a guarantee of success. To see a developer's real history, you need to look past the current project. Go to the RERA Punjab website and search for the promoter's name. You will often find a list of several companies. In real estate, developers create a new company for every project. If you only search for the current project, you are seeing a blank slate. You must find the names of their previous SPVs. 

Once you find a past project, look for the Completion Certificate (CC) or Occupancy Certificate (OC) in the uploaded documents. Compare the date on that certificate with the Original Proposed Date of Completion listed in the initial registration. This is where the truth comes out. If a developer promised delivery in December 2022 but the CC was issued in June 2024, you are looking at an 18-month delay. In my experience, developers who delay by 18 months once tend to repeat that pattern because their cash flow cycles are built on those extended timelines.

## Why is the 'Promised vs Actual' possession gap the most important metric?

The reason I stress the possession gap is that it directly impacts your return on investment (ROI). As someone with a background in capital markets, I evaluate real estate through the lens of internal rate of return (IRR). If you buy a flat for ₹1.5 crore expecting possession in 3 years, but it takes 5 years, your holding cost has destroyed your profit. You have lost two years of rental income: roughly ₹8 to ₹10 lakh in Mohali's current market: and you have likely continued paying interest on a home loan for an unproductive asset. 

A developer with a clean track record of delivering within 6 months of the promised date is worth a 10% to 15% premium. A developer who consistently delivers 2 years late is actually selling you a more expensive product than the price on the paper suggests. When you are reading our [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), you will see that sectors like 82A and Aerocity have moved fast because the delivery of infrastructure stayed relatively close to the promised windows.

## How to distinguish between serious and cosmetic RERA complaints?

Not all complaints on the RERA portal are equal. When you are doing your [developer and project due diligence](/blog/mohali-developer-project-due-diligence-checklist), you must click through to the Orders section.

**Cosmetic Complaints:** These are often about minor snags, delay in club membership activation, or maintenance disputes. While annoying, they do not necessarily mean the project is a risk.
**Serious Complaints:** Look for orders related to refund with interest or non-execution of sale deed. If multiple buyers are asking for their money back because the project stalled, that is a terminal red flag. 

If you see a pattern where the RERA Authority has ordered a refund but the developer has not paid, it suggests a liquidity crisis. Having navigated project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and the Municipal Committee, I know that once the money stops flowing to the contractors, the regulatory clearances also stop.

## What should you look for during a physical site visit to a delivered project?

Brochures show you 3D renders; site visits show you the developer's character. I always tell my clients that the best way to predict your future is to look at someone else's present. Visit a project the developer delivered 3 to 5 years ago. Do not look at the paint; look at the bones:
1. **The Basement:** Is there seepage? Seepage in the basement of a 3-year-old building in Mohali suggests poor RCC quality or a lack of proper waterproofing.
2. **The Lifts:** Are they a Tier-1 brand or a local assembly? Lift maintenance is one of the highest recurring costs for a society. 
3. **The Common Areas:** Has the developer handed over the maintenance corpus to the Residents Welfare Association (RWA)? 

If the previous project looks dilapidated, your future project will likely follow suit once the sales team has moved on to the next site.

## The 'Silent' track record: Why talking to current residents is non-negotiable

The most accurate data in Mohali real estate is in the lobbies of delivered buildings. If you want to know the truth about a developer, talk to the people who already gave them money. Ask them if the builder asked for extra hidden charges at the time of possession. Refer to our list of [hidden costs in Mohali property](/blog/hidden-costs-mohali-property-purchase) for what to watch for.

Ask if the electricity connection was provided by PSPCL immediately, or if they were running on DG sets for the first year. Also, ask how long it took to get the registry done after moving in. I have seen cases where possession was given but the [PUDA approved status](/blog/puda-approved-project-mohali-verify) was still in limbo, meaning residents could not get their properties registered for years. This silent delay is often more painful than the construction delay because you are living in the house but do not legally own it yet. 

## The Reality of Escrow Accounts in Mohali
RERA mandates that 70% of the money collected from buyers must be kept in a separate escrow account and used only for construction and land costs. A developer with a strong track record will be transparent about this. If you ask for the escrow account number and the bank details, and the sales team gets defensive, you have your answer. When I evaluate projects for HNI and CXO clients, I look for escrow discipline. If a developer is using your money to buy more land instead of finishing your building, they are not a developer: they are a land-banker playing with your capital.

## What are the red flags in a developer's RERA filings?

Beyond the dates, look at the Quarterly Progress Reports (QPR):
* **Missing Quarters:** If the developer has not updated their progress for 6 months, they are hiding something or their compliance team has checked out.
* **Stagnant Work:** If the percentage of work completed stays the same for two quarters, the site is likely soft-stalled.
* **Financial Encumbrances:** Check if the land is mortgaged to a bank. This is normal, but you must ensure the developer has an NOC for Sale from the bank.

Don't be fooled by award-winning developers. Many awards are based on marketing spend, not delivery speed. I prefer a developer who has delivered two projects on time over one who has won ten awards but has three ongoing litigations on Airport Road.
