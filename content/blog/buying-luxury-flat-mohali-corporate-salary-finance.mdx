For a senior corporate executive earning ₹40 lakh or more annually, the most tax-efficient way to finance a ₹3 to 5 crore luxury flat in Mohali is through a balanced mix of a 70:30 or 80:20 home loan to down payment ratio. This structure allows you to maximize Section 24(b) deductions of ₹2 lakh on interest for self-occupied property, or unlimited interest set-off for a "deemed let-out" second property. To optimize further, you should switch from HRA to home loan benefits once the interest component exceeds your HRA exemption. Additionally, leveraging a "company housing allowance" model, where you rent the property back to your employer, can provide significant tax arbitrage. For properties in this bracket, the goal is not just owning the asset but managing the liability to lower your effective cost of borrowing while benefiting from Mohali's 12 to 15% annual appreciation in growth corridors like Sector 82A or the IT City belt.

## Should you prioritize a high down payment or a maximum home loan for a ₹3 to 5 crore purchase?

When you are looking at a ticket size of ₹3 to 5 crore, your natural instinct as a successful professional might be to clear the debt as quickly as possible. I see this often with CXOs who have accumulated significant bonuses or ESOP liquidations. However, from a capital markets perspective, if your home loan is costing you 8.5% to 9% and your capital is earning 12% to 15% in a diversified portfolio or through real estate appreciation, the math favors leverage.

In Mohali's current market, I advise my CXO clients to maintain a 75% to 80% loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. The reason is simple: liquidity and tax efficiency. By keeping more of your capital in liquid investments, you maintain an "opportunity fund." If a distress deal or a high-value pre-launch opportunity arises in a vision corridor like Airport Road, you have the cash ready. Furthermore, the cost of your home loan is significantly reduced by tax savings. For someone in the 30% plus surcharge tax bracket, an 8.5% interest rate effectively feels like 6% after accounting for the deductions you will claim.

## How do you optimize HRA, Section 24, and joint ownership for maximum tax savings?

Many senior executives are already living in a rented villa and claiming substantial HRA. When you move to your own ₹4 crore flat, you lose that HRA exemption. The rule of thumb is this: if your HRA exemption is higher than ₹2 lakh, you might actually pay more tax after buying your home. However, for a ₹3 crore loan, your interest component in the first few years will be roughly ₹25 lakh annually. If you treat your new Mohali luxury flat as a second property, you can set off the entire ₹25 lakh interest against "Income from House Property."

To truly optimize, you must look at joint ownership. If you and your spouse are both earning high salaries, you can both claim the ₹2 lakh deduction for interest and the ₹1.5 lakh deduction for principal repayment under Section 80C. This doubles your immediate tax relief. By documenting the intent to earn rental income or future capital gains, you move into a different category of financial management. I have seen clients save upwards of ₹5 lakh in annual taxes simply by moving from a self-occupied mental model to an asset management model. ## What is a "Company Housing Allowance" architecture and how does it work?

For those at the VP or Director level, your company might offer a "Leased Accommodation" or "Company Owned Accommodation" (COA) policy. If your company policy allows it, you can buy the property in your name or your spouse's name and then lease it back to your company. The company then "allots" this house back to you as your official residence. The company pays the rent to you, which you use to service your home loan. 

In many cases, the rent you receive from the company is higher than the tax you pay on the perquisite. This effectively means your employer is paying off a significant portion of your ₹4 crore luxury flat. This is a common strategy in Gurgaon and Mumbai that is now becoming popular in Mohali as more MNCs move into the IT City corridor. It is about using your professional status to fund your personal asset growth.

## What are the hidden financial risks in luxury pre-launch bookings for CXOs?

The allure of a pre-launch discount is strong. Developers often offer CXOs a special rate before the RERA registration is even public. Having been a developer myself, I know how these prices are set. The risk isn't just that the project might stall; it is that the discount is often offset by a longer holding period. 

If the project takes 5 years instead of 3, your IRR drops significantly because your capital is locked without any tax benefit or rental yield. Before you commit your hard-earned corporate bonus to a pre-launch project, verify the developer's escrow management. RERA has made things better, but as an advisor, I still look for the cash flow health of the developer.
