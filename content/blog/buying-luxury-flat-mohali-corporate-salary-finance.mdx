This structure allows you to maximize Section 24(b) deductions of ₹2 lakh on interest for self-occupied property, or unlimited interest set-off for a "deemed let-out" second property. To optimize further, you should switch from HRA to home loan benefits once the interest component exceeds your HRA exemption. Additionally, leveraging a "company housing allowance" model, where you rent the property back to your employer, can provide significant tax arbitrage. For properties in this bracket, the goal is not just owning the asset but managing the liability to lower your effective cost of borrowing while benefiting from Mohali's 12 to 15% annual appreciation in growth corridors like Sector 82A or the IT City belt.
In Mohali's current market, I advise my CXO clients to maintain a 75% to 80% loan-to-value (LTV) ratio. The reason is simple: liquidity and tax efficiency. By keeping more of your capital in liquid investments, you maintain an "opportunity fund." If a distress deal or a high-value pre-launch opportunity arises in a vision corridor like Airport Road, you have the cash ready. Furthermore, the cost of your home loan is significantly reduced by tax savings.
