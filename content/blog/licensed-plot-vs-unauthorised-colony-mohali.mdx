The primary difference between a licensed plot and an unauthorised colony in Mohali is the legal authority's approval, which directly dictates your ability to secure a home loan and the property's eventual resale liquidity. A licensed plot follows Greater Mohali Area Development Authority (GMADA) and RERA guidelines, while an unauthorised colony lacks a Change of Land Use (CLU) certificate and valid developer licensing. This distinction is critical because banks do not provide financing for illegal colonies, and the government frequently conducts demolition drives in such areas. For investors and land sellers, buying into an unauthorised colony means trading legal safety for a lower entry price, a decision that often leads to a total loss of resale value when the time comes to exit the investment.

### The Legal Framework: Understanding PAPRA and GMADA

In Punjab, real estate development is governed by the Punjab Apartment and Property Regulation Act (PAPRA). This act mandates that any developer intending to carve out a colony must first obtain a license from the competent authority, which in the case of Mohali is GMADA. 

A licensed colony undergoes a rigorous vetting process. The developer must prove ownership of the land, obtain a Change of Land Use (CLU) from agricultural to residential or commercial, and pay External Development Charges (EDC) and License Fees. These charges are used by the government to provide arterial roads, electricity grids, and water supply to the area.

Conversely, an unauthorised colony is built in total violation of these laws. These are often agricultural lands that have been subdivided by local land aggregators without any formal approvals. According to reports in The Tribune, GMADA has identified hundreds of such illegal colonies in the periphery of Zirakpur, Kharar, and New Chandigarh. Investors who buy here are essentially purchasing a share in a legal dispute rather than a piece of real estate.

### The Allure of the Unauthorised Colony: Why Buyers Fall for the Trap

The most significant draw of an unauthorised colony is the price. In many sectors of Mohali, a licensed plot might cost significantly more than a plot of the same size in an adjoining illegal colony. For a land seller who has just received a payout and is looking to maximize their acreage, the "discount" offered by unauthorised developers looks like a bargain.

Marketing tactics often include promises that the colony will be "regularized" by the government soon. However, regularization is not a guarantee. Even when it happens, the property owners are often slapped with heavy compounding fees and development charges that bridge the initial price gap. Furthermore, as discussed on our YouTube channel @Amritrealty, the infrastructure in these "regularized" colonies rarely matches the standards of planned GMADA sectors.

### Why the Distinction Destroys Resale Value

The real tragedy of buying in an unauthorised colony reveals itself when you try to sell. In the real estate market, liquidity is driven by the availability of home loans. 

#### 1. The Banking Barrier
Nationalized and private banks like SBI, HDFC, and ICICI have strict compliance departments. They require a "Title Search Report" and a "No Objection Certificate" (NOC) from GMADA. If the colony is unauthorised, the bank will refuse the loan. This immediately eliminates 80 percent of your potential buyer pool. You are left selling only to "cash buyers," who will demand a massive discount, further eroding your resale value.

#### 2. The Registry and Mutation Hurdle
While some unauthorised colonies manage to get "registries" done through legal loopholes or during periods of relaxed norms, the "Mutation" (Inteqal) in revenue records can become a nightmare. Without a clear mutation in favor of the buyer, the legal title remains shaky. Smart buyers today are well aware of the [Mohali property registration process guide](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026) and will avoid any property that does not have a clean paper trail.

#### 3. The Threat of Demolition
The Punjab government and GMADA have become increasingly aggressive. Headlines in the Economic Times often highlight demolition drives where boundary walls, sewage lines, and even houses in illegal colonies are razed to the ground. A property under the shadow of a demolition notice has zero resale value.

### Infrastructure: The Invisible Cost of "Cheap" Plots

When you buy a licensed plot, you are paying for more than just the earth. You are paying for a lifestyle and basic human rights like clean water and safe roads.

In a licensed colony, the developer is legally bound to provide:
*   Structured storm-water drainage.
*   Paved roads of a minimum width (usually 30 to 40 feet).
*   Dedicated green belts and parks.
*   Underground electricity cabling.

In an unauthorised colony, these amenities are either missing or poorly executed. Roads are often narrow, barely allowing two cars to pass. Without a proper drainage system, these colonies become flooded during the monsoon, a common sight in the outskirts of Kharar. When you attempt to sell such a plot, a discerning buyer will look at the crumbling infrastructure and walk away, or offer a price that reflects the poor living conditions. ### The 5-Point Verification Checklist for Mohali Buyers

Before signing any cheque, you must verify the licensing status of the project. Do not rely on the developer's brochures. Use these objective steps:

1.  **Check the RERA Punjab Portal:** Every licensed project must be registered with the Real Estate Regulatory Authority. Search for the project's registration number on the official RERA Punjab website. If it is not there, it is likely unauthorised. See our guide on [how to check RERA Punjab property](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property) for a step-by-step walkthrough.
2.  **Verify the CLU:** Ask for a copy of the Change of Land Use certificate issued by the Town and Country Planning Department, Punjab.
3.  **Check the Layout Approval:** Ensure the layout plan has been signed and stamped by the Chief Town Planner.
4.  **Confirm the License to Develop:** This document is the ultimate proof that the developer has paid the necessary fees to the government.
5.  **Search the GMADA "Blacklist":** GMADA regularly updates a list of unauthorised colonies on its website. Ensure the area you are looking at is not on that list.
