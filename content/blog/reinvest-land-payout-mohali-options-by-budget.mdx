## The Shift from Agricultural to Urban Assets in Punjab

The landscape of Punjab real estate is undergoing a massive transformation. According to reports from the Tribune and Economic Times, the expansion of the Ludhiana-Ropar Expressway and the Delhi-Amritsar-Katra Expressway has injected significant liquidity into the rural economy. Farmers and landowners are no longer looking for more agricultural land in distant districts. Instead, the trend is "Urbanization of Capital."

Mohali (SAS Nagar) has emerged as the primary beneficiary of this trend. Unlike the saturated market of Chandigarh or the disjointed development in parts of Ludhiana, Mohali offers planned GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority) sectors. When you reinvest land payout Mohali options by budget, you are not just buying "bricks and mortar," you are buying into the infrastructure of the future.



## Budget Tier 1: ₹50 Lakhs to ₹80 Lakhs (Entry-Level Growth)

At this price point, the investor is looking for maximum "unit count" or entry into the Mohali ecosystem. 

### Residential Apartments
In 2026, the ₹50L to ₹80L bracket is dominated by high-rise developments in the Kharar-Kurali road belt and the Landran-Banur road. These areas are popular among the young IT workforce and university students. A 3BHK in a gated community with basic amenities fits well within this budget. While the immediate rental yield is 3% to 4%, the capital appreciation is driven by the widening of internal arterial roads.

### Peripheral Plots
Small plots (80 to 100 square yards) in private developer projects approved by RERA Punjab are available. These are often located in "Extension" zones. For a land seller, this is a "set and forget" investment. It mirrors the land ownership they are used to but in a residential format. On our YouTube channel @Amritrealty, we often highlight how these peripheral colonies eventually get absorbed into the main city grid.

## Budget Tier 2: ₹1 Crore to ₹1.8 Crores (The Core Mid-Market)

This is the most active segment for Punjab land sellers. It represents the "Sweet Spot" where you get prime GMADA-linked locations without the extreme premium of Chandigarh.

### IT City and Aerocity (Plots)
If your payout is around ₹1.5 crore, your first look should be at IT City (Sectors 101, 82A, 83). While prices have climbed significantly since the initial allotments, the 100 and 150 square yard categories are still accessible for those with a ₹1.5 crore+ budget. These plots are highly liquid. If you need to sell in five years, there is always a buyer for IT City.

### Luxury Independent Floors
In sectors like 82 and 83, the concept of "Boutique Floors" has taken over. A stilt+3 or stilt+4 configuration on a 250 square yard plot offers a high-end living experience. For land sellers who want to move their families to the city, this budget provides a luxury 3BHK or 4BHK floor with modern fittings, elevators, and dedicated parking.

## Budget Tier 3: ₹2 Crores to ₹3.5 Crores (High-Yield Commercial and Luxury)

When the payout exceeds ₹2 crore, the strategy should pivot toward income generation and tax saving under Section 54 of the Income Tax Act.

### Commercial SCOs (Smaller Configurations)
A budget of ₹3 crore allows for a down payment or full purchase of a smaller SCO (Shop-cum-Office) or a commercial bay in developing blocks. Sector 82 (specifically 82A where Realty Holding and Management Consultants is located) is a hub for these investments. The rental income from a commercial property is significantly higher than residential, often touching 6% to 8% in high-footfall areas.

### Large Residential Plots (250-500 Sq. Yards)
For those who wish to build a multi-generational home, 300 to 500 square yard plots in Sector 66, 67, or the newer GMADA Eco-City phases are viable. These areas are characterized by wide roads, underground utilities, and proximity to the International Airport.



## Budget Tier 4: ₹4 Crores to ₹5 Crores and Beyond (Wealth Preservation)

This is the "Institutional" tier of individual reinvestment. At this level, you are looking at landmark assets.

### Prime Commercial SCOs
A full-sized SCO on the PR-7 Airport Road or in the heart of Sector 82 is the gold standard. These properties are leased by banks, luxury car showrooms, or national food chains. The stability provided by a corporate lease is the perfect hedge against the volatility often found in agricultural income.

### Industrial Plots
With Mohali becoming a manufacturing and warehousing hub for the tricity, industrial plots in the JLPL (Janta Land Promoters Ltd) sectors or GMADA industrial phases have seen massive growth. A ₹5 crore budget allows for entry into the industrial real estate sector, which benefits from various state government subsidies and high demand for logistics space.

## Tax Implications: Section 54 and 54F

One of the biggest mistakes land sellers make is not planning for Capital Gains Tax. If your agricultural land was within a specific distance from the municipal limits, it might be subject to tax. 

1. **Section 54:** Allows you to save tax by investing the capital gains in another residential property.
2. **Section 54F:** Allows you to save tax by investing the net consideration in a residential house if the original asset was not a residential house (like a commercial plot or agricultural land).
