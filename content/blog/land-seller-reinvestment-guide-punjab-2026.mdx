### Section 1: The Tax Shield: Navigating Section 10(37), 54, and 54F

Taxation is the single largest "leak" in any land seller’s payout. In Punjab, where agricultural land is often the primary asset being acquired for projects like Aerotropolis or the Bharatmala bypass, understanding the Income Tax Act is as vital as the property deal itself.

#### 1.1 The Section 10(37) Powerhouse
For many of our clients, Section 10(37) is the ultimate wealth preservation tool. This provision grants a 100% exemption on capital gains arising from the compulsory acquisition of urban agricultural land for individuals and HUFs. 

**The Conditions are Strict:**
- The land must have been used for agricultural purposes for at least two years prior to acquisition.
- It must be a "compulsory acquisition" (Awards from the Land Acquisition Collector).
- The compensation must be received on or after April 1, 2004.

**Amritpal’s Advisory Note on Interest:** A critical technical trap exists here. Following the *Mahender Pal Narang vs. CBDT (2020)* ruling by the Punjab & Haryana High Court, the "Principal" and "Enhanced Compensation" are exempt, but the **Interest** received under Section 28 is now treated as "Income from Other Sources" and is taxable. You get a 50% statutory deduction under Section 57(iv), but the remaining 50% is taxed at your slab rate. We have seen families lose ₹20 Lakhs in avoidable tax simply because they didn't separate the interest component in their tax planning.

#### 1.2 Section 54 vs. Section 54F
If your sale was a private transaction or involves non-agricultural land, you must look at the reinvestment exemptions:
- **Section 54:** Buying a residential house from the gains of another residential house.
- **Section 54F:** Buying a residential house from the *total sale proceeds* of any other asset (like a commercial plot or SCO).

**The 24-Month Clock:** You have two years to buy or three years to construct. If you miss this, the tax liability can hit 20% (plus surcharges). This is why the Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS) is mandatory. If you haven't identified a property by the time you file your ITR, the money **must** go into a CGAS account at a nationalized bank to preserve your exemption eligibility.

#### 1.3 Section 54EC: The "Bonds" Safety Valve
If you are unable to find a suitable property within the timeline, you can invest up to ₹50 Lakhs in specified bonds (REC, NHAI, PFC, IRFC) under Section 54EC. While the interest rate on these bonds is typically lower than the market (around 5, 5.25%), they provide an immediate tax deferral. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we usually recommend this only as a last resort or for a small portion of the payout that isn't allocated to growth assets.

### Section 5: Technical Due Diligence: The Realty Holding and Management Consultants 9-Point Checklist

When you receive a payout, you become a "target" for every developer and broker in the state. They will offer "assured returns" and "pre-launch discounts." Use our 9-point filter before you sign a single cheque.

1.  **RERA Punjab Verification:** Always check `rera.punjab.gov.in`. Look at the "Quarterly Progress Reports." If the developer hasn't updated them for two quarters, it’s a red flag.
2.  **CLU and Layout Approval:** A "PUDA Approved" stamp is not enough. You must verify the Change of Land Use (CLU) certificate specifically for the project name and the exact survey numbers.
3.  **The Loading Factor:** In Mohali, an acceptable loading factor is 25-30%. If a developer is selling you a 2000 sq. ft. commercial unit with only 1200 sq. ft. carpet area, that is 40% loading: a major "invisible tax" on your investment.
4.  **PSPCL Load Sanction:** Does the project have the sanctioned electrical load from Punjab State Power Corporation Limited? Without this, you will be dependent on diesel generators, which can eat up 20% of your rental income in maintenance charges.
5.  **Separate Property Tax IDs:** Especially for SCO floors, ensure the Municipal Committee has issued separate Property IDs. Shared liability is the number one cause of "frozen" transactions during resale.
6.  **The "Nala" and "Forest" Clearance:** Many projects near the Ghaggar or the Shivalik foothills are under "Forest and Conservation Authority" restrictions. A cancelled Forest Department approval can stall a project for a decade.
7.  **Circle Rate vs. Agreement Value:** Ensure the transaction is transparent. Stamp duty in Mohali is 8% for males and 6% for females. Any "under the table" cash component increases your legal risk and reduces your ability to show reinvestment for capital gains.
8.  **Mutation (Intkal) Status:** Check if the developer actually owns the land or just has a "Collaboration Agreement." A direct title is always safer for long-term hold.
9.  **Maintenance MOU:** Review the maintenance charges. For luxury flats in 2026, ₹7 to ₹8 per sq. ft. is the standard for full service. Anything higher is a drain on your yield.

### Section 7: Regulatory Navigation: GMADA, PUDA, and Beyond

Navigating the bureaucracy in Punjab requires more than just knowing the rules: it requires knowing the process.

- **GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority):** Handles plot allotments, transfers, and licensing for most of Mohali's urban sectors.
- **PUDA (Punjab Urban Planning and Development Authority):** The parent body often involved in Change of Land Use (CLU) and regional planning.
- **Municipal Committee (MC):** Handles building sanctions, Occupation Certificates (OC), and property tax once a sector is handed over by GMADA.
- **Forest Department:** Critical for any project near the Shivalik belt or protected green zones.
