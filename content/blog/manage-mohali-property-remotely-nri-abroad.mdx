### The Challenge of Distance: Beyond Family Favors

For many NRIs in Toronto, Vancouver, or London, the default strategy for managing a property in Mohali has historically been "the relative route." While well-intentioned, this often leads to the two most common issues we see at Realty Holding and Management Consultants: neglect and awkwardness. A family member might ignore a damp patch in your Sector 82 flat because they are too busy with their own work, or they might feel hesitant to push a tenant for late rent.

Managing a Mohali property remotely requires shifting from an informal arrangement to a structured business process. The Mohali real estate market in 2026 is far more regulated than it was a decade ago. With the strict implementation of RERA Punjab and the digitization of GMADA records, "winging it" from abroad is no longer a viable strategy for long term capital appreciation.



### The Financial Framework: NRE/NRO and Tax Compliance

The first step in remote management is ensuring your financial plumbing is leak-proof. Rent from an Indian property must be credited to an NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) account. 

1. **Rent Collection and TDS:** Under Section 195 of the Income Tax Act, a tenant is technically required to deduct TDS (Tax Deducted at Source) at a rate of 31.2% before paying rent to an NRI. However, many owners overlook this. If you are managing remotely, ensure your tenant is aware of this or apply for a Lower Deduction Certificate to reduce this burden.
2. **Automated Payments:** Never rely on cheques being couriered or picked up. Ensure your lease agreement mandates bank transfers. This creates a clear audit trail which is essential for your annual tax filings in both India and your home country (Canada/UK).
3. **Repatriation:** Funds in your NRO account can be repatriated up to USD 1 million per financial year, provided you have the chartered accountant certificates (15CA/15CB) ready.

### Regulatory Compliance: GMADA and Municipal Committee Duties

Mohali is unique because of the dual jurisdiction of GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority) and the local Municipal Committee (MC). Remote management is not just about keeping a tenant; it is about keeping the "paper" clean.

**Property Tax IDs:** A common mistake we find during our audits for NRI clients is the lack of separate property tax IDs for multi-floor buildings. If you own a floor in a plot in Sector 70 or 82A, and the tax is being paid collectively for the whole building, you are at risk. If one owner defaults, the entire building’s clearance is stalled. We have seen cases where GMADA transfers were delayed by months because of a back-dated tax liability of just a few thousand rupees.

**Annual Maintenance Audits:** Use video calls to perform quarterly walkthroughs. At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we often send our team to record high definition videos of the property, focusing on "hidden" areas like the roof, plumbing joints, and electrical panels. Seeing the property via a WhatsApp video call is good, but having a recorded report is better for tracking wear and tear over years.

### Tenant Vetting: The Punjab Police Verification

In Punjab, and specifically in the Mohali-Chandigarh-Panchkula tricity area, police verification for tenants is not optional; it is a legal requirement. As an NRI owner, you can be held legally liable for any illegal activities conducted on your premises if you have not completed this process.

When you manage Mohali property remotely, your local manager must:
* Collect Aadhaar and permanent address proof from the tenant.
* Submit the verification form at the local police station (e.g., Phase 8 or Sector 82 police posts).
* Keep a stamped copy of the acknowledgment in your digital vault.

This process protects you from the "professional tenant" who might try to overstay or create a title dispute. We discuss these protections further in our article on [how NRIs can verify property advisors](/blog/mohali-property-advisor-nri-trust-questions).

### Maintenance Coordination: Proactive vs Reactive

Distance often turns a small leak into a structural disaster. In Mohali, the extreme weather (45-degree summers and heavy monsoons) takes a toll on buildings. 

* **The Monsoon Check:** Every May, before the rains hit, your property manager should check the terrace drainage and waterproofing.
* **The AC Service:** If you provide appliances, annual maintenance contracts (AMCs) are essential.
* **The Repair Fund:** Keep a small "float" (approx Rs 20,000-50,000) with your local advisor or in your NRO account specifically for emergency repairs. This prevents the need for an international wire transfer for a broken water pump.



### The Power of Local Representation (Liaisoning)

Managing from Canada or the UK means you cannot stand in line at the PSPCL (Punjab State Power Corporation Limited) office if there is a bill discrepancy, nor can you visit the Forest Department if a nearby green belt issue affects your property value.

A professional property manager or advisor acts as your "boots on the ground." For example, we recently assisted an NRI client where a neighbor had encroached on a shared boundary wall. Because we were monitoring the property weekly, we caught it within 48 hours and resolved it through the Municipal Committee before any permanent structure was built. Had the owner waited for their annual trip to India, the cost of litigation would have far exceeded the cost of a year's worth of management fees.
