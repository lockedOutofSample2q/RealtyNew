# Mohali Sector-by-Sector Property Guide 2026: Where to Buy, Where to Wait, Where to Avoid

In 2026, the best places to buy property in Mohali are the "Expansion Corridors"—specifically Sector 82A (₹13,500 psf), the IT City belt (₹8,500 psf), and Aerocity (₹11,500 psf)—where infrastructure triggers like the Airport expansion and Bharatmala connectivity are still driving appreciation. You should "Wait" in New Chandigarh (Mullanpur) until utility reality catches up with marketing promises, and "Avoid" land-locked, stagnant zones like Phase 7 or the 3B2 commercial belt, where prices have hit a ceiling due to zero expansion capacity. For residential safety, Sector 76 and 77 (₹10,500 psf) remain the GMADA-backed gold standard. Successful investing in today's Mohali market requires moving beyond "location" and adopting the "Expansion Principle": buy where new roads, new companies, and new project phases are physically arriving, rather than where the story has already finished.

## The Mohali Real Estate Framework: Understanding the 2026 Map

If we were walking through the master plan at my office in Sector 82A, I’d tell you that Mohali is no longer a single market. It is a collection of micro-markets, each with its own gravity. In my decade of navigating approvals across PUDA, GMADA, and the Forest Department, I’ve learned that a sector’s "feel" matters less than its "zoning."

The 200-foot Airport Road has divided the city into the "Old Mohali" (Phases 1-11) and the "New Growth Corridors" (Sectors 66-109). If you are looking at the [complete Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), you’ll see that the wealth has shifted south and east. The buyers who made 200% returns since 2019 are the ones who bought into the vision of Airport Road when it was just a sketch. 

Today, we apply the **Expansion Principle**. Je aapaan vision ton bina challaange taan aapaan mehngi cheez khareeddaange (If we move without vision, we will buy expensive things). This means looking for sectors where the "delta"—the gap between what is built and what is possible—is still large.

## Sector 82A: The Luxury and Commercial Heartbeat

**Current Price Range:** ₹12,500 – ₹14,500 psf (Residential) | ₹12Cr – ₹16Cr (Commercial Units)

Sector 82A is the strongest "Buy" for HNI investors and corporate CXOs. Having handled over 180 transactions, I’ve seen this sector transform from an industrial periphery into the most coveted luxury address in the Tricity. 

What makes it a winner? Connectivity. It sits at the intersection of Airport Road and the IT City belt. I remember when [Sector 82A property prices](/blog/sector-82a-mohali-property-price-2026) were at ₹4,500 psf in 2019. The jump to ₹13,500 today isn't a bubble; it's the result of corporate headquarters, luxury hotels, and high-end retail moving in.

**Honest Verdict: BUY.** This is the "Gurgaon of Mohali." If you want rental yields of 3-4% on luxury flats or the prestige of a JLPL-backed address, this is the sector. It has the highest liquidity in the market.

## Aerocity: The GMADA-Backed Gateway

**Current Price Range:** ₹10,500 – ₹12,500 psf (Plots)

Aerocity remains the most organized GMADA development. For an NRI buyer who wants clean titles and wide roads without the headache of private developer defaults, this is the safe play. It is divided into blocks (A to J), and the blocks closer to the Airport Road (A, B, C) command a significant premium.

I often tell [NRI investors](/blog/nri-property-guide-mohali-2026) that Aerocity is a "Buy and Hold" asset. It isn't speculative anymore; it is functional. With the hotel cluster and international terminal proximity, it has a "peace of mind premium" that few private projects can match.

**Honest Verdict: BUY FOR SAFETY.** You are paying for the GMADA stamp. Don't expect the 200% jumps of the past, but expect consistent 8-12% annual appreciation and zero title risk.

## Mullanpur (New Chandigarh): The "Wait and Verify" Zone

**Current Price Range:** ₹8,500 – ₹10,500 psf

New Chandigarh is a masterclass in how marketing can get ahead of reality. There are stunning "branded" projects here, but from my experience managing project documentation, the infrastructure delivery is uneven. I have seen families buy luxury villas only to discover that the sewerage connectivity or the PSPCL load sanction for the sector is still 18 months away.

Before you commit, check the [Mullanpur property guide](/blog/mullanpur-new-chandigarh-property-2026) for specific project delivery data. The Master Plan 2031 is ambitious, but the execution is corridor-dependent.

**Honest Verdict: WAIT.** Only buy if the project has a physical Occupancy Certificate (OC) and live utility connections. Don't buy on the promise of the "upcoming" 200ft road—buy when the asphalt is laid.

## Sector 76 and 77: The Residential Benchmark

**Current Price Range:** ₹9,500 – ₹11,500 psf

These sectors are the residential heart of Mohali. They are GMADA-allotted and perfectly positioned between the old city and the new airport corridors. In 2019, prices were at ₹3,500 psf. The 200% growth here was driven by the completion of civil services.

If you are a family relocating from rural Punjab, [Sector 76-77](/blog/sector-76-77-mohali-property-2026) offers a community feel that Aerocity lacks. It is "built-out" and safe.

**Honest Verdict: BUY FOR END-USE.** This is where you build your home. As an investment, it is stable, but the explosive growth phase has likely plateaued.

## Kharar IT City Belt: The Undervalued Growth Corridor

**Current Price Range:** ₹7,500 – ₹9,000 psf

This is my top recommendation for "Vision Investing" in 2026. While everyone is focused on Sector 82A at ₹13,500 psf, the IT City expansion toward Kharar is offering similar corporate-linked growth at a 35% discount. 

As someone who tracks [real estate investment analysis](/blog/mohali-real-estate-investment-analysis-2026), I see the IT corridor shifting. Companies like Infosys and the various IT SEZs are creating a massive demand for mid-segment housing that the core city cannot fulfill.

**Honest Verdict: STRONG BUY.** This is where the next 50% jump will happen. Use our [Kharar IT City guide](/blog/kharar-it-city-property-investment-2026) to identify specific project clusters with RERA approval.

## Sunny Enclave (Sector 105-109): The Entry-Level HNI Play

**Current Price Range:** ₹5,500 – ₹7,000 psf

Sunny Enclave is where many [Land Sellers](/blog/land-seller-reinvestment-guide-punjab-2026) first enter the Mohali market. It offers large plots at a price point that established sectors can't match. However, it is a "developer-led" colony, meaning the infrastructure depends on the promoter, not just GMADA.

I have seen buyers get stuck here because they didn't verify the licensing status. Always distinguish between a "Licensed Colony" and an "Unauthorised" one before signing.

**Honest Verdict: BUY FOR BUDGET.** If you want a 500 sq. yard plot but only have the budget for a 250 sq. yard plot in Aerocity, [Sunny Enclave](/blog/sunny-enclave-sector-105-mohali-property) is your destination. Just do double the due diligence.

## Sector 70-72 and Phase 7: The Stagnation Zones

**Current Price Range:** ₹14,000 – ₹16,000 psf (Secondary Market)

These are the "Old Money" sectors. They are beautiful, green, and highly central. However, they suffer from what I call "Sector Fixation." Prices here are high because of sentiment, not because of growth capacity.

There is no more land to expand. Parking is a nightmare in the commercial pockets of 3B2 and Phase 7. While [rental yields in Mohali](/blog/rental-yield-mohali-property-2026) are generally healthy, they are actually lower here compared to the new corridors because the entry price is too high.

**Honest Verdict: AVOID FOR INVESTMENT.** You are buying at the absolute peak. There is no infrastructure trigger left to pull. Buy here only if you must live next to your parents or want the 1990s Mohali charm.

## Why Infrastructure Triggers Matter More Than Current PSF

In my years of [government liaisoning](/blog/rhmc-amritpal-singh-mohali-real-estate-consultant), I’ve seen projects triple in value not because of the building, but because of the road. 

Look at the **Bharatmala Project**. This road is going to link the Airport Road directly to the Rajpura-Patiala highway. Sectors sitting on this upcoming junction are currently undervalued. I recall an [industrial land case](/blog/industrial-land-investment-mohali-rajpura) where rates jumped from ₹18.70L to ₹45L per vigha in six months simply because the road connectivity was officially confirmed.

When you evaluate a sector, don't ask what it looks like today. Ask: "Where is the next road coming from?" and "Where is the next big company hiring?"

## Common Questions: Mohali Sector Guide 2026

### Which sector has the highest rental yield in Mohali?
In 2026, **Sector 82A and the IT City belt** offer the highest rental yields (3.5% - 4.5% for residential, 5% - 7% for commercial). This is driven by the high concentration of corporate professionals and the shortage of premium move-in-ready inventory.

### Is Mullanpur (New Chandigarh) part of Mohali?
Administratively, it falls under the jurisdiction of GMADA and the Punjab government, but it is marketed as a separate satellite of Chandigarh. For buyers, the [New Chandigarh Master Plan 2031](/blog/new-chandigarh-master-plan-2031-property) is the governing document, distinct from core Mohali's development phases.

### Why is Aerocity more expensive than Sector 76-77?
Aerocity commands a premium because of its proximity to the International Airport and the hotel cluster, making it a primary choice for NRIs and frequent travelers. Sector 76-77 is more residential and family-oriented, which reflects in its slightly lower speculative pricing.

### What should I check before buying in Sunny Enclave?
Verify the **PUDA/GMADA licensing status** and the "NOC" for the specific block. Unlike GMADA sectors, infrastructure here is developer-provided. Ensure the project is RERA-registered and the seller has a clear "Mutation" (Intkal) in their name.

## The Honest Verdict: My Top 3 Picks for 2026

If I were putting my own capital into the market today—as someone who has seen the "inside" of how developers price their inventory—these would be my moves:

1. **For Maximum Appreciation:** The Kharar-IT City extension. The price gap vs Sector 82A is too large to ignore.
2. **For Rental Income:** Commercial SCOs on the Airport Road junction near Sector 82. The [commercial vs residential](/blog/mohali-commercial-vs-residential-investment-2026) math favors these corridors heavily.
3. **For Family Living:** Sector 76 or 77. It is the perfect balance of safety, community, and appreciation.

Real estate is a game of patience and vision. If you buy where everyone else is already talking, you are paying for their profit. If you buy where the infrastructure is being laid tonight, you are securing your own.
