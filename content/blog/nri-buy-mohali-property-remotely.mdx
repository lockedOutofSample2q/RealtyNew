### The Live Video Walkthrough Protocol: Beyond the Marketing Clip

A pre-recorded marketing video is not due diligence. When buying remotely, you must insist on a live, unedited video walkthrough. This protocol should be conducted during daylight hours to assess natural light, ventilation, and the actual state of the neighborhood.

On our YouTube channel @Amritrealty, we emphasize that a remote buyer should direct the camera person like a remote-controlled drone. You should insist on seeing:

1. **The Approach Road:** Is the road 40 feet wide as promised, or is it a narrow lane? Check for potholes or unauthorized encroachments that a brochure would never show.
2. **The Peripheral View:** What is next to the project? Is there a vacant plot that might become a high-rise, or is there an existing industrial unit that could cause noise pollution?
3. **Internal Common Areas:** In a luxury apartment or SCO (Shop-cum-Office) complex, the quality of the lift lobby, the basement drainage, and the fire safety equipment tells you more about the developer than the actual unit.
4. **The "Hidden" Spots:** Ask to see the electricity transformer location, the garbage collection point, and the water tank area. These are the elements that determine the long-term livability and resale value of a Mohali property.

### Legal Verification: The RERA and GMADA Audit

In Mohali, the two pillars of legality are RERA Punjab and GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority). Remote buyers must verify that the project is not just "applied for" but fully approved.

The first step is a screen-share session where you or your consultant navigates the RERA Punjab official portal. You must check the registered completion date and the quarterly progress reports. If a developer has missed three consecutive updates, it is a red flag. Furthermore, for plots in sectors like 82A or 101, verifying the Allotment Letter and the Change of Land Use (CLU) certificate is mandatory.

During a video call, the seller or developer should hold the original documents under a bright light. You are looking for the embossed seals of the Punjab Government and the signatures of the Estate Officer. For a more comprehensive understanding of these authorities, you can refer to our [GMADA Mohali Explained Guide](/blog/gmada-mohali-explained-guide).

### Technical Due Diligence: Measuring from a Distance

One of the greatest fears for an NRI buying remotely is the discrepancy between the "Super Area" and the "Carpet Area." In Mohali, developers often quote the super area, which includes common spaces. 

Your remote due diligence protocol must include a live measurement session. Your representative should carry a digital laser measurer. As the red dot hits the wall, you can see the actual dimensions on your screen. This ensures that the "luxury master bedroom" is actually 12x14 feet and not a cramped 10x10. 

Additionally, check the quality of fixtures. If the agreement specifies Kohler or Jaquar fittings, the video call should focus on the branding on the taps and sanitary ware. The Tribune has frequently reported on "bait and switch" tactics where developers use premium names in brochures but install local substitutes in the final units. Remote verification stops this in its tracks.

### Financial Safeguards and the Paper Trail

The financial aspect of buying property in Mohali remotely requires strict adherence to FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) guidelines. All payments must originate from your NRE or NRO account. 

Before any token money is transferred, you must have a digital copy of the 'Agreement to Sell' reviewed by a local legal expert. This document should explicitly state that the payment is contingent upon the verification of all original documents. 

In Mohali's secondary market (resale), the protocol is even stricter. You must insist on a "No Dues Certificate" from the developer or the Residents Welfare Association (RWA) and a verification of the "Transfer Letter" from GMADA. These documents must be scanned in high resolution and sent to you before the RTGS/NEFT transaction occurs. Using a trusted firm ensures that your funds are not released until the "acknowledgment of transfer" is received from the government office.

### Understanding the Mohali Market Micro-Climates

Mohali is not a monolithic market. Each sector has its own growth trajectory. For instance, the demand in IT City (Sectors 82, 83, 101) is driven by corporate leasing, while Aerocity is dominated by commercial SCOs and retail demand. 

As an NRI, your remote due diligence must include a "market sentiment check." This involves looking at the occupancy rates of nearby projects. A live video of the neighboring society's parking lot at 8:00 PM can tell you more about the occupancy and rental potential than any spreadsheet. If the parking lot is empty, the rental yields will be low, regardless of what the developer claims.

### Avoiding Common Remote Buying Pitfalls

There are several traps that NRIs often fall into when buying without visiting:

*   **The "Sample Flat" Illusion:** Developers often show a beautifully furnished sample flat. You must insist on seeing the "Actual Site Unit." The view from the sample flat might be a garden, while your actual unit might face a transformer or a slum area.
*   **Infrastructure Delays:** A project might be ready, but the GMADA sewerage line or the PSPCL electricity connection might be six months away. Your remote check must involve visiting the local department office or checking the status of external infrastructure.
*   **The "Hidden Cost" Trap:** Ensure that the IFMS (Interest-Free Maintenance Security), club membership, and power backup charges are all documented. On a video call, ask the sales manager to show the official price list with all these "other charges" clearly visible.
