# NRI Buying Mohali Property Without Visiting: What Remote Due Diligence Actually Looks Like

Buying Mohali property from abroad without a physical visit is entirely possible and safe if you follow a rigorous remote due diligence protocol. The process involves five mandatory steps: a live video site walkthrough to verify ground reality, digital verification of RERA and title documents, a legal review of the agreement to sale, payment exclusively through NRO/NRE accounts for FEMA compliance, and registration via a specific Power of Attorney (POA). By insisting on seeing "live" evidence of approvals and construction progress rather than relying on glossy brochures, an NRI can secure an investment in Mohali with the same level of certainty as a local buyer. This remote-first approach removes the need for expensive travel while ensuring that every regulatory check, from GMADA transfer eligibility to RERA compliance, is completed before a single rupee leaves your account.

## Can an NRI really trust a remote property purchase in Mohali?

Trust in real estate is not built on promises: it is built on verifiable data. Having been on every side of the table, as a consultant, a developer managing RERA-approved projects, and an advisor, I can tell you that the distance is only a risk if your process is weak. 

The reality of Mohali real estate in 2026 is that the digital infrastructure is now robust. Between the RERA Punjab portal, GMADA's online records, and high-quality video streaming, you can see more of a project from your living room in London than a buyer could see on-site a decade ago. 

Many NRIs come to me after having been burned by "virtual tours" that only showed the polished side of a project. I've spent over a decade navigating the complexities of PUDA, PSPCL, and the Municipal Committee. I know that what matters isn't the granite in the lobby, but the load sanction from the electricity department and the NOC from the Forest Department.

## Step 1: The Video Site Walkthrough Protocol (What to ask your advisor to show)

When I do a video walkthrough for a client in the UK or UAE, I am not showing them the "experience center" or the air-conditioned model flat. A proper remote site visit must focus on the "bones" of the project. 

Ask your advisor to show you:
1. **The Actual Tower and Floor:** Don't settle for "it will look like this." See the slab, the brickwork, and the distance from the neighbouring tower. 
2. **Construction Pace:** Are there workers on-site? Is the equipment moving? 
3. **The Surrounding Infrastructure:** Show me the approach road. Show me if the "green belt" promised in the brochure is actually a dumping ground. 
4. **Site Hygiene:** A developer who keeps a clean site usually builds a clean project. 

## Step 2: Document Verification on Video Call (Seeing the originals)

A PDF scan can be edited, but a live document on a video call is harder to fake. Before you commit, I always recommend a "Document Review Call." 

We go through:
* **The Original Allotment Letter:** Especially for GMADA plots, seeing the original stamp and the serial number is critical. 
* **RERA Registration:** We open the RERA Punjab website together on a screen share and verify the project's quarterly progress reports. 
* **Approved Building Maps:** I make sure the client sees the seal of the Municipal Committee or GMADA on the maps. 
* **NOCs:** Whether it is the Forest Department clearance or the PSPCL load sanction, we verify the document dates.

## Step 3: The Agreement to Sale Review (Before you sign anything)

The agreement to sale is the most important document you will ever sign in your property journey. As an advisor, I review these from the perspective of a former developer. I know exactly where the "one-sided" clauses are hidden.

We look for the "Possession Date" and the "Penalty Clause." We also check for the "Loading Factor." In Mohali, 25:30% is standard. If I see 35% or more, I tell my clients they are being overcharged for unusable space. For a deeper dive into these calculations, refer to our [complete buyer protection guide](/blog/mohali-property-buyer-protection-guide).

## Step 4: The Correct Payment Route (FEMA compliance is non-negotiable)

I've seen NRIs get into trouble because they used a resident relative's account to make payments. This is a violation of FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) rules.

The protocol is simple:
* All payments must originate from your **NRE or NRO account**.
* Never pay in "cash" or via unofficial channels. 
* Ensure the developer provides a receipt that mentions the payment came from an NRI account. 

## Step 5: Registration via Power of Attorney (Structuring the POA safely)

You do not need to fly to India for the registry. You can execute a "Specific Power of Attorney" (POA) in your country of residence, get it apostilled or attested by the Indian Embassy, and send it to your representative in Mohali.

However, I always advise: **Never give a General Power of Attorney.** Your POA should be limited only to the specific property and the specific task. I discuss the full POA process in our [dedicated guide](/blog/power-of-attorney-property-mohali-nri).
