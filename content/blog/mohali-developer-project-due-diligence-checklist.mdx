When considering **mohali developer project due diligence checklist**, it is essential to understand the underlying market dynamics. The complete developer and project due diligence checklist for Mohali in 2026 involves verifying the RERA registration number against the Punjab RERA portal, confirming clear land title via the Jamabandi records, and auditing the developer's past delivery record. Investors must also scrutinize the Change of Land Use (CLU) status, Forest Department NOCs, and the specific escrow account structure to ensure funds are not diverted. As the Mohali real estate market matures in 2026, the complexity of project evaluation has shifted from simple site visits to deep financial and legal audits. With the expansion of the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority (GMADA) jurisdiction and the surge in "New Mohali" sectors, buyers are often overwhelmed by glossy brochures and aggressive marketing. However, the difference between a high yield investment and a stalled project lies in the rigor of pre booking due diligence.

### 1. The RERA Punjab Deep Dive: Beyond the Registration Number

While every new project must have a RERA number, the mere presence of a registration code is not a guarantee of safety. In 2026, sophisticated investors use the Punjab RERA portal to audit the quarterly progress reports (QPRs).

**Quarterly Compliance Audit**
Developers are legally required to update their project progress every quarter. You must check if the developer has consistently filed these reports. A project that has missed more than two consecutive QPRs is a major red flag indicating internal administrative or financial distress. 

**The 70 Percent Escrow Rule**
Under RERA, 70 percent of the funds collected from buyers must be deposited in a separate escrow account to be used only for construction and land costs. During your due diligence, ask for the specific bank and branch where the RERA escrow account is maintained. Cross reference this with the RERA certificate. If the developer asks for booking amounts in a company name that does not match the RERA registered entity, do not proceed.

### 2. Land Title and Ownership Verification

In Punjab, land records can be complex due to the transition from manual to digital systems. The "Jamabandi" or the Record of Rights is your primary document for title verification.

**The Mutation (Intkaal) Process**
Verify that the land is mutated in the name of the developer or the collaborating entity. If the project is a Joint Development Agreement (JDA), the terms of the agreement must be registered. Ensure the developer has the "Power of Attorney" to sell the specific units they are marketing.

**Change of Land Use (CLU) and License to Develop**
Agricultural land cannot be used for residential or commercial purposes without a CLU certificate from the Department of Town and Country Planning, Punjab. Following the CLU, the developer must obtain a "License to Develop" a colony. These documents must be valid and not expired. ### 3. Statutory Approvals and NOCs: The "Big Five"

Even with a RERA number, a project can face delays if specific NOCs are pending. In Mohali, the following five approvals are critical:

1.  **Building Plan Approval:** Issued by GMADA or the Municipal Corporation. Ensure the maps shown in the sales gallery match the "Sanctioned Plan."
2.  **Forest Department NOC:** This is particularly critical in sectors near the Mullanpur (New Chandigarh) area or the Zirakpur periphery, where "Plowed Land" or "Forest Land" restrictions may apply.
3.  **Airport Authority of India (AAI) NOC:** Since Mohali hosts an international airport, height clearances are mandatory for high rise projects. Verify the permissible height against the proposed number of floors.
4.  **Fire Safety and Environment Clearance:** Mandatory for projects exceeding certain built up area thresholds. 
5.  **PSPCL Load Sanction:** Ensure the Punjab State Power Corporation Limited has sanctioned the required electricity load for the entire township or group housing.

### 4. Developer Track Record and Financial Health

The most common cause of project failure in Mohali is not lack of sales, but financial mismanagement. 

**Delivery History vs. Promised Timelines**
Analyze the developer's last three projects in the Tricity area. Did they deliver on time? More importantly, did they deliver the promised quality of finishing? Check the RERA portal for any active litigation or "Execution Orders" against the developer. A high number of consumer court cases for "Refund of Principal" is a sign of systemic delivery failure.

**Financial Leverage**
Using platforms like the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) website, check the "Charges" registered against the developer's company. If the land is heavily mortgaged to private equity firms or non banking financial companies (NBFCs), the developer may be under pressure to prioritize debt repayment over construction.

### 5. Construction Quality and Technology

In 2026, the methodology of construction is a key differentiator for resale value. 

**Mivan Shuttering vs. Brick and Mortar**
Aluminum formwork (Mivan technology) provides superior structural strength, earthquake resistance, and faster delivery compared to traditional brick and mortar. If you are buying a premium high rise, verify that the developer is using modern shuttering techniques. 

**Soil Testing and Structural Stability**
Request a copy of the soil testing report. In certain parts of Mohali, particularly near the seasonal rivulets (choes), the water table and soil bearing capacity require specific foundation depths. A reputable developer will provide a structural stability certificate from a recognized engineering institution like PEC or IIT.

### 6. The Payment Plan and Hidden Costs Audit

Marketing teams often highlight "Attractive Payment Plans," but these can be traps for the unwary.

**Construction Linked Plan (CLP) vs. Flexi Plans**
In a CLP, you pay as the building rises. This is generally the safest for buyers. Be cautious of "Subvention Schemes" or "No EMI till Possession" plans if the developer is not a Tier 1 brand. In these cases, the interest burden is often hidden in the base price.

**Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**
The Basic Sale Price (BSP) is rarely the final price. Your due diligence checklist must include:
*   External Development Charges (EDC) and Infrastructure Development Charges (IDC).
*   Preferential Location Charges (PLC) for park facing or corner units.
*   Interest Free Maintenance Security (IFMS).
*   Club Membership and Power Back up charges.
*   Possession Linked Charges such as legal fees and stamp duty.

In Mohali, these can add 15 to 25 percent to the quoted BSP.

### 7. Exit Liquidity and Market Viability

Buying a property is easy; selling it is the real test of an investment. 

**Secondary Market Demand**
Before booking, visit the secondary market (resale) for the developer's older projects. Are people buying them? What is the gap between the developer's new booking price and the actual resale price? If the resale price is lower than the new booking price, the project is artificially inflated.

**Infrastructure Connectivity**
Check the GMADA Master Plan 2026. Is the 200 foot wide road promised in the brochure actually under construction? A project that remains "landlocked" due to delayed government infrastructure will never achieve high rental yields or capital appreciation.

### 8. The Final Walkthrough: Pre Booking Checklist Summary

Before you sign the Application Form and pay the booking amount, ensure you have ticked these boxes:

*   RERA Punjab portal checked for QPR status and litigation.
*   Jamabandi and Mutation verified for clear land title.
*   CLU and License to Develop are valid and displayed.
*   Sanctioned Building Plan matches the architectural model.
*   All hidden costs (EDC/IDC/IFMS) are documented in writing.
*   Construction technology (Mivan vs. Brick) is confirmed.
*   Escrow account details match the RERA registration.
