## The Evolution of Property Tax in Mohali (2024 to 2026)

The landscape of municipal governance in Mohali has undergone a substantial shift over the last twenty-four months. The Mohali Municipal Corporation (MC) recently exceeded its collection targets, bringing in over 50 crore rupees in the 2024-25 financial year. For 2025-26, the target has been scaled to 55 crore rupees, reflecting the rapid urbanization of the city. This growth is driven largely by the expansion of the MC limits to include 14 surrounding villages, such as Balongi and Landran, alongside 22 new sectors ranging from Sector 80 to Sector 94, including the high-growth IT City corridor.

A significant point of discussion in early 2026 was the "Photo Rollback" incident. The Municipal Committee had initially mandated the submission of house photographs for property tax payments. However, following strong resident opposition and concerns regarding privacy and potential misuse for building violation notices, the Mayor announced a complete rollback of this requirement in March 2026. This move demonstrates the sensitive nature of property records in Mohali and the importance of maintaining accurate, dispute-free documentation.

## How Property Tax IDs are Created and Managed

In the traditional Mohali development model, the Municipal Committee issued a single Property ID for an entire plot. While this served single-family bungalows well, it has become a significant hurdle in the modern era of independent floors. When a building is constructed with a ground, first, and second floor, a single ID creates a "financial marriage" between the owners. If the first-floor owner defaults on their tax, the entire building is flagged as "Dues Pending" on the government portal.

The creation of these IDs is handled through the mSeva Punjab portal (mseva.lgpunjab.gov.in). During the initial assessment, the MC records the plot area, the total covered area, and the usage type (self-occupied or rented). It is a common mistake for owners to ignore the Property ID until the day they decide to sell. As I often emphasize on our YouTube channel @Amritrealty, a single shared ID is a ticking administrative time bomb that can stall a multi-crore transaction at the final hour.



## The Necessity of Separate IDs for Multi-Floor Buildings

Under the Punjab Unified Building Rules 2025, the state has formally recognized the sale of independent floors in residential sectors. For properties of 250 square yards and above, stilt-plus-four floors are now the norm. For smaller plots, stilt-plus-three is permissible. To make these floors legally tradable, each unit must have its own unique Property ID.

The process of bifurcation involves several steps:
1.  **Application for Separation:** Filing a formal request via the mSeva portal or at the MC Mohali office.
2.  **Verification of Independent Units:** An MC inspector visits the site to confirm that each floor has an independent entrance and a functional kitchen.
3.  **Registered Sale Deed:** Providing a copy of the registered deed for the specific floor to prove ownership.
4.  **Utility Evidence:** While not always mandatory, having a separate PSPCL electricity meter is the strongest supporting evidence for an independent unit.

Once the IDs are separated, the liability is individualized. This is a foundational part of the [Mohali property registration process guide 2026](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026), as it ensures that your No Dues Certificate is not blocked by a neighbor’s negligence.

## What an NDC Means for Your Resale

The No Dues Certificate (NDC) is the ultimate clearance document from the Municipal Committee. It is a digital certificate that confirms all property tax, water charges, and sewerage bills have been paid up to date. In the SAS Nagar Tehsil, the registration software is now integrated with the MC database. If your Property ID shows even a small amount of pending dues, the Sub-Registrar cannot generate the sale deed for your property.

The NDC is not just a receipt of payment; it is a validation of the property's status. It confirms:
-   **No Arrears:** No outstanding principal or interest from previous years.
-   **Correct Classification:** The property is accurately recorded as residential or commercial.
-   **Usage Compliance:** The tax paid matches the actual usage (self-occupied vs. rented).

When I advise clients on [selling property in Mohali](/blog/selling-property-mohali-checklist-process-2026), I recommend checking the NDC status at least 45 days before the intended registry date. This allows time to resolve any "ghost" arrears that often appear in the system due to portal migrations.

## Resolving Historical and Back-Dated Disputes

The most frequent dispute in Mohali involves the "Year of Construction." Many owners find that the MC system has recorded their building as being completed several years before it actually was. This leads to a demand for back-dated taxes and heavy interest penalties.

To resolve a historical dispute, you must move from verbal claims to empirical proof. The Municipal Committee recognizes three primary pieces of evidence:
1.  **PSPCL Load Sanction Date:** The date your first permanent electricity connection was released.
2.  **Occupation Certificate (OC):** The official completion document issued by GMADA or the Municipal Committee.
3.  **Sewerage Connection Receipt:** Proof of when the building was officially connected to the municipal infrastructure.

If you can prove that the building was completed in 2024 rather than 2020, the MC is legally obligated to revise the tax demand. Furthermore, the Punjab government frequently introduces One-Time Settlement (OTS) schemes, such as the OTS 2025 which offered a 100% waiver on interest and penalties for those clearing their principal amount by July 31, 2025. Monitoring these windows is essential for resolving long-standing issues economically.

## Property Tax Rates and Rebate Timelines

Property tax in Mohali is calculated based on the annual rental value or a per-square-foot rate depending on the property type. 

-   **Residential (Self-Occupied):** Lowest rates, with exemptions for certain categories like widows and disabled persons.
-   **Residential (Rented):** Higher rates, typically a percentage of the annual rent.
-   **Commercial (SCOs/Booths):** Calculated based on the zone and category of the commercial corridor (e.g., Airport Road vs. Phase 3B2).

The standard rebate for prompt payment is 10%, usually available if the tax is paid by September 30 of the financial year. Payments made after the March 31 deadline attract a 20% penalty plus 18% annual interest. It is financially prudent to use the mSeva portal to clear dues in the first quarter of the year.

## The Role of the MOU in Shared Buildings

Until you achieve full Property ID bifurcation, a Memorandum of Understanding is your best legal shield. In Mohali, where many people buy floors in "builder houses," the lack of a separate ID is common. An MOU drafted by a professional should cover:
-   **Proportionate Tax Shares:** Based on the registry documents.
-   **Responsibility for Arrears:** Ensuring the seller is liable for any pre-purchase tax.
-   **Consent for Separation:** All owners must agree to provide signatures for the MC bifurcation process.

## Step-by-Step Guide to NDC and ID Separation

1.  **Verification:** Search your property on the mSeva Punjab portal. Check if the "Category" and "Area" match your physical property.
2.  **Arrears Check:** Look for any "Historical Dues" or "Interest" columns.
3.  **Bifurcation Application:** If the ID is shared, submit an application to the MC Mohali office along with your Sale Deed and ID proof.
4.  **Field Inspection:** Ensure the MC inspector can verify the independent kitchen and entrance.
5.  **Payment of Dues:** Clear the individualized arrears.
6.  **NDC Generation:** Download the final digital No Dues Certificate from the portal.
