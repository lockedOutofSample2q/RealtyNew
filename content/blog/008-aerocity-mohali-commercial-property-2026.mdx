# Aerocity Mohali Commercial Property 2026: Rental Yield Data, Occupancy Reality, and Who Should Actually Buy Here

Commercial property in Aerocity Mohali currently delivers rental yields between 4% and 6% for ground floor SCO units, with occupancy levels hovering around 65% across the primary blocks. While the corridor is anchored by its proximity to the Chandigarh International Airport and a growing cluster of luxury hotels, the reality on the ground is one of varying absorption: some blocks are thriving with high-quality F&B and banking tenants, while others remain in a holding pattern. This micro-market is ideal for corporate CXOs and long-term investors who prioritize capital safety and steady, white-collared rental income over aggressive, immediate footfall. If you are looking for a speculative "double your money" play in 12 months, this is not it. If you want a generational asset in an expansion corridor that will benefit from the airport's 24/7 operations and Bharatmala connectivity, Aerocity is the strongest commercial play in Mohali today.

## What are the actual rental yields for Aerocity Mohali commercial units in 2026?

Rental yields in Aerocity have stabilized after a period of rapid price appreciation. For an SCO (Shop-cum-Office) unit, the yield depends heavily on the floor and the frontage. Ground floors command the highest rentals because they cater to retail showrooms, banks, and premium F&B outlets. Upper floors, while seeing slower absorption, are gradually being taken up by corporate offices, clinics, and professional studios.

| Unit Type | Rental Range (per sqft) | Actual Yield (%) | Tenant Profile |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Ground Floor Retail | ₹150 – ₹220 | 4.5% – 6.0% | Banks, Showrooms, F&B |
| First Floor | ₹60 – ₹90 | 3.5% – 4.5% | Offices, Clinics, Gyms |
| Second/Third Floor | ₹45 – ₹65 | 3.0% – 4.0% | IT Offices, Studios |
| Basement | ₹35 – ₹50 | 2.5% – 3.5% | Storage, Back-office |

In my experience navigating project approvals and dealing with developer documentation, I have seen that many investors overlook the loading factor and maintenance costs when calculating these yields. In Aerocity, a ground floor unit that looks expensive on paper often yields better net returns because of the tenant quality and the lower vacancy risk compared to upper floors. ## What is the occupancy reality across different blocks in Aerocity?

The occupancy in Aerocity is not uniform. Blocks closer to the Airport Road and the established hotel cluster see much higher occupancy, often exceeding 80%. As you move deeper into the residential pockets of Aerocity, the commercial units see a "waiting game" occupancy of 40-50%. 

The "Airport Road commercial trajectory" is a real phenomenon I have tracked closely. A few years ago, units here were available at ₹3–4 crore. Today, those same units trade at ₹12–16 crore. This appreciation was driven by the infrastructure actually arriving: the airport expansion, the IT corridor growth, and the arrival of national brands. However, new investors must be careful: the price you pay today already accounts for a lot of this growth. To understand if you are buying at the peak, check our [Mohali real estate investment analysis 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-investment-analysis-2026).

Blocks that are thriving today are those that have secured a "magnet tenant"—usually a major bank or a popular food chain. Once a magnet tenant arrives, the rest of the SCO usually fills up within 6 to 9 months. If you are evaluating a block that has been ready for two years but has no "anchor," you need to ask why.

## What drives footfall in the Aerocity commercial hub?

Footfall in Aerocity is driven by four distinct funnels. Understanding these is critical because they determine the type of tenant who will pay you rent.

1. **Airport Transit:** With the airport moving toward 24/7 operations, there is a constant flow of transit passengers and crew. This supports high-end F&B, convenience retail, and boutique hotels.
2. **The Hotel Cluster:** Aerocity is Mohali’s "Hospitality District." The presence of major hotel brands brings in a floating population with high spending power. 
3. **IT City Proximity:** The nearby IT Park and the Kharar IT City belt bring in thousands of corporate employees daily. This drives the weekday lunch and evening business. You can find more details on this in our [Kharar IT City property investment 2026](/blog/kharar-it-city-property-investment-2026) guide.
4. **Local HNI Resident Base:** The residential sectors surrounding Aerocity are home to some of Mohali’s most affluent families. They prefer Aerocity for their daily premium needs over the congested markets of older sectors.

If your property doesn't cater to at least two of these funnels, your rental growth will be capped. This is the "Vision Investing" principle I always speak about: if you don't see where the crowd is coming from before they arrive, you will buy a dead asset.

## Who should actually invest in Aerocity commercial property?

Aerocity commercial is a "capital preservation" play. It is perfect for a corporate CXO or a senior executive who has a corpus of ₹3 crore to ₹8 crore and wants to park it in a safe, high-visibility asset. It is not for the small-time speculator.

As someone who has worked in equity markets and holds NCFM certifications, I evaluate real estate returns through the lens of opportunity cost. If you put ₹5 crore in a bank FD, you might get 7% interest, but after 30% tax and 6% inflation, your real return is negative. In Aerocity, while the yield might start at 5%, the capital appreciation in an expansion corridor provides the "alpha" that an FD cannot. For a detailed breakdown of this math, read our [rental yield Mohali property 2026](/blog/rental-yield-mohali-property-2026) analysis.

I often advise NRI clients that Aerocity is one of the few places in Mohali where you can "buy and forget" because the GMADA framework ensures that the infrastructure will not decay. The roads are wide, the parking is planned (mostly), and the zoning is strict. This clarity is what a CXO buyer needs when they are managing a high-pressure career and cannot spend every weekend chasing tenants or fixing plumbing.

## Where does Aerocity underdeliver for commercial buyers?

I believe in being blunt about the risks. Aerocity is not perfect. There are three areas where it currently underdelivers:

1. **Parking Congestion:** Despite the wide roads, the internal parking management in certain blocks is already becoming a headache. If you buy a unit with poor frontage or limited parking access, your tenant will struggle to attract walk-in customers.
2. **Slow Upper Floor Absorption:** While ground floors fly off the shelf, the second and third floors in many blocks remain vacant for years. If your investment logic depends on 100% occupancy of the whole SCO to make your EMI, you are taking a massive risk.
3. **Maintenance Quality:** In some areas, the transition from developer maintenance to the local association has been rocky. Poorly maintained common areas lead to lower-quality tenants and, eventually, lower rents.

Before you sign an allotment letter, you must perform a deep-dive [due diligence on the commercial unit](/blog/mohali-sco-investment-due-diligence-commercial). I have seen buyers lured by "guaranteed rental" schemes that the developer stops paying the moment the project is handed over. Don't fall for that.

## How does Aerocity compare to Airport Road and Sector 82A?

Many buyers ask me if they should buy in Aerocity or the Sector 82A belt. The answer depends on your goal. Sector 82A (the JLPL corridor) is more industrial and IT-focused. It has seen the most dramatic appreciation, with prices moving from ₹3–4 crore to ₹12–16 crore. Aerocity is more "lifestyle and hospitality" focused.

| Feature | Aerocity Commercial | Sector 82A / Airport Road |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Primary Demand | Hotels, Retail, F&B | IT, Industrial, Corporate |
| Yield Stability | High | Moderate |
| Capital Entry | ₹4 Cr+ | ₹8 Cr+ |
| Growth Driver | Airport / Tourism | IT / Bharatmala |

Aerocity offers a lower entry point compared to the prime Airport Road frontage units, but it also has more competition. There is a lot of commercial inventory coming up in Aerocity, and only the best-positioned units will see premium rental growth. ## The Honest Verdict: Should you buy here in 2026?

If you are a CXO looking for a stable asset that will yield rent for the next 20 years, yes. Buy a ground floor unit in a block that already has at least two national brands signed up. If you are an investor looking for quick appreciation, you are likely five years too late for the "easy money." The easy money was made when the airport road was just a plan on a GMADA map. 

Today, you have to be surgical. You have to look at the frontage, the parking, and the tenant mix. Having managed project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and the Municipal Committee, I know that what a developer promises in a brochure and what the regulator allows are often two different things. Always verify the RERA filings and the approved map before you transfer the booking amount.
