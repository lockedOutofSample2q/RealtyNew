# Property Possession in Mohali: The Checklist to Carry on Possession Day and What to Refuse to Sign

Possession day is the final transition from being a buyer to being an owner, but in Mohali’s real estate market, it is also your last point of leverage. Before accepting keys, you must verify the Occupation Certificate (OC) or Completion Certificate (CC) to ensure the building is legal for habitation. Your primary task is creating a detailed 'snag list'—documenting every construction defect, from hollow floor tiles to plumbing leaks, with timestamped photos and videos. Crucially, refuse to sign any 'no-objection' or 'full-satisfaction' letters that waive your right to future repairs if the developer has not yet resolved these snags. Under RERA Punjab, you have a 5-year structural defect liability period, but accepting possession without a recorded snag list makes enforcement significantly harder. Collect all original documents, including the possession letter, electricity meter readings, and the maintenance agreement, before making the final payment.

Taking possession is not just about getting the keys and planning the housewarming. It is a legal milestone. Once you sign that possession letter without reservations, the builder’s liability starts to diminish. Having navigated project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and various Municipal Committees, I have seen where the gaps are. This guide is built on those gaps—the things most buyers realize six months too late. If you are new to the market, I recommend starting with our [complete guide to Mohali real estate in 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026) to understand the broader developer landscape before diving into the specifics of handover.

## Why is the Occupation Certificate (OC) your most important document?

In Mohali, whether you are in a GMADA sector or a private licensed colony, the Occupation Certificate (OC) is the only proof that the building has been constructed according to the sanctioned plans and is safe for occupancy. Without an OC, your building is technically unauthorized. You might face issues with permanent electricity connections from PSPCL or, worse, challenges during resale.

Always ask to see the original OC issued by the relevant authority—be it GMADA or the local Municipal Committee. Do not settle for "we have applied for it." Taking possession of a unit in a building without an OC is a risk I rarely advise my clients to take. It leaves you vulnerable to regulatory penalties and infrastructure gaps that the builder may never fix once the majority of buyers have moved in. This is a core part of any [buyer protection strategy](/blog/mohali-property-buyer-protection-guide).

## How to create a professional snag list on possession day?

A "snag" is any defect or unfinished work in your property. Builders are often in a rush to hand over possession to stop the delay penalties they might be paying under RERA. You must be the one to slow things down. 

Carry a notebook, a measuring tape, a mobile charger (to test sockets), and a marble (to test floor slope). Document everything with photos and video. 

![A buyer inspecting floor tiles for snags in a Mohali flat](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/images/blog/snag-list-inspection.jpg)

If possible, bring a professional architect or a construction supervisor with you; their trained eyes will catch things you will miss.

### The Interior Checklist:
*   **Flooring and Skirting:** Tap on every single tile, especially in the center and corners, to check for a hollow sound. This indicates poor cement-sand bonding, and these tiles will likely crack or come loose within a year. Check the grout lines for consistency. Look for "lippage" where one tile is higher than the next, creating a tripping hazard.
*   **Walls and Paint:** Look for dampness (seepage) near bathrooms, kitchens, and external walls. Use your hand to feel for cold spots or bubbling paint. Check the finish of the POP (Plaster of Paris) under a flashlight held parallel to the wall; this reveals uneven surfaces that are invisible under direct light.
*   **Electrical Points and Fixtures:** Plug your charger into every socket and check if the indicator light comes on. Test the two-way switches for bedrooms. Open the distribution board (DB) and ensure every circuit is clearly labeled. Check if the earthing is functional—a faulty earth is a safety hazard that most buyers ignore until they get a shock from a microwave.
*   **Plumbing and Sanitary Ware:** Turn on every tap simultaneously to check for water pressure drops. Flush every toilet and watch the refill speed. Check for "dead legs" in the plumbing where water might stagnate. Inspect the area under the kitchen sink and bathroom vanities with a dry tissue; any moisture on the tissue after five minutes of running water indicates a slow leak.
*   **Doors, Windows, and Hardware:** Open and close every door and window multiple times. They should move smoothly without any screeching or resistance. Check the weather-stripping on UPVC windows; if it's missing or damaged, your air conditioning bills will be 20% higher due to leakage. Ensure all locks and latches are of the specified brand and are properly aligned.

### The External and Common Area Checklist:
Do not ignore the areas outside your front door. Your property value is tied to the common infrastructure. Check the lift's functioning—is there a valid safety certificate displayed? Inspect the state of the lobby and the fire-fighting equipment on your floor. Ensure the parking spot assigned to you matches the dimensions in your allotment letter and is not obstructed by structural columns or service pipes. If you are in a multi-floor building, refer to our [process and paperwork guide](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026) to understand how shared spaces and maintenance responsibilities are divided among owners.

## What documents must you collect before leaving the site?

You should leave the site with a physical or digital folder containing the following:
1.  **Possession Letter:** The formal document stating that the developer has handed over the property. Ensure it mentions the correct unit number, floor, and area.
2.  **Occupation Certificate (OC):** A copy of the OC issued by GMADA or the Municipal Committee. This is your insurance against demolition or utility disconnection.
3.  **No-Dues Certificate (NOC):** A document from the builder’s accounts department confirming that you have no outstanding dues. This is vital for future resale.
4.  **Maintenance Agreement:** A signed copy of the agreement with the Maintenance Agency. Check the "Sinking Fund" clause—this money should be transferred to the Residents' Welfare Association (RWA) eventually.
5.  **Keys:** All sets, including master keys and spares for all internal doors.
6.  **Warranty Documents and Manuals:** For every fixture provided—geysers, ACs, kitchen chimneys, and even the electrical wires and pipes if the builder provides them.
7.  **Meter Readings:** Take timestamped photos of the electricity and water meters. In many Mohali projects, builders use "construction power" for months after handing over. You should only pay from the reading recorded on your possession day.

## What should you refuse to sign on possession day?

This is where the real negotiation happens. Builders often present a stack of papers and ask you to sign them in a hurry. One of these will likely be a "Satisfaction Letter" or a "No-Objection Certificate" in favor of the developer.

**Never sign a document that says you are "fully satisfied with the quality of construction" if you have found even one snag.**

Builders use these letters in RERA courts to prove that the buyer accepted the property without any complaints, effectively neutralizing your 5-year defect liability. If they refuse to hand over the keys without this signature, do the following:
1.  **Cross out the "Satisfied" clauses.**
2.  **Write "Possession taken under protest subject to the attached snag list" in bold.**
3.  **Initial every page of the snag list and have the builder’s representative sign it too.**

This creates a legal record that the possession was conditional. If the builder refuses to sign your snag list, send it to them immediately via registered post and an email to their official RERA-registered address. This "paper trail" is what wins cases in [real estate disputes in Punjab](/blog/real-estate-disputes-legal-rights-punjab-2026).

## The Regulatory Framework: GMADA and RERA Punjab

Mohali’s real estate is governed by two major entities: GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority) and RERA Punjab. 

GMADA is responsible for the master plan and the initial licensing. They ensure the developer has the right to build and that the project complies with zoning laws. RERA, on the other hand, is the buyer’s watchdog. Before taking possession, verify that the developer has filed all quarterly progress reports on the [RERA Punjab portal](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property). If there are discrepancies between what was promised in the RERA filings and what is delivered at the site, you have the right to demand rectification before taking possession.

One common issue in Mohali is the "Partial OC." Builders sometimes get an OC for only a few towers in a large project. While this is legal, it often means you will be living in a construction zone for the next three years. Ensure you know whether your tower has a full OC and if the promised common amenities (clubhouse, park, security systems) are functional as per the RERA commitment.

## The Importance of Separate Utility IDs

In my experience resolving property disputes, many headaches stem from shared utility IDs. In the "Sandeep Case," we dealt with a multi-floor building where the property tax and electricity were not properly separated at the time of possession. It took three months of coordination with the Municipal Committee and PSPCL to generate separate IDs and meters. 

If you are buying a floor in a 3-storey building, ensure that your electricity meter is in the process of being transferred to your name and that you have a separate property tax ID. Without this, your resale value will be severely impacted, and you will find it nearly impossible to get an [NDC from the Municipal Committee](/blog/municipal-committee-property-tax-mohali-ndc) later. Always insist on seeing the [PSPCL load sanction letter](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-pspcl) for your specific unit.

### FAQ: Common Questions on Possession Day

### Can I take possession if the lift isn't working?
Technically, yes, but you should document it as a major snag. If you are on a higher floor, a non-functional lift makes the property uninhabitable. Ensure the builder gives a written timeline for the lift's certification and operation before you sign the handover documents.

### What if the electricity meter is still in the builder's name?
This is common during the initial handover phase. However, you must ensure that you are not being billed at construction-phase rates (which are much higher). Demand a separate [PSPCL connection for your floor](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-pspcl) as soon as possible.

### Should I pay the maintenance advance before getting keys?
Most developers in Mohali ask for 12–24 months of maintenance advance at the time of possession. While this is standard practice, ensure that the maintenance agreement clearly defines what services are included. If the amenities (gym, pool, etc.) are not yet ready, you should negotiate a reduction in the maintenance rate until they are functional.
