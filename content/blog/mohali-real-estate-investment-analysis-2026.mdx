### 3. The Regulatory Framework: Navigating the 5 Bodies (The RHMC Guide)

One of the biggest mistakes investors make in Mohali is assuming that "approved" means "ready." In Punjab, you must navigate five distinct regulatory bodies. My experience in government liaisoning for my own development projects has taught me that a delay in any one of these can stall a project for years.

#### 3.1 GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority)
GMADA is the primary authority for plot allotments and colony licensing in its jurisdiction. 
- **Critical Knowledge:** Property transfers in GMADA require a certified copy with a Tehsildar stamp. I often tell my clients: do not trust WhatsApp photos of documents. 
- **Process Note:** The OTP for transfer now goes to the last registered buyer. If your contact information is not updated in the GMADA records, you are effectively locked out of your own asset. A standard transfer takes 4-8 weeks, assuming documentation is 100% compliant.
- **Regulatory Record:** GMADA reserve prices for 3BHK units in 2026 start at ₹1.15 Cr, which sets the baseline for the entire secondary market.

#### 3.2 PUDA (Punjab Urban Development Authority)
PUDA controls licensing outside GMADA limits. 
- **The CLU Trap:** Many developers market projects as "Applied for PUDA approval." This is not an approval. The Change of Land Use (CLU) and the Layout Approval are two separate technical milestones. A project is only legitimate when both are signed and the RERA number is issued.
- **PAPRA 1995 Compliance:** The Punjab Apartment and Property Regulation Act (PAPRA) is the foundational law. Any developer collecting money without a PAPRA license is committing a criminal offense.

#### 3.3 PSPCL (Punjab State Power Corporation Limited)
Electrical load is the most overlooked aspect of commercial and multi-floor residential investing. 
- **The Separate Meter Rule:** For any multi-floor building, you must ensure separate meters for each floor. This requires a separate application, load sanction, and connection process. Without a separate meter, obtaining a No Dues Certificate (NDC) at the time of resale becomes a legal nightmare, as tax and power liabilities are shared across floors.
- **Load Sanctions:** Ensure the developer has paid the security for the sanctioned load. If they are running on temporary connections, your future maintenance costs will skyrocket due to DG set reliance.

#### 3.4 Municipal Committee (MC)
The MC handles building plan sanctions and property tax IDs. 
- **The Property Tax MOU:** In multi-floor buildings (like S+4 floors), property tax is often calculated at the building level. If you own the ground floor and the second-floor owner defaults, your NDC is blocked. At RHMC, we assist clients in drafting an MOU between floor owners and generating separate property IDs to isolate liability. 
- **The Sandeep Case Study:** We resolved a property tax deadlock for a client named Sandeep by coordinating with the MC to split the property ID, a process that took 3 months of persistent follow-up but secured the asset's resale value.

#### 3.5 Forest and Conservation Authorities
If a project is near a designated green belt or forest area, you need specific clearances. I have personally seen cases where Forest Department approval cancellations led to project shutdowns. Always verify the forest clearance status via an RTI if the project is located on the periphery of Mohali or near the Shivalik foothills.

### 4. Technical Deep Dive: The Mutation (Intkal) Process

Mutation is the most misunderstood process in Punjab real estate. Registration of a sale deed is only 50% of the work. Mutation (Intkal) is the actual update of the revenue records (Jamabandi).

**The Step-by-Step Process:**
1. **Registration:** Sale deed executed at the Sub-Registrar's office. (Stamp Duty: 6% for men, 4% for women; Registration: 1%).
2. **Patwari Entry:** The deed is sent to the local Patwari.
3. **Verification:** The Patwari enters the transaction in the 'Register Intkalat'.
4. **Public Notice:** A 15-day notice period is observed for objections.
5. **Approval:** The Kanungo and Tehsildar/Naib-Tehsildar verify and sanction the mutation.
6. **Timeline:** Should take 30-45 days. In reality, it can take 6 months without follow-up.

**Why it matters:** Without mutation, you cannot get a home loan, you cannot sell the property, and you do not have a legally recognized title in the eyes of the Revenue Department.

#### 5.1 Industrial Investing: Red vs. Green vs. Orange Zones
Industrial plots are categorized by their environmental impact.
- **Red Zone:** High-pollution industries (chemicals, pharma, heavy manufacturing). These require specific distance from residential zones and proximity to high-capacity drainage. Investment in Red Zones is high-yield because these industries have very few locations to choose from.
- **Green Zone:** Low-impact (IT, textiles, food processing). These are easier to find but have lower appreciation velocity.
- **Orange Zone:** Medium-impact. A balanced bet for investors looking for stability.

#### 5.2 Commercial Stagnation vs. Expansion
A key principle I discuss on my YouTube channel is the difference between stagnant and expanding corridors. Old markets like Phase 7 or 3B2 in Mohali have reached their peak. Rental values are down, and prices are fixed. 
Conversely, new corridors like Airport Road are expanding. "Je aapaan vision ton bina challaange taan aapaan mehngi cheez khareeddaange." In expansion zones, you are buying the future; in stagnant zones, you are buying the past.

### 6. The 9-Point Pre-Launch Technical Checklist

Buying in "pre-launch" is high-risk, high-reward. If you are an HNI or CXO looking for the maximum ROI, you must use this checklist before writing that first cheque (usually 25% of total value).

1. **Land Ownership:** Does the developer actually own the land? Verify the Jamabandi. Many pre-launches are sold on "collaboration" agreements.
2. **CLU Approval:** Has the Change of Land Use been granted by PUDA?
3. **RERA Number:** Is the project registered with RERA Punjab? Never buy without a RERA number.
4. **License to Develop (LDC):** Has the developer obtained the license to develop a colony?
5. **Approved Layout Map:** Does the map you are being shown match the one approved by the authorities?
6. **Connectivity Plan:** Is the project's approach road already built?
7. **Sewage and Power Load:** Has the developer obtained load sanctions from PSPCL and sewerage disposal approvals?
8. **Environmental Clearance:** If the project is over 20,000 sq. meters, it needs EC.
9. **Builder-Buyer Agreement (BBA):** Does it have a fixed possession date and a penalty clause of 10-12% for delays?

### 8. Common Legal Disputes: Titles, Checks, and Forest Approvals

Property disputes in Punjab are long and expensive. Prevention is the only advisory I offer.

#### 8.1 Title Disputes
Often, a seller might have a "stay order" from a civil court due to a family dispute. Registration will still happen, but you will never get mutation. 
**Prevention:** Always check the "Remarks" (Kaifiyat) column in the Jamabandi.

#### 8.2 Section 138: Cheque Bouncing
In property transactions, post-dated cheques are common. 
**Technical Note:** If a cheque bounces, you have exactly 30 days to send a legal notice. If you miss this window, the criminal liability under Section 138 is significantly harder to prove.

#### 8.3 Forest Department Approvals
Many "luxury" projects on the Chandigarh-Mohali periphery are built on the edge of protected zones. If the Forest Department cancels an approval, the project is effectively dead. 

### 9. The NRI Perspective: FEMA, POA, and TDS

Managing property from abroad requires a trust-based framework. At RHMC, we handle the full lifecycle for NRI investors.

#### 9.1 NRI Tax: LTDC and Form 15CA/CB
- **TDS on Sale:** When selling, the TDS rate for NRIs is 22.66% (20% + surcharge + cess). 
- **Lower Tax Deduction Certificate (LTDC):** You can apply to the Income Tax Department for an LTDC to reduce this to as low as 1-3%.
- **Repatriation:** Funds can be moved back to your foreign account via an NRO account using Form 15CA and 15CB.

#### 9.2 The Power of Attorney (POA)
A POA must be adjudicated in India to be valid for property transactions. Blood relatives (parents, siblings, spouse) pay nominal fees, while non-blood relatives pay 2% stamp duty on the circle rate.

### 15. The 10 Unbreakable Rules of Mohali Investing

1. **Vision Before Bitumen:** If you buy after the road is paved, you are the exit liquidity for the person who bought during the planning stage.
2. **Never Buy Unauthorised:** A "Lal Dora" plot might be cheap, but it will never have a GMADA sewerage connection or a clean bank loan.
3. **Verify at the Authority:** Do not trust the broker's folder. Visit the Estate Officer at GMADA or the MC yourself.
4. **Check the Patwari Record:** Mutation (Intkal) is the real proof of ownership, not just the sale deed.
5. **Loading is an Invisible Tax:** A 40% loading factor means you are paying for the lift and lobby at the price of your bedroom.
6. **Separate Metering is Mandatory:** Multi-floor assets must have independent electrical connections to be salable.
7. **RTI is your Best Friend:** When in doubt, file an RTI. It costs ₹10 and saves ₹10 Lakh.
8. **Say No to Assured Returns:** Any developer promising 12% "guaranteed" return is likely paying you from your own capital.
9. **Infrastructure Corridors only:** Buy in expansion zones (Airport Road, Bharatmala), avoid stagnation zones (Phase 7, 3B2).
10. **The Post-Sale Relationship:** The advisor who disappears after the registry was never an advisor; he was a salesman.
