The investment outlook for Mohali real estate in 2026 is defined by a transition from speculative growth to institutional stability. Investors entering the market today can expect average annual appreciation rates of 18 percent to 24 percent in high demand corridors such as IT City and Aero City. Commercial rental yields have stabilized between 7 percent and 9 percent for premium SCO units, while residential yields in the gated apartment segment hover around 3 percent to 4.5 percent. This growth is underpinned by the operationalization of the Bharatmala project and the massive expansion of the Shaheed Bhagat Singh International Airport. For institutional investors and HNIs, the 2026 window represents a final opportunity to capture value before the market reaches a price ceiling similar to established sectors in Chandigarh. Buying now is supported by the rapid absorption of Grade A office space and the emergence of the Banga Rajpura corridor as a primary industrial and logistics hub.

## The Macro Economic Landscape of Mohali in 2026

By early 2026, the economic profile of Mohali has fundamentally shifted. While the previous decade was marked by residential plotting, the current cycle is driven by corporate migration and infrastructure milestones. Data from the Economic Times and recent Punjab Government industry reports indicate that Mohali now accounts for over 45 percent of new IT and service sector registrations in the state. This corporate influx has created a sustained demand for both luxury residential units and high street commercial spaces.

The completion of major segments of the Bharatmala road network has effectively integrated Mohali into a larger regional logistics grid. This connectivity reduces transit times to major industrial zones like Rajpura and Banur, making Mohali the preferred residential base for senior management and business owners. The infrastructure is no longer a promise: it is a visible, operational reality that is directly reflected in property valuations.

## Sector Level Appreciation Data: Where the Value Lies

Analyzing appreciation requires a granular look at specific micro markets. In 2026, the performance of various sectors shows a clear divergence based on proximity to the Airport Road and IT hubs.

### The IT City and Aero City Corridor
These sectors continue to be the primary engines of appreciation. Looking back at historical data, commercial units in the JLPL area of Sector 82 that were priced at Rs 3 crore to 4 crore just a few years ago have now crossed the Rs 12 crore to 16 crore mark. In 2026, we are seeing a secondary wave of appreciation in the extension sectors. Licensed plots in these zones have seen a year on year growth of 22 percent, driven by the scarcity of GMADA approved inventory.

### New Chandigarh (Mullanpur)
New Chandigarh has emerged as the luxury residential alternative to Chandigarh. With the Master Plan 2031 execution in full swing, the area has moved past the initial stagnation phase. Large scale integrated townships from branded developers have seen price corrections on the upside, with a focus on low density living that appeals to the HNI and NRI segments.

### The PR 7 Expansion Zone
The areas surrounding the PR 7 Airport Road have transitioned from emerging to established. The demand for SCO (Shop cum Office) plots here remains at an all time high. Institutional buyers are increasingly targeting these units for their long term capital gains potential and immediate rental prospects.

## Due Diligence in the 2026 Market

Despite the positive momentum, the Mohali market requires rigorous due diligence. We have seen cases where buyers faced 8 month delays in plot recovery because the seller had not disclosed builder cancellations. In another instance, shared property tax liabilities across multiple floors caused significant legal hurdles for a ground floor purchaser.

Before committing capital in 2026, ensure you have verified the following:
* **The Five Body Check:** Confirm clearances from GMADA, PUDA, PSPCL, the Municipal Committee, and Forest Authorities where applicable.
* **Property Tax IDs:** For multi floor buildings, ensure separate property IDs are generated to avoid collective liability.
* **Loading Factor Transparency:** Do not accept vague verbal promises: verify the carpet area against the super area in the RERA filings.
* **Circle Rates vs Market Rates:** With the 6 percent stamp duty in Punjab, the gap between circle rates and actual agreement values must be managed legally and transparently.
