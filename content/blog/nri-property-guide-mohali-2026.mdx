# Complete NRI Property Guide — Mohali 2026: Buying From the UK, Canada, Gulf, or Australia

NRIs can legally buy residential and commercial property in Mohali without RBI permission, provided the payment is routed through NRO/NRE accounts and the property is not agricultural land, a farmhouse, or plantation property. Under FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) rules, Non-Resident Indians (NRIs) and Overseas Citizens of India (OCIs) are treated on par with resident Indians for most real estate transactions. To ensure a secure investment in 2026, you must prioritize RERA-registered projects, use a Specific Power of Attorney (POA) for registration, and maintain a clear audit trail of funds moving from overseas into Indian NRO/NRE accounts.

In my years of navigating project approvals and handling transactions for the Punjabi diaspora, I’ve seen that the biggest hurdle isn't the law—it's the distance. Buying from London, Toronto, or Dubai requires a level of due diligence that goes beyond a glossy brochure. This guide breaks down the end-to-end process I use to protect my NRI clients.

## Can NRIs Buy Any Property in Mohali?

The short answer is: Almost. Under [FEMA NRI property purchase India rules](/blog/fema-nri-property-purchase-india-rules), you are permitted to acquire any number of residential or commercial properties. 

However, you are strictly prohibited from purchasing:
*   Agricultural land
*   Farmhouses
*   Plantation property

If you are looking at the [industrial land investment near Mohali](/blog/industrial-land-investment-mohali-rajpura), you are generally permitted as these fall under commercial/industrial usage, but always verify the zoning. I have handled cases where land was marketed as "industrial" but was still classified as agricultural in the revenue records—a trap that can lead to FEMA violations.

## The Banking Backbone: NRE vs. NRO Accounts

You cannot use a regular resident savings account once your status changes to NRI. For property transactions, you need:

1.  **NRE (Non-Resident External) Account:** Use this for funds earned outside India. Money here is fully repatriable (you can take it back out of India easily).
2.  **NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary) Account:** Use this for income earned in India (rent, dividends). This is the account typically used for the [property registration process in Mohali](/blog/property-registration-mohali-2026-stamp-duty).

**The Golden Rule:** Never pay a builder or seller directly from a foreign bank account. The funds must first land in your NRO/NRE account in India, from which the payment is made. This creates the "Banking Channel" record required for future repatriation of sale proceeds.

## Remote Due Diligence: Buying Without Visiting

Most of my NRI clients buy without stepping foot in Mohali during the search phase. This requires a [remote due diligence protocol](/blog/nri-buy-mohali-property-remotely).

*   **Video Site Walkthroughs:** Don't just look at the flat. Ask your advisor to show you the approach road, the neighboring plots, and the actual construction pace.
*   **RERA Verification:** Every project must be verified on the [RERA Punjab portal](/blog/mohali-real-estate-faq-2026). Check for any complaints or delays in previous phases.
*   **Document Verification:** I insist on seeing the original allotment letter, the [GMADA plot transfer](/blog/gmada-plot-transfer-ownership-process) records, and the approved building plan via a video call before any token money is exchanged.

## Power of Attorney (POA): The Safe Way

To sign the registry (sale deed) while abroad, you will need a [Power of Attorney for property in Mohali](/blog/power-of-attorney-property-mohali-nri). 

I always advise a **Specific Power of Attorney (SPOA)** rather than a General one. An SPOA limits the holder’s power to a single transaction. It must be executed on a stamp paper, attested by the Indian Consulate/Embassy in your country, and then adjudicated (stamped) in Mohali within three months of arrival.

## Tax Implications: Buying and Selling

### At the Time of Purchase
*   **TDS (Tax Deducted at Source):** If you buy from a resident Indian, you must deduct 1% TDS if the value exceeds ₹50 Lakhs. If you buy from another NRI, the TDS rate is much higher (often 20% + surcharge).
*   **GST:** Only applies to under-construction properties (typically 5%). Ready-to-move properties with an Occupancy Certificate (OC) have 0% GST.

### At the Time of Sale
When you sell, you will face [NRI tax on Mohali property sale](/blog/nri-tax-property-sale-mohali-tds-capital-gains). The buyer will deduct TDS at 20% (plus surcharge and cess) for long-term capital gains. To reduce this, you can apply for a "Lower Deduction Certificate" from the Income Tax department.

## FAQ: Common NRI Questions

### How can I verify if a project is NRI-friendly?
Check if the developer has a track record of delivering to NRIs and if the project is approved by major banks like HDFC, ICICI, or SBI. You can also use my [10 questions for property advisors](/blog/mohali-property-advisor-nri-trust-questions) to vet the person representing you.

### Can I get a home loan as an NRI?
Yes, most Indian banks offer home loans to NRIs for tenures up to 15-20 years. The interest rates are similar to resident rates, but the documentation (salary slips, work permit, NRE/NRO statements) is more rigorous.

### What is the repatriation limit?
You can repatriate up to USD 1 million per financial year from your NRO account, subject to payment of applicable taxes and filing of Forms 15CA and 15CB.

### Is it better to buy a plot or a flat?
For pure appreciation, [GMADA plots](/blog/gmada-property-nri-allotment-process) are excellent. For rental yield and ease of management, luxury flats in gated societies are often better for NRIs.
