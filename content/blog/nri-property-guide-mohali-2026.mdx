## Section 1: The Regulatory Landscape in Punjab (Deep Dive)

Understanding who controls what is the first step in protecting your capital. In Mohali, you are dealing with five distinct regulatory bodies, each with its own set of rules and "file movement" logic.

### 1.1 GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority)
GMADA is the primary authority for planned sectors like Aerocity, IT City, and the 76-80 sector series. They handle plot allotments, colony licensing, and the ultimate record of property transfers (mutations).
- **The Transfer Process:** A "GMADA Transfer" is not just a piece of paper. It requires a certified copy with a Tehsildar stamp. We often see NRIs accepting WhatsApp photos of "Allotment Letters" as proof of ownership. This is a catastrophic risk. A genuine transfer requires the presence of both parties or their POA holders at the GMADA office in Phase 8. The process now includes biometrics and OTP-based verification for the last registered buyer.
- **Regulatory Baseline:** GMADA reserve prices for 3BHK references are currently around ₹1.15 Cr, setting the market floor.

### 1.2 PUDA (Punjab Urban Development Authority)
PUDA oversees licensing for areas outside the immediate GMADA jurisdiction. 
- **The Approval Trap:** Developers often market projects as "PUDA Approved." In reality, this often only refers to the Change of Land Use (CLU). A project is only fully legitimate when it has the Layout Approval and the License to Develop a Colony (LDC). At RHMC, we use RTI filings to verify these milestones directly.
- **PAPRA 1995:** The Punjab Apartment and Property Regulation Act is the core law. Any collection of funds before RERA registration and PAPRA licensing is a criminal offense in Punjab.

### 1.3 PSPCL (Punjab State Power Corporation Limited)
Electricity is a major technical hurdle for multi-floor assets.
- **Separate Metering:** In multi-floor buildings (common in sectors like 82A), developers often provide a single "bulk connection." We insist on separate load sanctions and individual meters for every floor. Without this, obtaining a No Dues Certificate (NDC) for resale becomes impossible as tax and power liabilities are shared.

### 1.4 Municipal Committee (MC)
The MC handles building sanctions, completion certificates (OC/CC), and property tax mutation.
- **The Property Tax Dispute:** Property tax IDs in Mohali are often building-level, not unit-level. We assist clients in drafting formal MOUs between floor owners to allocate liability and generate separate property IDs through the MC portal. This process typically takes 3 months of persistent coordination.

### 1.5 Forest and Conservation Authorities
Projects in New Chandigarh or peripheral zones face Forest Department restrictions.
- **The PLPA Risk:** If land is under the Punjab Land Preservation Act (PLPA), construction is restricted. We verify forest clearance status via official records to ensure the approach road is not on protected land. I have personally seen cases where Forest Department approval cancellations led to project shutdowns.

## Section 2: FEMA and RBI Guidelines: The Legal Pillar for NRI Buyers

The Foreign Exchange Management Act (FEMA), 1999, is the federal law governing your purchase.

### 2.1 Permitted vs. Prohibited Assets
NRIs and OCIs can purchase residential and commercial properties. 
- **The Agricultural Prohibition:** Agricultural land, farmhouses, and plantation properties are strictly prohibited for purchase by NRIs. 
- **The "Gair Mumkin" Trap:** Developers often sell "farmhouse plots" in unauthorized colonies. If the revenue record lists the land as "Gair Mumkin" (non-cultivable) or "Chahi" (irrigated agricultural), your purchase violates FEMA. Only inheritance of such land is permitted for NRIs.

### 2.2 Funding the Transaction: The Paper Trail
Funds must originate from NRE, NRO, or FCNR accounts. 
- **The Foreign Currency Prohibition:** You cannot pay the seller in foreign currency or via traveler's cheques. The transaction must happen in Indian Rupees from an Indian NRI bank account. 
- **FIRC Importance:** Always retain the Foreign Inward Remittance Certificate (FIRC) for every remittance. This is the primary proof required for repatriation of funds later.

## Section 3: Banking, Accounts, and the NRI Tax Matrix

Understanding the difference between NRE and NRO accounts is a tax strategy.

### 3.1 NRE (Non-Resident External)
- **Source:** Foreign earnings only.
- **Taxation:** Interest is tax-free in India.
- **Repatriability:** Fully repatriable (principal + interest).
- **Strategy:** Ideal for the initial purchase if you intend to move capital back abroad later.

### 3.2 NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary)
- **Source:** Income earned in India (rent, etc.) or foreign transfers.
- **Taxation:** Interest is taxable at 30% plus surcharge.
- **Repatriability:** Limited to USD 1 million per financial year.
- **Strategy:** Best for managing local expenses, property taxes, and receiving rental income.

| Feature | NRE Account | NRO Account |
|---|---|---|
| **Tax on Interest** | Tax-free | Taxable at 30% |
| **Repatriability** | Full | Up to $1M per year |
| **Source of Funds** | Foreign Income | Local + Foreign Income |
| **Best Use** | Property Purchase | Local Maintenance + Rent |

### 3.3 The PAN Card Requirement
A PAN card is mandatory for all property transactions in India. Without it, TDS on sale is significantly higher, and registration of the sale deed is blocked. We recommend applying at least 60 days in advance.

### 3.4 NRI Taxation on Sale (Section 195)
- **Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG):** Assets held for >24 months attract 20% tax plus surcharge and cess (approx 22.66%). 
- **Short-Term Capital Gains (STCG):** Assets held for less than 24 months are taxed at slab rates (up to 30%).
- **TDS on Sale:** Buyers must deduct TDS on the total sale value for NRI sellers. 
- **LTDC (Lower Tax Deduction Certificate):** We coordinate with Chartered Accountants to apply for an LTDC, reducing the withholding tax to as low as 1-3% of the gain, preventing capital from being locked with the IT department for 18 months.

## Section 4: The Power of Attorney (POA) Framework in Punjab

Since you are abroad, the POA is your legal proxy.

### 4.1 Drafting and Attestation
The POA must be drafted abroad and attested by the Indian Consulate or Apostilled. 

### 4.2 Adjudication in Punjab (The 2% Rule)
Once the POA arrives in India, it must be adjudicated (stamped) at the District Administrative Complex in Mohali.
- **Non-Blood Relatives:** If the POA is given to a non-blood relative, a **2% stamp duty** on the circle rate is mandatory. This was implemented to curb unauthorized trading.
- **Blood Relatives:** Nominal fees (approx ₹1,00,0-2,000) apply for parents, siblings, or spouses.
- **Specific vs. General:** At RHMC, we recommend a "Specific POA" that limits the power to signing the sale deed for a specific Khasra or Plot number, protecting you from unauthorized asset sales.

## Section 9: Legacy Planning: Wills and Inheritance for NRIs

Managing a legacy in India requires proactive legal measures.

### 9.1 The Need for an Indian Will
While a foreign Will may be valid, a separate Indian Will for your Mohali assets significantly speeds up the probate and mutation process after your time. It prevents family disputes and ensures a clean transfer to your heirs.

### 9.2 Inheritance Tax
As of 2026, India does not have an inheritance tax. However, the eventual sale of inherited property by an NRI is subject to capital gains tax based on the original purchase price (indexation applies from the date of acquisition by the previous owner).

## Section 13: Detailed FAQ for NRI Buyers in 2026

**1. Can I buy agricultural land in Punjab?**
No. FEMA prohibits NRIs from purchasing agricultural land. You can only inherit it.

**2. Is a separate PAN card needed for my spouse?**
Yes, if the property is in joint names, both owners must have a PAN card for registration and TDS purposes.

**3. How do I get my POA adjudicated?**
It must be submitted to the Financial Commissioner (Revenue) or the Divisional Commissioner in Punjab after arrival in India.

**4. What if my property is in a non-RERA project?**
It is a massive risk. You have no protection under the RERA Authority for possession delays or construction defects.

**5. Can I transfer funds directly from my UK account to a builder?**
No. It must route through your NRE/NRO account in India to maintain the paper trail for future repatriation.

## Section 17: Glossary of Technical Terms for NRIs

- **Jamabandi:** The bi-annual record of rights (ownership).
- **Intkal (Mutation):** The update of ownership in the revenue department after a sale.
- **Circle Rate:** The minimum government-set value for property registration.
- **NDC (No Dues Certificate):** Proof that all taxes and utilities are cleared.
- **OC (Occupation Certificate):** Government permission to occupy the building.
- **FAR (Floor Area Ratio):** The permissible construction area on a land parcel.
- **PAPRA:** Punjab Apartment and Property Regulation Act, 1995.
