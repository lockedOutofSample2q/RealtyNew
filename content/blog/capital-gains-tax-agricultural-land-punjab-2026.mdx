## The Direct Answer
In 2026, the taxability of agricultural land in Punjab depends primarily on its classification as **Urban or Rural.** If your land is situated within a Municipal Committee limit or is classified as Urban, it is subject to Capital Gains Tax unless you qualify for an exemption under **Section 10(37)** (Compulsory Acquisition) or **Section 54B** (Reinvestment in other agricultural land). For most sellers in the Mohali and Rajpura corridors, the key to protecting your payout is identifying the correct "Circle Rate" vs. "Agreement Value" to calculate your indexed cost of acquisition accurately.

## Section 10(37): The Compulsory Acquisition Shield
Many families in Punjab are currently receiving payouts due to highway expansions like the Bharatmala or GMADA acquisitions for new sectors. 
- **The Rule:** If your agricultural land is compulsorily acquired by the government, the compensation received is **exempt from tax** under Section 10(37) of the Income Tax Act.
- **The Catch:** This exemption only applies to "Individual" or "HUF" owners. If the land is held by a company or partnership, the tax dynamics shift significantly.

## Section 54B and 54EC: Strategies for Voluntary Sales
If you are selling your land voluntarily—for example, to a private developer or another investor—you will face Long-Term Capital Gains (LTCG) tax (typically 20% with indexation). To mitigate this, you have three primary routes:
1. **Section 54B:** Reinvest the gains in another piece of agricultural land within **2 years.**
2. **Section 54EC:** Invest the gains (up to ₹50 Lakh) in specified infrastructure bonds like NHAI or REC within **6 months.**
3. **Capital Gains Account Scheme:** If you haven't identified a new property by the tax filing deadline, the funds must be parked in a specialized bank account to maintain your exemption eligibility.

## The Role of Circle Rates in 2026
Punjab's revenue department has been aggressively updating circle rates to match market values. Selling below the circle rate triggers **Section 43CA**, where the tax is calculated on the circle rate, not your actual receipt. 

Before committing to a sale, we advise our clients to obtain a "Clear Title Audit" and a current "Valuation Report" from a registered valuer. This prevents the "Tax Gap" trap where you owe more to the IT department than you actually banked.

## Critical Checklist for Land Sellers
- **Nakal & Jamabandi:** Ensure the land is classified as 'Chahi' or 'Barani' agricultural land in the revenue records.
- **Holding Period:** Hold for at least 24 months to qualify for Long-Term Capital Gains.
- **Improvement Costs:** Keep receipts for land leveling, tubewell installation, or fencing. These can be added to your cost base to reduce tax.
