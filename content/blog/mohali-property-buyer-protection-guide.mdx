# Mohali Property Buyer's Complete Protection Guide 2026: Everything to Verify Before You Sign Anything

To protect yourself as a property buyer in Mohali in 2026, you must conduct a multi-layered due diligence process that moves beyond glossy brochures to verify the legal, financial, and physical reality of a project. At a minimum, this includes verifying the project’s RERA registration on the official Punjab RERA portal, conducting a title search through the Revenue Department (Jamabandi/Fard), and analysing the builder's payment plan for "time-linked" versus "construction-linked" risks. You must also calculate the all-in cost, including hidden EDC, IDC, and PLC charges, and verify the developer’s actual delivery track record by inspecting previous project completion certificates (OC/CC). My decade of experience on both the developer and advisory sides has shown that the most significant risks are not found in what is promised, but in what is omitted from the legal fine print. By following this sequenced 7-step protection framework, you ensure that your capital is secured against the most common traps of the Punjab real estate market.

## Why is a Sequenced Protection Strategy Mandatory in 2026?

If you were sitting in my office in Sector 82A, I’d tell you that the biggest mistake buyers make is getting emotional before they get technical. In the current Mohali market, where multiple new corridors like the IT City belt and Airport Road expansion are launching simultaneously, the "trust" factor is often used as a marketing tool rather than a legal guarantee. 

I have seen this from the other side, having managed project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, the Municipal Committee, and the Forest Department. I have seen approvals granted and then reversed. I have seen projects stall because the developer’s cash flow was tied to a different project entirely. This guide is built on those "inside" lessons. If you follow these seven steps in order, you will not just buy a property; you will secure an asset.

## Step 1: How do I Verify a Project on RERA Punjab?

The first rule of Mohali real estate: If it is not on RERA, it does not exist for you. RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) is your primary shield. You must visit the [RERA Punjab portal](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property) and search for the project’s unique registration number.

What most buyers miss is the "Complaints" section and the "Project Status" updates. Do not just check if the project is registered. Check if the developer has been filing quarterly progress reports. If a project is 24 months old but the RERA portal shows 0% construction progress, you are looking at a potential stall. In my years navigating these approvals, I have found that the RERA filing is often more honest than the sales executive sitting in the site office.

## Step 2: What Does a Clean Title and Ownership Look Like?

A RERA registration does not automatically mean the title is clean. You must conduct a title search. In Punjab, this involves the "Jamabandi" or "Fard," the record of rights maintained by the Revenue Department.

You are looking for three things:
1. **Clear Chain of Title:** Does the developer actually own the land, or do they only have a "Collaboration Agreement" with a landlord?
2. **Encumbrance Certificate:** Is the land mortgaged to a bank?
3. **Court Attachments:** Are there any ongoing litigations involving the land parcel?

I recall a case where a client had paid 65% for two plots, only to discover the builder had cancelled them due to a dispute with a previous owner that was not disclosed. It took eight months of persistent follow-up to get those plots reinstated. A proper title check at Step 2 prevents these disasters.

## Step 3: How do I Spot Red Flags in a Builder's Payment Plan?

The payment plan is where the developer’s financial health is revealed. I always advise my clients to look for "Construction-Linked Plans" (CLP) over "Time-Linked Plans." 

A time-linked plan forces you to pay regardless of whether construction is moving. If the project stalls, you are still liable for installments. A [red flag in a payment plan](/blog/mohali-builder-payment-plan-red-flags) is any demand for more than 25% of the total value before significant construction milestones (like the casting of the first-floor slab) are reached. From my time managing developer finances, I know that developers who push for "early bird" time-linked discounts are often the ones facing the most immediate cash-flow stress.

## Step 4: What Should I Check in the Possession Clause?

The "Agreement to Sale" is the most important document you will ever sign, yet most buyers skip the fine print. You must look specifically at the possession clause. 

Does the agreement specify a date for "Possession for Fit-out" or "Final Possession with OC"? There is a massive legal difference. A "fit-out" possession means the building is not actually ready for occupation, and the developer is trying to stop their penalty clock. Also, check the penalty amount. RERA Punjab mandates that the interest paid by the developer for delays must be the same as the interest charged to the buyer for late payments. If your agreement says you pay 18% for delays but they pay ₹5 per sqft, that clause is illegal. You have [specific legal rights](/blog/mohali-property-possession-delay-legal-rights) under RERA to challenge such unfair contracts.

## Step 5: How do I Calculate the "All-In" Cost Without Surprises?

The price you see on the hoarding is never the price you pay. Mohali developers have a list of "hidden costs" that can add 15-20% to your budget.

When we do a [hidden cost calculation](/blog/hidden-costs-mohali-property-purchase), we include:
*   **EDC & IDC:** External and Internal Development Charges (often ₹200-400 per sqft).
*   **PLC:** Preferential Location Charges (for corner plots or park-facing units).
*   **Floor Rise:** Charges that increase as you go higher in a multi-storey building.
*   **Club Membership & Car Parking:** These are often non-negotiable add-ons.
*   **GST & Stamp Duty:** 5% GST on under-construction flats and 7% for registration in Punjab.

I have seen buyers forced to walk away from a deal at the registry stage because they had not accounted for the ₹15-25 lakh in [floor rise and PLC charges](/blog/floor-rise-charges-mohali-flat-negotiate) that only appeared in the final demand letter.

## Step 6: How do I Verify a Developer's Actual Track Record?

In 2026, do not buy the brand. Buy the delivery data. I have seen "branded" developers from outside Punjab struggle with local regulatory hurdles while local Mohali builders deliver months ahead of schedule.

The only way to verify a track record is to visit their previous projects. Don't look at the lobby. Look at the basement for seepage. Talk to the residents about the [maintenance charges](/blog/mohali-luxury-project-maintenance-charges) and whether the builder actually delivered the promised amenities. Ask for the Occupancy Certificate (OC) of their last project. If they cannot show you an OC for a project they "delivered" two years ago, they are operating in a legal grey area that you should avoid.

## Step 7: How do I Assess Exit Liquidity?

A property is only as good as your ability to sell it. Before you sign, ask: "Who is the buyer for this property five years from now?"

If you are buying a [luxury flat in a pre-launch](/blog/prelaunch-property-mohali-risk-reward), assess if there is enough expansion capacity in that sector. As I always say: "Je aapaan vision ton bina challaange taan aapaan mehngi cheez khareeddaange" (If we move without vision, we will buy the wrong thing). Areas where expansion is possible appreciate. Areas that are land-locked stagnate. If the sector is already saturated, your exit liquidity will be low. 

I have seen this in the older phases of Mohali where [stagnation zones](/blog/mohali-property-appreciation-vs-stagnation-zones) have seen zero price movement while the new corridors have tripled. Always check the master plan for upcoming connectivity like the Bharatmala roads before committing.
