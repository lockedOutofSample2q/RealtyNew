## The RERA Punjab Framework: More Than Just a Number

The Real Estate Regulatory Authority (RERA) of Punjab was established to bring transparency to the sector, but simply checking for a registration number is insufficient. In 2026, savvy buyers must dive deeper into the RERA Punjab portal. You should search for the project’s specific registration and navigate to the Quarterly Progress Reports (QPRs). 

These reports are mandatory filings where the developer must disclose the percentage of construction completed, the number of units sold, and any litigation involving the project. If a developer has failed to update their QPRs for more than two consecutive quarters, it serves as a massive red flag regarding their internal management or financial stability. We often discuss these technical indicators on our YouTube channel @AmritRealty to help buyers understand how to read between the lines of government data.

Furthermore, verify that the bank account mentioned in the RERA filing is an escrow account. Under the law, 70 percent of the funds collected from buyers must be deposited into this account to be used solely for construction and land costs. A developer who asks for payments in accounts other than the registered RERA escrow account is violating the law and putting your money at risk.

## Title Verification: The Thirty Year Search Rule

A clear title is the bedrock of any safe property transaction. In Punjab, revenue records like the Jamabandi, Fard, and Mutation (Intakal) are the primary documents that prove ownership. We recommend a 30 year search of the property’s history to ensure there are no undisclosed liens, mortgages, or legal disputes.

When dealing with plots in Mohali, particularly those in licensed colonies or GMADA sectors, you must verify the Allotment Letter and the subsequent Transfer Letter. For agricultural land being converted for residential use, the Change of Land Use (CLU) certificate issued by the Town and Country Planning Department of Punjab is critical. Without a valid CLU, any construction on the land is technically unauthorized and subject to demolition by authorities like GMADA or PUDA.

One often overlooked document is the Non Encumbrance Certificate (NEC). This certificate, usually issued by the Sub Registrar’s office, confirms that the property is free from any registered legal charges or mortgages for a specific period. If you are taking a home loan, the bank will perform this check, but for cash or self funded purchases, you must initiate this independently.

## Developer Due Diligence: Beyond the Glossy Brochure

The track record of a developer is the most reliable predictor of future performance. In Mohali, we have seen various developers enter the market, some with exceptional delivery standards and others who struggle with liquidity. Before signing, you must investigate the developer's past projects. Did they deliver on time? Was the quality of construction consistent with the sample flat? Most importantly, did they obtain the Occupation Certificate (OC) and Completion Certificate (CC) for their previous buildings?

In 2026, it is also wise to check the financial health of the developer’s parent company through the Ministry of Corporate Affairs (MCA) filings. Use the Director Identification Number (DIN) of the promoters to see if they are involved in other stalled projects or have significant outstanding debt. A developer who is overleveraged on one project will likely divert funds from your project to service their debts elsewhere. 

At RHMC, our experience as former developers gives us an "inside the fence" perspective on how these firms operate. We know that a developer with three active projects and no finished ones is a higher risk than a developer who finishes one project before moving to the next.

## The Agreement to Sale: Clauses That Can Trap You

The Agreement to Sale (ATS) is the legal contract that governs your relationship with the developer until the final Sale Deed is registered. Many buyers sign this document without reading the fine print, assuming it is a standard RERA format. While RERA does provide a model agreement, many developers insert custom clauses that favor them.

Key areas to scrutinize include the "Force Majeure" clause. While typically covering "acts of God," some developers expand this to include routine administrative delays or labor strikes to avoid paying penalties for late possession. Another critical area is the interest rate parity. RERA mandates that the interest a developer pays you for delays must be the same as the interest you pay them for late installments. If the agreement states you pay 18 percent for a late payment but they pay only 5 percent for a delay in possession, it is a direct violation of the law.

You should also look for the "Loading Factor" in the area calculation. In Mohali, an acceptable loading factor is typically between 25 and 30 percent. If the difference between the Super Area (what you pay for) and the Carpet Area (what you actually use) is more than 35 percent, you are paying a significant premium for common spaces that may not add proportional value to your lifestyle.

## Case Study: The Hidden Utility and Tax Trap

A common pitfall in Mohali property transactions involves the transition of utility bills and municipal taxes. We recently handled a case for a client buying a ground floor unit in a multi story building in a GMADA sector. The seller had not paid the Municipal Committee property tax for several years, which appeared as a collective liability across all floors when our client tried to clear it.

Our intervention involved contacting all upper floor owners and drafting a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to allocate the liability correctly. We coordinated with the Municipal Committee to generate separate property IDs for each floor and ensured the electricity bill was transferred to the buyer's name. This process took three months of persistent coordination.

The lesson here is simple: never assume that the seller has cleared all dues. Always demand a No Dues Certificate (NDC) from the Municipal Committee and the Punjab State Power Corporation Limited (PSPCL) before the final registration. Without these, you might find yourself liable for years of backdated taxes and interest penalties that the previous owner ignored.

## Regulatory Bodies: Navigating the Five Pillars

A project in Mohali is not "fully approved" unless it has cleared multiple hurdles across five different regulatory bodies. As an advisory with direct liaisoning experience, we track these closely:

1.  **GMADA/PUDA:** For colony licensing, plot allotment, and master plan compliance.
2.  **PSPCL:** For electrical load sanctions. Many high rise projects in Mohali have faced delays because the developer failed to secure the necessary transformer capacity for the entire building.
3.  **Forest and Conservation Authorities:** Especially relevant for projects near the Shivalik foothills or designated green belts. We have seen cases where forest department approval cancellations led to significant legal battles for developers and buyers alike.
4.  **Municipal Committee:** For building sanctions and the all important Occupation Certificate.
5.  **Industrial Departments:** If you are buying industrial plots or sheds near Rajpura or the IT City corridor, the classification of the zone (red, orange, or green) determines what business activities you can legally conduct.

## Hidden Costs: The 2026 Checklist

The "Basic Selling Price" (BSP) is rarely the final price you pay. In Mohali, several additional charges can inflate your budget by 10 to 15 percent:

*   **External Development Charges (EDC) and Internal Development Charges (IDC):** These are paid to the government for infrastructure like roads and sewerage. Ensure these are clearly defined in your agreement.
*   **Preferential Location Charges (PLC):** For corner plots, park facing units, or higher floors. 
*   **Interest Free Maintenance Security (IFMS):** A one time deposit for the upkeep of the society, usually held in a separate fund.
*   **Club Membership and Parking:** Often charged separately from the flat cost.
*   **Possession Linked Charges:** Like electricity meter installation, gas pipeline connection, and legal fees for the Sale Deed.

Always ask for an "All Inclusive Price" sheet that details every single rupee you will spend from booking to possession. If a developer is vague about these charges, they are likely hiding a significant future liability.

Never buy a property in Mohali based solely on a virtual tour or a site office visit. You must walk the actual site. Check the proximity to high tension wires, sewage treatment plants, or upcoming infrastructure projects that might cause noise or pollution. 

Verify the "Vision Corridors" of the city. For instance, the expansion of the Airport Road toward the Bharatmala Road junction is a major appreciation driver. However, if your property is too close to a proposed flyover, your access might be restricted.
