## The 2026 Reality: Infrastructure vs. Marketing

For anyone evaluating [Mohali real estate in 2026](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), Mullanpur (rechristened as New Chandigarh) represents the "clean slate" version of urban living. Unlike the organic growth of Zirakpur or the dense commercialization of the Airport Road, New Chandigarh is built on the [New Chandigarh Master Plan 2031](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/blog/new-chandigarh-master-plan-2031-property), which dictates low-density residential zones and wide internal roads.

The primary lifeline of this region in 2026 is the VR-1 road. This is the direct extension of Madhya Marg, connecting Chandigarh’s PGI and Panjab University directly to the heart of Mullanpur. While the road is functional, the 2026 reality is that peak-hour congestion at the PGI-Mullanpur barrier remains a factor for daily commuters. If a developer tells you it is "0 minutes from Chandigarh," they are referring to the boundary, not the commute time to Sector 17.

Infrastructure milestones reached by early 2026 include:
*   **The PCA Cricket Stadium:** Now a fully functional international venue and home to IPL matches, which has significantly driven up commercial values in the surrounding SCO segments.
*   **MediCity:** The 50-acre medical hub is no longer just a promise. With the Homi Bhabha Cancer Hospital and Research Centre operational, several other private healthcare providers have begun construction on their allotted sites.
*   **Education City:** Premium schools like the DLF School and Omaxe Foundation School are active, making the area viable for families who previously hesitated due to the lack of local schooling options.

## Major Pockets: What is Actually Built and Functional

When we look at the [Mohali sector property guide for 2026](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/blog/mohali-sector-property-guide-2026), New Chandigarh is divided into distinct private and government-led pockets. Understanding the occupancy levels in each is critical before booking.

### 1. DLF Hyde Park
As of 2026, DLF Hyde Park remains the most occupied and "settled" private township in New Chandigarh. The plots, floors, and independent villas here have a high percentage of end-user residency. The internal parks, wide 60-80 feet roads, and the functional clubhouse are the benchmarks for the region. However, the premium here is high, and secondary market transactions often require significant upfront liquidity.

### 2. Omaxe New Chandigarh (The Lake, Cassia, Celestia)
Omaxe has the largest footprint in the region. Projects like Omaxe Cassia and Celestia Royal have seen significant handovers by 2026. "The Lake" project, with its high-rise luxury positioning, is now a landmark. The challenge for buyers in Omaxe pockets often relates to the "loading factor" and the density of construction compared to DLF. Always verify the super built-up vs. carpet area ratio before committing.

### 3. GMADA Eco City-I & II
The government-developed Eco City projects are the "safest" in terms of title and utility connectivity. By 2026, Eco City-I is well-developed with many independent houses constructed. Eco City-II is the current focus for investors. The infrastructure here is maintained by GMADA, which ensures reliable water supply from the Kajauli waterworks and managed sewerage systems.

### 4. Manohar Singh and Altus
Manohar Singh's "The Palm" and various Altus projects cater to a mid-to-high segment. These are pockets where "vision" is still being converted into reality. While the plots are functional, the integration of all promised amenities like clubhouses or dedicated shopping complexes in newer phases is still ongoing in 2026.

## The Utility Checklist: What Most Buyers Miss

In my decade of experience handling over 180 transactions, I have seen that buyers often get distracted by Italian marble and false ceilings while ignoring the "invisible" infrastructure. In Mullanpur, this is where the risk lies.

### Electricity Load and PSPCL Status
A major hurdle in newer New Chandigarh projects is the load sanction from Punjab State Power Corporation Limited (PSPCL). Do not assume that because the building is ready, the electricity connection is permanent. Many developers run on temporary construction loads or high-capacity generators for months after possession. Verify if the project has its own dedicated 66KV substation, as promised in the master plan.

### Water and Sewerage Connectivity
New Chandigarh is an "Eco City," but that requires functional sewerage treatment plants (STPs). Before booking, check if the project is connected to the main GMADA trunk sewer line or if it relies on internal septic tanks. For water, ensure the project has a valid connection to the Kajauli waterworks. Relying solely on groundwater in New Chandigarh is increasingly difficult due to regulatory restrictions.

### Separate Property IDs and MC Tax
Even in premium townships, getting separate property tax IDs for floors or apartments is often a post-possession struggle. Ensure the developer provides a clear "No Dues Certificate" (NDC) at the time of registration. I have seen cases where buyers were saddled with collective tax liabilities because the developer failed to create individual property IDs at the Municipal Committee level.

## The OC Status: Why It Matters in 2026

The single biggest mistake buyers make in New Chandigarh is moving into a "ready-to-move" unit that does not have an Occupation Certificate (OC). Without an OC, you are technically an unauthorized occupant.

In 2026, RERA Punjab has become stricter, but some developers still push for "informal possession" to avoid delay penalties. An OC is proof that the building was constructed according to the sanctioned maps and that it is safe for habitation. If you are buying in the secondary market, do not release the final payment until you see the OC and the [GMADA plot transfer ownership documents](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/blog/gmada-plot-transfer-ownership-process).

## What to Verify Before Booking: The Independent Checklist

Before signing any booking form or paying the token amount, run through this [Mullanpur project due diligence checklist](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/blog/mullanpur-project-due-diligence-checklist-2026):

1.  **RERA Punjab Verification:** Go to the RERA Punjab portal and search for the project registration number. Check the "Quarterly Progress Reports" (QPR). If the developer is not updating these, it is a red flag for project management.
2.  **Forest Department Clearance:** Large parts of Mullanpur fall under the Punjab Land Preservation Act (PLPA). Ensure the specific Khasra numbers of the project are not under "Forest" classification. We have covered this in our guide on [how to check RERA Punjab property](https://www.realtyconsultants.in/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property).
3.  **Bank APF Code:** Ask for the Approved Project Facility (APF) code. If leading nationalized banks (like SBI) or top private banks (HDFC/ICICI) are not funding the project, their legal teams likely found an issue with the title or approvals.
4.  **The Site Visit Reality:** Do not just visit the air-conditioned experience center. Walk to the back of the project. See where the "Green Belt" is supposed to be. Check the distance from the nearest functional PR road. You can see our actual site visit walkthroughs on our YouTube channel @Amritrealty.
5.  **Historical Delivery:** Check the developer’s previous projects in Mohali or elsewhere in Punjab. Did they deliver on time? Was the quality of construction maintained in the common areas five years after handover?
