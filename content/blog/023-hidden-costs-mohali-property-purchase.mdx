# Hidden Costs in Mohali Property Purchases: The Complete List Most Buyers Discover Too Late

When you see a banner on Airport Road or a glossy brochure in Sector 82A advertising a luxury flat for ₹1.5 crore, that figure is rarely the amount you actually write on the final set of cheques. In Mohali’s real estate market, the "Base Sale Price" (BSP) is merely the starting point. Between that advertised number and the day you receive your keys, there is a layer of hidden costs — ranging from statutory taxes like GST and stamp duty to developer-levied charges like EDC, IDC, and PLC — that can add 15% to 25% to your total outflow. For a ₹1.5 crore property, this means you must be prepared for an additional ₹30 lakh to ₹40 lakh in "hidden" expenses that most buyers only calculate when they are too deep into the transaction to back out.

The core "hidden" costs include External Development Charges (EDC) of ₹200–400 per sqft, Infrastructure Development Charges (IDC) of ₹100–200 per sqft, and statutory costs like 6% Punjab stamp duty and 5% GST on under-construction units. When you add car parking (₹3–6 lakh), club membership (₹50k–2 lakh), and Preferential Location Charges (PLC), the final price often bears little resemblance to the initial quote.

## What are the most common hidden costs in Mohali projects?

In my years of navigating project approvals and managing sales from the developer side, I have seen many buyers get frustrated during the final payment stages. This frustration usually stems from a lack of transparency during the initial "chai and brochure" stage. To avoid this, you need to understand that a property price is built like an onion — there are layers.

### External and Infrastructure Development Charges (EDC & IDC)
EDC and IDC are fees that the developer pays to government bodies like GMADA or PUDA for developing the infrastructure around the project — roads, sewerage, street lighting, and water supply. In Mohali, EDC typically ranges from **₹200 to ₹400 per sqft**, while IDC falls between **₹100 and ₹200 per sqft**. In a standard 1,500 sqft apartment, these two combined can easily add ₹6 lakh to ₹9 lakh. Most "base prices" exclude these because they are subject to government revisions, and builders pass that variable cost directly to the buyer.

### Preferential Location Charges (PLC)
If your apartment faces a park, a wide road, or is a corner unit, the builder will charge you a premium. This is the PLC. In Mohali luxury projects, PLC usually ranges from **5% to 10% of the base price**. On a ₹1.5 crore flat, a 5% PLC adds ₹7.5 lakh. There are different types of PLC you might encounter: Park Facing, Corner Unit, Wide Road Facing, or specific North-East facing units.

### Floor Rise Charges
Typical floor rise charges in Mohali range from **₹50 to ₹150 per sqft per floor**. If you are buying on the 10th floor, you could be paying ₹1,000 per sqft more than the person on the ground floor. For a 2,000 sqft flat, that is a ₹20 lakh difference. This is a purely developer-driven cost and is one of the most negotiable parts of a deal during a pre-launch phase.

## Why is "Statutory Cost" the biggest surprise for buyers?

Statutory costs are non-negotiable payments to the government. Developers have no control over these, but buyers often forget to budget for them until the registry date nears.

### GST (Goods and Services Tax)
Currently, for residential projects, the GST is **5% without Input Tax Credit (ITC)**. On a ₹1.5 crore property, this is a direct ₹7.5 lakh addition. However, if the project has received its Completion Certificate (CC) or Occupancy Certificate (OC), GST is not applicable. This is one reason why "ready-to-move-in" properties can be more tax-efficient in the long run.

### Stamp Duty and Registration Fees
In Punjab, the stamp duty for property registration is **6%**, with a **1% registration fee**. These are calculated on the circle rate or the agreement value, whichever is higher. If your property is valued at ₹1.5 crore, you are looking at ₹9 lakh in stamp duty and ₹1.5 lakh in registration fees. ## What are the utility and "One-Time" charges?

Beyond the construction and the taxes, there are several "one-time" fees that builders collect.

*   **Car Parking Charges:** These range from **₹3 lakh to ₹6 lakh per spot**.
*   **Club Membership:** Expect a one-time charge of **₹50,000 to ₹2 lakh**. Check what this covers — often, cafe or spa usage remains pay-per-use.
*   **IFMS (Interest-Free Maintenance Security):** This is a corpus fund collected by the builder and eventually handed over to the Resident Welfare Association (RWA). It is usually ₹50 to ₹100 per sqft.
*   **Electricity Connection & Meter Charges:** In my experience, especially in multi-floor setups, this can cost **₹30,000 to ₹70,000**. We've discussed the importance of [separate electricity connections](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-pspcl) in detail elsewhere.

## Frequently Asked Questions About Hidden Costs

### Are EDC and IDC charges negotiable?
No. These are statutory-linked charges. While the builder might offer a "discount" on the base price to offset these, the charges themselves are fixed based on the project’s approvals with GMADA or PUDA.

### Do I have to pay GST on ready-to-move-in flats?
No. GST is only applicable to under-construction properties. Once a project receives its Completion Certificate (CC), the tax burden disappears. This is a critical distinction we cover in our [Mohali real estate glossary](/blog/mohali-real-estate-glossary-terms).

### Why is the loading factor considered a "hidden cost"?
If a 1,500 sqft flat has a 35% loading, your actual usable (carpet) area is only 975 sqft. You are paying the full per-sqft rate for that 525 sqft difference. Understanding the [super built-up vs carpet area calculation](/blog/super-built-up-carpet-area-mohali-loading-factor) is essential for knowing your real cost per usable square foot. 

### What happens if I don't pay the maintenance security (IFMS)?
IFMS is a mandatory part of the builder-buyer agreement. Without paying this, you will not receive the Possession Letter or the No Objection Certificate (NOC) required for registration. It is an interest-free safety net for the building's long-term maintenance.

### Is brokerage a hidden cost?
In Mohali, brokerage typically ranges from **1% to 2%**. Some advisors work on a fee-for-service model rather than a percentage, which can be more transparent for large-ticket transactions.

## How to protect yourself from cost escalations?

Demand a **"Complete Cost Sheet"** before you pay even a token amount. This sheet should itemize every single charge from the BSP down to the last rupee of GST. Having managed project documentation for developers, I know exactly where these costs are tucked away. I always advise my clients to look for the "Other Charges" clause in the allotment letter.
