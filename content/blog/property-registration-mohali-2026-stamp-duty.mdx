The **property registration mohali 2026 stamp duty** is a critical element of the local market landscape. Property registration in Mohali for 2026 involves a mandatory stamp duty of 7 percent for male buyers and 5 percent for female buyers. This rate applies to the higher value between the government-declared circle rate (collector rate) and the actual transaction value mentioned in the sale deed. In addition to stamp duty, a registration fee of 1 percent of the total property value is required. The process is managed through the National Generic Document Registration System (NGDRS) Punjab portal, where buyers must pay the fees online, generate an e-stamp, and schedule an appointment at the Sub-Registrar Office (SRO). Physical presence of the buyer, seller, and two witnesses is mandatory at the SRO in Mohali (SAS Nagar), Kharar, or Dera Bassi for biometric verification. Completion of the registration process typically takes 24 to 48 hours for the final deed collection after successful verification.

## Understanding Stamp Duty and Registration Fees in Punjab 2026

The cost of transferring property in Punjab is primarily determined by two components: Stamp Duty and Registration Fees. For the 2026 fiscal year, the Punjab government has maintained a slab-based system that encourages female ownership through lower rates.

Stamp duty is not a single tax but a combination of the basic duty, social security fund, and infrastructure cess. When calculating your total budget for a property in Mohali, you must account for these percentages:

*   **Male Buyers:** 7 percent total (composed of 5 percent basic stamp duty, 1 percent social security fund, and 1 percent infrastructure cess).
*   **Female Buyers:** 5 percent total (composed of 3 percent basic stamp duty, 1 percent social security fund, and 1 percent infrastructure cess).
*   **Joint Ownership (Male and Female):** 6 percent total (calculated as the average of the two rates).

The registration fee is a flat 1 percent across all categories. Unlike some states that cap registration fees at a fixed amount for high-value transactions, Punjab continues to calculate this on the full declared value. For a property worth Rs 1 crore in Aerocity or IT City, the male buyer would pay Rs 7 lakh in stamp duty and Rs 1 lakh in registration fees, bringing the total government cost to Rs 8 lakh.

## Circle Rates in Mohali: The 2026 Collector Rates

Circle rates, often referred to as collector rates, are the minimum values set by the district administration for property transactions. If you buy a property at a price lower than the circle rate, you are still legally required to pay stamp duty based on the circle rate.

## The Step-by-Step Process of Property Registration

The transition to the NGDRS portal has made the registration process more transparent, though it still requires meticulous attention to detail. Following the correct sequence is essential to avoid rejection at the SRO.

### Step 1: Document Preparation and Draft Deed
The process begins with drafting the Sale Deed (Vasiat). This document must include details of the buyer, seller, property description, and the final sale consideration. It should also specify the source of the property (how the seller acquired it) and confirm that the property is free from all encumbrances.

### Step 2: Online Entry on NGDRS Punjab
Visit the official NGDRS Punjab website. You will need to create a user ID and enter the details of the transaction. This includes the property's location, area, and the calculated value based on circle rates. The system will automatically generate the required stamp duty and registration fee amount.

### Step 3: Purchase of E-Stamps
Once the calculation is confirmed, you must purchase e-stamps through authorized vendors or designated banks. In 2026, digital payment through the Stock Holding Corporation of India Limited (SHCIL) portal is the standard method. Ensure that the e-stamp reflects the correct names of the parties involved.

### Step 4: Booking the Appointment
After paying the fees and generating the e-stamp, you can book an appointment for the physical visit. The Mohali SRO handles a high volume of cases, so it is advisable to book at least 3 to 5 days in advance. You will receive a slot time and a reference number. For a visual guide on navigating the NGDRS portal, we have a detailed walkthrough on our YouTube channel @Amritrealty.

### Step 5: The Physical Visit and Verification
On the day of the appointment, the buyer, seller, and two witnesses must reach the Tehsil office in Sector 76 (for GMADA areas) or the relevant SRO office. You must carry original ID proofs (Aadhar and PAN), the e-stamp paper, the draft sale deed, and the No-Objection Certificates (NOCs) from relevant authorities like GMADA or the Municipal Committee.

The Sub-Registrar will verify the documents and the biometrics (fingerprints and photographs) of all parties. If the property is a flat in a multi-story building, ensure that the Completion Certificate (OC) or the developer's NOC is in order.

## Mandatory Documents Checklist for 2026

Failure to provide even one document can lead to the cancellation of your appointment. Before heading to the SRO, verify this checklist:

1.  **Original Sale Deed:** The final drafted document ready for signatures.
2.  **E-Stamp Paper:** Of the required value (7 percent or 5 percent plus 1 percent).
3.  **Identification:** Aadhar Cards and PAN Cards of the buyer, seller, and both witnesses.
4.  **Proof of Ownership:** Allotment letters, previous registered deeds, or mutation (Intkal) records.
5.  **No-Objection Certificate (NOC):** Required from GMADA for alloted plots or from the Municipal Committee to prove no pending property tax dues.
6.  **Property Tax Receipts:** Proof that all Municipal Committee taxes are cleared up to the current date.
7.  **Photographs:** Passport-sized photos of the buyer and seller.
8.  **Maps and Layouts:** For specific plot registrations, a site map might be required by the SRO.

## Common Pitfalls: Property Tax and Shared Liabilities

One of the most frequent issues we encounter during the registration process in Mohali involves hidden property tax liabilities. In many multi-floor buildings (such as the independent floors in Sector 126 or Sector 115), property tax IDs are often created for the entire building rather than individual floors.

We recently handled a case where a buyer discovered back-dated taxes worth several lakhs when attempting to register a ground floor. The seller had not cleared the dues, and because the tax ID was shared, the buyer could not obtain the necessary No-Dues Certificate (NDC). We had to coordinate with all floor owners to draft a Memorandum of Understanding (MOU) to split the liability and generate separate property IDs. This process took three months of persistent follow-up with the Municipal Committee. Always verify that a separate property tax ID exists for your specific unit before you reach the registration stage.

## Post-Registration: The Mutation (Intkal) Process

Registration at the SRO is only the first half of the ownership transfer. The second, equally critical step is the Mutation of the property, known as Intkal in Punjab. Registration is the record of the transaction, but Mutation is the update of the government's revenue records to reflect you as the new owner.

Once the deed is registered, the data is sent to the Patwari of the area. You must follow up to ensure the mutation is recorded in the Jamabandi. This is vital for future sales, obtaining home loans, or applying for utility connections like electricity from PSPCL. Without a successful mutation, your legal title remains incomplete in the eyes of the revenue department.
