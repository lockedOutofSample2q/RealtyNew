# PUDA Approved vs Unapproved Projects in Mohali: What the Difference Means at Purchase and at Resale

To verify if a project is PUDA approved in Mohali, you must cross-reference the project’s RERA registration number on the RERA Punjab portal and verify the License to Develop on the GMADA/PUDA official website. A genuine "PUDA Approved" status means the developer has secured the Change of Land Use (CLU), obtained layout plan approval, and paid the required External Development Charges (EDC). The core difference between approved and unapproved projects lies in legal safety and financial liquidity: approved projects allow for easy bank financing and seamless title transfers (Mutation/Intkal), whereas unapproved projects often face registry bans, lack of basic utilities, and a total collapse in resale value due to the inability of future buyers to secure home loans.

In my years of navigating project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and the Municipal Committee, I have seen many buyers fall for the trap of "pre-launch" prices in colonies that never get their final license. If a project is not approved, you aren't just buying property; you are buying a decade of litigation and paperwork.

## What Does "PUDA Approved" Actually Mean in Practice?

When a developer in Mohali tells you their project is "PUDA Approved," they are effectively saying that the government has vetted every inch of the land and the proposed development. But "approval" isn't a single stamp; it's a sequence of hard-fought clearances that can take years to consolidate.

1.  **Change of Land Use (CLU):** This is the foundational hurdle. Most land in the periphery of Mohali is originally agricultural. The developer must pay significant fees to the government to convert this land for residential or commercial use. Without a CLU, any construction is technically illegal and can be demolished by authorities without notice. I have seen developers start "plotting" on land where the CLU was still pending, only for the application to be rejected due to master plan zoning conflicts.
2.  **Layout Plan Approval:** PUDA (or GMADA in the Mohali context) reviews the map. They ensure that 35-45% of the land is dedicated to public utilities—roads, parks, schools, and community centers. In unapproved colonies, developers often squeeze these areas to maximize the number of sellable plots. They might promise you a 60-foot road, but if the government map only shows 40 feet, your front yard could literally be reclaimed for road widening later.
3.  **External Development Charges (EDC) and IDC:** This is where many projects stall. The developer must pay the government to bring services like sewerage, water lines, and main roads to the project boundary. If these charges aren't paid, the government will not issue the 'License to Develop' or the 'Completion Certificate.' Many "approved" projects are actually stuck in a limbo where they have the initial LOI but haven't paid the EDC, meaning the buyer can never get a legal electricity connection from PSPCL.

Understanding this framework is essential for any [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026) reader. If these three pillars aren't in place, the project is a "Kacha" colony, regardless of how fancy the entrance gate looks.

## How to Verify a Project's PUDA Approval Status Online?

Don't take a broker’s word or a glossy brochure as proof. You need to do your own due diligence using the tools the Punjab government has provided. Having managed documentation from the developer side, I know that "applied for" is often used as a mask for "rejected" or "unlikely to happen."

### Step 1: The RERA Punjab Portal
Every approved project must be registered with RERA. Visit [rera.punjab.gov.in](https://rera.punjab.gov.in) and search for the project name or registration number. If the project isn't there, or if the registration has expired, walk away. RERA registration is a filter that keeps out the most fraudulent developers. You can learn the specifics of this in our guide on [how to check RERA Punjab property](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property).

### Step 2: The GMADA/PUDA List of Approved Colonies
GMADA maintains a list of licensed colonies on their official website. Look for the "License to Develop" (LTD). This document will list the total area approved, the name of the promoter, and the validity period. If a developer claims to have 50 acres approved but the LTD only shows 20 acres, you know there is a problem with the remaining 30.

### Step 3: Physical Document Verification
Ask the developer for the following:
*   **The LOI (Letter of Intent):** This shows the government's intent to grant a license provided conditions are met. It is NOT the final approval.
*   **The Final License (LTD):** This is the ultimate proof. It bears the signature of the Chief Administrator of PUDA/GMADA.
*   **Approved Layout Map:** Ensure the plot or flat you are buying exists on the government-stamped map with the correct dimensions.

If you find this process overwhelming, it's part of the standard [mohali developer project due diligence checklist](/blog/mohali-developer-project-due-diligence-checklist) we use for our clients.

## The Dangerous Gap Between "Applied for Approval" and "Approved"

This is the most common trap in the Mohali market. A developer will show you a "receipt" of application and claim that approval is "expected in 15 days." 

In reality, the gap between applying and receiving a license can be years—or it might never close. I’ve seen projects where the CLU was applied for, but the Forest Department raised an objection because the land was too close to a protected green belt. In one case I handled, a project faced an 8-month delay just because of a minor mismatch in the revenue records (Fard) that required a manual correction in the Patwari's office.

If you buy during the "applied for" stage:
*   **Zero Bank Financing:** You cannot get a home loan. No bank will touch a project that doesn't have a RERA number and a clear LTD.
*   **Layout Instability:** The layout might change. The government might mandate a park right where your plot is supposed to be.
*   **Approval Cancellation:** The project might be cancelled entirely, leaving you to fight for a [refund for a stalled project](/blog/mohali-project-stalled-cancelled-booking-refund).

## Why Buying in an Unapproved Project Destroys Resale Value

Many buyers, especially [land sellers reinvesting in Mohali](/blog/land-seller-reinvestment-guide-punjab-2026), are tempted by unapproved projects because they are 30-40% cheaper. They think, "I'll live here, so why do I care about a license?"

Here is why you should care: **Liquidity and Legal Survival.**

### The "Cash-Only" Trap
When you want to sell that property 5 years from now, your pool of buyers will be limited to people with 100% cash. No major bank (SBI, HDFC, ICICI) will provide a home loan for an unapproved property. In a market where 80% of buyers rely on financing, you have just eliminated the vast majority of your potential customers. This effectively caps your appreciation because you can only sell to people who have large amounts of liquid cash—who are usually looking for "distress deals" anyway.

### The Registry and Mutation Nightmare
The government of Punjab periodically bans registries in unapproved colonies to curb illegal development. If the registry is banned, you cannot transfer the title. Even if you manage a registry through some loophole, the [mutation (intkal) process](/blog/property-mutation-intkal-mohali-process) in the revenue records will be blocked. Without a mutation, you are not the owner in the eyes of the Tehsildar, and the previous owner can still theoretically sell the land to someone else.

Furthermore, the government refuses electricity connections (PSPCL) to such areas. You might be forced to rely on expensive diesel generators or illegal "kundi" connections that can be cut off any day. Understanding [GMADA Mohali explained](/blog/gmada-mohali-explained-guide) is crucial here, as GMADA is the body that will eventually decide if your colony can ever be regularized—and the regularization fees (Compounding Charges) are often so high that they wipe out all your initial "savings."

## Common Developer Language That Sounds Like Approval (But Isn't)

Over the years, I've heard every creative way a developer tries to avoid saying the word "unapproved." Watch out for these phrases:

*   **"Municipal Committee Approved":** While MC approval is necessary for building plans, it is NOT a substitute for PUDA's Change of Land Use (CLU) if the land is in a controlled area.
*   **"Registry Start Hai":** A common lie. Just because a sub-registrar is allowing registries doesn't mean the colony is licensed. A registry is a record of sale; it is not a certificate of legality.
*   **"Boundary Wall Completed":** A wall is easy to build. A sewerage treatment plant, rainwater harvesting, and 60-foot roads are hard.
*   **"In a Red Zone/Yellow Zone":** These are master plan terms indicating where residential development *could* happen. It doesn't mean *this specific project* has been approved.

## The Hidden Cost of "Unapproved" Success

Some people will tell you, "I bought an unapproved plot and made money." They are the lucky ones. For every success story, there are fifty families whose life savings are stuck in a piece of land that has no road access and no hope of a registry.

In my experience as a developer, I know that the cost of getting approvals is roughly 15-20% of the project cost. If a developer is selling at a price that seems too good, they are likely skipping these costs. They aren't giving you a discount; they are transferring their legal and financial risk to you.

## Frequently Asked Questions About PUDA Approval

### Is PUDA approval same as GMADA approval?
In the context of Mohali, yes. PUDA is the parent body for the state of Punjab, while GMADA is the specific authority for the Greater Mohali area. A project in Mohali is approved by GMADA following PUDA’s guidelines. If someone says "GMADA Approved," they are referring to the same licensing framework.

### Can I get a home loan on a project without PUDA approval?
Generally, no. Scheduled commercial banks (SBI, PNB, HDFC, etc.) will not fund unapproved projects. Some non-banking financial companies (NBFCs) might offer loans at high interest rates (12-14%), but they often require the property to be in a "notified" area, which most illegal colonies are not.

### How long does the PUDA licensing process take?
Ideally, it should take 6-9 months if all documents are clear. In practice, it often takes 18-24 months due to the multiple NOCs required from departments like Fire Safety, Forest Department, and the State Power Corporation (PSPCL). Any developer claiming they will get it in "30 days" is being dishonest.

### What happens if I buy an unapproved plot and the government demolishes it?
Under the Punjab Apartment and Property Regulation Act (PAPRA), the government has the right to demolish unauthorized structures. As a buyer, your legal recourse is against the developer, but recovering money from a developer who has already broken the law is a long and painful process.

### Does RERA registration mean the project is PUDA approved?
Not necessarily. RERA registration requires the developer to have the initial approvals (like CLU), but the "License to Develop" might still be pending certain conditions. However, RERA provides a level of protection that unapproved projects lack entirely.
