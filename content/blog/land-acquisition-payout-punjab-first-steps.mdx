# Land Acquisition Payout in Punjab: The First 90 Days: What to Do, What to Avoid, Who to Speak With

![Step-by-step guide for the first 90 days after receiving a land acquisition payout in Punjab](/assets/images/blog/050-land-acquisition-payout-punjab-first-steps.jpg)

The first 90 days after receiving a land acquisition payout in Punjab are the most critical for protecting your family’s future wealth. You must take three immediate steps: first, consult a Chartered Accountant to clarify your tax position under Section 10(37) or 54F: second, park your funds in the Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS) to preserve your 24-month reinvestment window: and third, align your family on whether the goal is pure investment or relocation to a hub like Mohali. Avoid the common traps of lending to relatives or rushing into low-yield Fixed Deposits that barely beat inflation. By the end of Month 3, you should have a documented budget and a shortlist of properties in expansion corridors. This period is not about buying quickly, it is about setting the foundation so that life-changing money does not vanish into bad decisions or unnecessary taxes.

## What should you do in the first 30 days after receiving a land acquisition payout?

The moment the money hits your account, the clock starts ticking. In Punjab, many land acquisitions for infrastructure projects like the Bharatmala Road are exempt from capital gains tax under Section 10(37) of the Income Tax Act, provided the land was used for agricultural purposes. However, you cannot assume this applies to you without professional verification.

Your first month must be dedicated to "Paperwork and Protection." You need to collect every piece of paper related to the acquisition: the initial notification, the award statement from the Land Acquisition Collector (LAC), and the final payment receipts. These documents are your shield against future tax scrutiny. Once your tax position is clear, address the "parking" problem. Keeping a large sum in a regular savings account is a security risk and an invitation for relatives to ask for loans. I always advise my clients to look at the Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS). This specialized bank account tells the tax department you intend to reinvest within the legal 24-month window, earns interest, and prevents the tax department from treating the entire payout as taxable income in the current year.

## Why is the 24-month reinvestment window so critical for Punjab land sellers?

In my years of navigating the [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), I have seen too many families wait until the 23rd month to start looking for property. This is a recipe for disaster. Under Section 54F, you generally have 24 months to purchase a residential property or 36 months to construct one to claim tax exemptions on your capital gains.

If you miss this window, that "life-changing money" gets chopped by 20% or more in taxes. You can read more about the specifics in my guide on the [land acquisition payout reinvestment window](/blog/land-acquisition-payout-reinvestment-window). The pressure of a closing window often leads people to buy the first thing a broker pushes on them, which is usually a project with low liquidity or high risk. By starting your strategy in the first 90 days, you give yourself the luxury of choice. You can evaluate whether you want to spread the money across multiple smaller properties or buy one high-quality asset in a growth corridor.

## Should you focus on relocation or pure investment?

By Month 2, the initial excitement has settled into a realization of responsibility. This is the time for a serious family meeting. You need to decide: are we staying in the village and just investing for returns, or is this the moment we move the family to a city like Mohali for better schools and healthcare?

This decision changes everything. If the goal is relocation, you are looking for ready-to-move-in flats or floors in sectors with established infrastructure. If the goal is pure investment, you might look at upcoming industrial zones or commercial SCOs on Airport Road where the "vision investing" principle applies. I have seen families relocate to Mohali because they wanted proximity to PGIMER for elderly parents or better education for their children. ## Should you park your land acquisition money in a Fixed Deposit (FD)?

The elder generation often feels that an FD in a government bank is the only "safe" place for money. Let me show you the math that most bankers will not. If you put your money in an FD at a 7% interest rate, and you are in the 30% tax bracket, your net return is actually 4.9%. Now, consider that inflation in India usually hovers around 6%. This means every year your money stays in that FD, your real wealth is shrinking by 1.1%.

Compare this to real estate in an expansion corridor. I have seen industrial land in the Rajpura belt go from ?18.70 lakh per vigha to ?45 lakh per vigha in just six months post-registration because of the Bharatmala connectivity. Even a conservative residential plot in a GMADA-approved sector in Mohali typically outpaces inflation significantly. ## What are the "Red Flags" to avoid in the first three months?

The first 90 days are when you are most vulnerable to "advice" from people who do not have your best interests at heart. Here is my blunt list of what to avoid:

1. **Lending to Relatives:** This is the fastest way to lose both your money and your relationships. 
2. **Impulsive Luxury:** Buying the latest SUV might feel good, but it is a depreciating asset. Your land was an appreciating asset: you must replace it with another one.
3. **Rushing into "Pre-launch" Deals:** Brokers love pre-launch deals because they get high commissions. You should only look at RERA-approved projects with a clear delivery track record. 
4. **Buying Without Title Clarity:** In Punjab, "fard" and "jamabandi" are everything. Never pay a token amount until a professional has verified the title.
