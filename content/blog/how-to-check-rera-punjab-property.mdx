# How to Verify Any Mohali Property on RERA Punjab: Step-by-Step With What Each Field Actually Means

To verify a Mohali property on RERA Punjab, visit the official portal at rera.punjab.gov.in, navigate to the 'Search' menu, and select 'Registered Projects'. You can search by the project name or the RERA registration number. A verified project will display an 'Active' status, a valid registration period, and updated Quarterly Progress Reports (QPRs). If a project does not appear, it is either exempt due to its small size, under 500 square meters or 8 apartments, or it is an unauthorized development lacking legal approval. Every serious buyer must ensure the registration number on the developer's brochure matches the official record on the portal before making any payment.

In my years of navigating the real estate system as both a developer and an advisor, I have seen that RERA is the single most powerful tool in a buyer's arsenal. Having managed project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and the Municipal Committee, I know what happens behind the scenes. Developers often present a polished image in brochures, but the RERA portal reveals the unfiltered truth of their financial health and construction progress.

## Why RERA Punjab is Your First Line of Defense in Mohali

Before RERA (Real Estate Regulatory Authority) was enacted, buying a property in Mohali was a game of trust where the rules were written by the developers. Today, the law requires every project with a land area exceeding 500 square meters or more than 8 apartments to be registered. This registration is not just a formality: it is a legal commitment that binds the developer to a specific timeline and a specific quality of construction.

When I served as a director of a RERA-approved company, I saw the level of detail the authority demands. They do not just ask for a map: they track every rupee that enters the project through a mandatory escrow account. They demand to see the approved layout maps, the land title documents, the encumbrance certificates, and the exact specifications of materials to be used. When you check a project on the RERA Punjab portal, you are essentially looking at the same compliance documents the government uses to monitor the developer.

## The Step-by-Step Walkthrough: Navigating rera.punjab.gov.in

The Punjab RERA website is fairly intuitive, but there are specific paths you must follow to get the full project brief. It is easy to miss the deeper reports if you only look at the summary page.

1. **Visit the Portal:** Open rera.punjab.gov.in on your browser. Always use the official government URL. Avoid third-party property portals that mirror RERA data, as they are often months behind.
2. **Access the Search Menu:** On the top navigation bar, look for the 'Search' tab. Hover over it to see the dropdown menu.
3. **Select Registered Projects:** Click on 'Registered Projects'. This will open a search interface where you can filter projects across all of Punjab.
4. **Filter by District:** Select 'SAS Nagar' from the district dropdown. It is a common mistake for buyers to look for 'Mohali', in official government records, the district is always SAS Nagar.
5. **Enter Project Details:** You can type the project name or, ideally, the registration number provided by the builder (e.g., PBRERA-SAS80-PRXXXX). Using the number is more accurate.
6. **View Detailed Report:** Once the project appears in the results, click on the 'View' icon. This will open a detailed report where the real due diligence begins.

## Decoding the Fields: What Each Section Actually Tells You

Once you open the project report, you will see a series of tabs and tables. Understanding what each field means, and what it implies for your money, is the difference between a cursory glance and professional-grade due diligence.

### 1. Project Registration Number
This is the birth certificate of the project. If a developer tells you they are "RERA applied" or "approvals expected soon," it means they do not have this number yet. Legally, they cannot market, advertise, or sell even a single unit without a valid registration number. 

### 2. Promoter Details and Past Track Record
This section lists the individuals or company directors behind the project. I always check if the promoter has other registered projects. If they have three other projects in Mohali and all are shown as 'Delayed' or have 'Revoked' status, it signals a pattern of poor management or financial instability.

### 3. Project Status: Active, Extended, or Revoked
Active means the project is within its original completion timeline. Extended means the developer has already asked for more time. Multiple extensions are a massive red flag. Revoked means the authority has cancelled the registration. Never buy into a revoked project.

### 4. Quarterly Progress Reports (QPRs): The Pulse Check
This is the most critical part of the portal. Developers are required to update their construction progress and sales data every three months. If you see that the last QPR was filed over six months ago, it is a sign that the developer is avoiding regulatory scrutiny.

### 5. Project Bank Account (The Escrow Logic)
RERA mandates that 70% of the money collected from buyers must be deposited into a dedicated escrow account. This ensures that the money you pay for your flat in Sector 82A is not being diverted to buy new land for a project in Mullanpur.

## How to Check for Litigations and RERA Orders

One of the most underutilized features of the RERA Punjab portal is the 'Orders' section. Many buyers check if a project is registered but fail to check if it is being sued.

1. **Go to the 'Judgment' or 'Orders' tab:** Search for the project name or promoter name.
2. **Read the Adjudicating Officer's Orders:** Look for orders related to 'Refund with Interest'. If there are multiple orders where the builder has been told to pay back buyers due to delays, it shows the project is in financial trouble.
3. **Check for 'Stay Orders':** Sometimes, the authority or a court might stay the sale of a project due to a title dispute or an environmental violation. 

I have navigated these regulatory traps personally. Having seen forest department approval cancellations and title disputes from the inside, I can tell you that a clean RERA record is non-negotiable. ## Common Red Flags in RERA Records

In my advisory practice, I use the RERA portal to spot "silent" failures before they become public news. Here is my checklist of red flags:

* **Promoter Mismatch:** The land owner listed on the RERA portal is different from the developer selling you the unit, and there is no registered Joint Development Agreement (JDA) shown.
* **Expired Registration:** The 'Estimated Completion Date' has passed, and there is no 'Extended' status.
* **Financial Gaps in QPR:** If the sales are high but the construction percentage is low, the developer is likely using your money to fund other operations.
* **Missing Statutory Approvals:** Look for the 'Approvals' tab. If the 'Fire NOC' or 'Environmental Clearance' is marked as "Applied for," the project could be halted later.

## What to do When a Project is Not Found on RERA Punjab

If you search for a property and it does not appear, there are three possibilities:

1. **The Project is Exempt:** Very small projects (under 500 sqm or 8 units) do not require RERA.
2. **It is an Old Project:** Projects that received their Completion Certificate (CC) before July 2017 are not required to be on the portal. For these, you must rely on the physical CC and Occupation Certificate (OC) documents.
3. **It is an Unauthorized Colony:** This is the most dangerous scenario. If a large project is being marketed without a RERA number, it is likely lacking CLU (Change of Land Use) or layout approvals.

Never fall for the "pre-launch" trap where a developer asks for a booking amount "before the RERA number comes." This is illegal and leaves you with zero protection if the approvals are eventually denied.
