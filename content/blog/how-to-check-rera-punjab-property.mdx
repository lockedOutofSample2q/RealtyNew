### Accessing the RERA Punjab Official Portal

The only authoritative source for property verification in the state is the Real Estate Regulatory Authority of Punjab. Avoid third party sites that claim to host RERA data, as these are often outdated or incomplete.

1. Open your browser and go to rera.punjab.gov.in.
2. Locate the "Registrations" menu on the homepage.
3. Click on "Search Projects" or "Registered Projects" to open the database.
4. For Mohali specific properties, you can filter by the district "SAS Nagar".

If you are evaluating a project in the Zirakpur or New Chandigarh areas, remember that these also fall under the SAS Nagar (Mohali) jurisdiction for RERA purposes.

### Searching by Name vs. Registration Number

Searching by the registration number (e.g., PBRERA-SAS80-PR0xxx) is the most accurate method. Developers often use "marketing names" that differ from the legal names registered with the authority. For instance, a project marketed as "Grand Plaza" might be registered under the developer's entity name like "ABC Infra Projects Phase 1". If a search by marketing name yields no results, ask the developer for the exact RERA registration number. Failure to provide this is a major red flag, as Section 3 of the RERA Act mandates that no developer can advertise or sell without a valid registration.

### Decoding the Project Detail Page: Field by Field

Once you click on a project, you will be presented with a comprehensive "Project Detail" page. Understanding these fields is where most buyers fail.

#### 1. Registration Valid Up To
This is the most critical date. It indicates the deadline the developer has promised the authority for project completion. If this date has passed and the project is still under construction, the developer is technically in default unless they have applied for an extension. According to recent reports in the Economic Times, RERA Punjab has become increasingly stringent about granting extensions, often requiring proof of force majeure or significant structural delays beyond the developer's control.

#### 2. Project Status
You will typically see "Ongoing" or "Completed". If a project is listed as "Completed", it should have an Occupancy Certificate (OC) or Completion Certificate (CC) uploaded. If you see "Under Investigation" or "Suspended", halt all transactions immediately.

#### 3. Promoter Details and Track Record
RERA requires developers to list all projects they have launched in the last five years, along with their current status. Use this section to verify if the developer has a history of delays in Mohali. A promoter with multiple "Stalled" projects in other sectors is a high risk choice, regardless of how attractive the current project looks.

#### 4. The 70% Separate Bank Account
Under Section 4(2)(l)(D) of the Act, developers must deposit 70% of the funds collected from buyers into a separate escrow account to be used only for construction and land costs. On the RERA portal, you can see the bank details and the quarterly progress reports (QPRs) that track these funds. This ensures that the money you pay for a flat in Sector 82 is not being diverted to fund the developer's other projects in Ludhiana or Patiala.

### Analyzing Land Title and Encumbrances

The "Land Details" section reveals if the land is owned by the developer or if there is a Joint Development Agreement (JDA) with a landowner. It also lists "Encumbrances," which refers to any bank loans or mortgages taken against the land.

It is common for developers in Mohali to take construction finance from banks. However, you must ensure that the developer has a "No Objection Certificate" (NOC) from the lending bank to sell individual units. If the RERA filing shows massive undisclosed encumbrances, it indicates a high debt load that could lead to project insolvency.

### The Importance of Quarterly Progress Reports (QPR)

A common mistake buyers make is checking RERA once and never looking back. RERA Punjab mandates that developers update their progress every three months. 

When you view the QPR on the portal, check for:
* **Percentage of Work Completed:** Compare the physical progress you see during a site visit with the percentage reported to RERA.
* **Number of Units Booked:** This shows the inventory status and the project's liquidity.
* **Changes in Layout:** Any significant change in the sanctioned plan requires the consent of two thirds of the allottees.

### Verifying the Real Estate Agent

In Mohali, most transactions happen through channel partners or agents. Just like projects, agents must be registered with RERA Punjab. 
* Go to the "Search Agent" section on the portal.
* Enter the agent's name or RERA number (e.g., PBRERA-SAS79-REA0xxx).
* Ensure their registration is active.

Dealing with an unregistered agent leaves you with no legal recourse under RERA if the agent misrepresents facts about the property.

### What a Clean Record vs. a Troubled Record Looks Like

A "Clean" RERA record shows:
1. Regular quarterly updates (QPRs) filed on time.
2. An "Encumbrance" list that matches the developer's verbal disclosures.
3. A completion date that aligns with the current stage of construction.
4. Uploaded copies of the approved building plan, layout plan, and proforma of the allotment letter.

A "Troubled" RERA record shows:
1. Missing QPRs for several quarters.
2. Frequent changes in the "Project Completion Date".
3. Litigation listed in the "Legal Details" section.
4. Discrepancies between the total area registered and the area being marketed.

### Local Market Context: The Mohali Expansion

The Tribune has recently highlighted that the Punjab government is integrating RERA data with land records to prevent double registration of properties. This is a significant move for sectors like 82, 83, and 101, where industrial and commercial interests overlap. When you check RERA for these areas, pay close attention to the "Approved Use" of the land to ensure you aren't buying a residential unit on land zoned for industrial or institutional use.

### Legal Recourse Through the Portal

If your verification reveals that a developer has provided false information, RERA Punjab allows you to file a formal complaint online. Under the "Complaints" tab, you can initiate Form M (Complaint to Authority) or Form N (Complaint to Adjudicating Officer for compensation). The portal tracks the status of these complaints, and you can even search for previous orders passed against a specific developer to see how they have handled past disputes.

### Summary of the Verification Checklist

Before signing any cheque for a Mohali property, perform this 10 minute RERA audit:
* **Step 1:** Verify the registration number on rera.punjab.gov.in.
* **Step 2:** Check the "Valid Up To" date.
* **Step 3:** Review the latest QPR to see if construction matches the timeline.
* **Step 4:** Look for any "Encumbrances" or "Litigation" entries.
* **Step 5:** Confirm the "Agent RERA" if you are working with a consultant.
