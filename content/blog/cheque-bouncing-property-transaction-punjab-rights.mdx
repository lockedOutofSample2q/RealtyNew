## The Direct Answer
In Punjab’s real estate market, a bounced cheque is not just a financial delay—it is a **criminal offense under Section 138 of the Negotiable Instruments Act.** If a buyer's booking amount or a seller's payment cheque returns with a "Funds Insufficient" memo, the law provides a strict 30-day window to issue a legal notice. For property transactions in Mohali, where tokens often range from ₹5 Lakh to ₹50 Lakh, the most effective "right" is the immediate suspension of the Sale Agreement followed by a formal RERA complaint if the transaction involves a licensed developer.

## The Section 138 Timeline: A Non-Negotiable Window
The legal machinery in Punjab for cheque bounce cases moves on a very specific clock. Missing even one of these milestones can weaken your recovery position:
1. **The Bounce:** You receive the "Return Memo" from your bank.
2. **The 30-Day Notice:** You must send a formal demand notice to the drawer within 30 days of the bounce. This notice should explicitly demand payment within 15 days of receipt.
3. **The 15-Day Wait:** The law gives the drawer 15 days to "make good" the payment.
4. **The Complaint:** If payment isn't made, you have exactly 30 days to file a criminal complaint in the relevant Judicial Magistrate's court.

## Cheque Bouncing in Developer Deals vs. Resale
The "rights" you have differ depending on who issued the cheque:
- **Developer Deals:** If a builder's refund cheque bounces, you can approach **RERA Punjab.** RERA has the power to impose heavy penalties and even arrest warrants for non-compliance with refund orders.
- **Resale (Private Parties):** This is purely a Judicial Magistrate matter. However, a bounced cheque also invalidates the "consideration" clause of the Sale Agreement (Iqrarnama). This allows the seller to legally cancel the deal and retain the forfeiture amount, provided the agreement was drafted with surgical precision.

## Strategic Advisory: The "Double-Verification" Rule
At Realty Holding and Management Consultants, we advise all clients to avoid relying solely on cheques for large token amounts. 
- **The Protocol:** Use RTGS or NEFT for any amount exceeding ₹2 Lakh. If a cheque must be used, ensure the "Cheque Number" is mentioned in the registered Sale Agreement.
- **The Proof:** Always keep the original "Bank Return Memo." Do not re-deposit a bounced cheque without consulting an advisor, as the date on the memo is what triggers your legal rights.

## Summary Checklist for Victims
- **Preserve Evidence:** Original cheque, return memo, and the speed-post receipt of the legal notice.
- **Stop the Transfer:** If you are a seller, immediately notify GMADA or the Tehsildar to stop any pending mutation (Intkal).
- **RERA Linkage:** If the deal is for a RERA-registered project, file an online complaint under Form M for "Recovery of Interest/Amount."
