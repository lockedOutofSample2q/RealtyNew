# NRI Tax on Mohali Property Sale 2026: TDS, Capital Gains, and Repatriation: What You Must Know Before Selling

![NRI tax on property sale in Mohali showing TDS and capital gains calculation](/assets/images/blog/041-nri-tax-property-sale-mohali-tds-capital-gains.jpg)

NRIs selling property in Mohali in 2026 face a mandatory TDS of 22.66% on the total sale value for long-term capital gains (assets held over 2 years). This deduction is often significantly higher than the actual tax liability, as it is calculated on the sale price rather than the profit. To optimize this, you must either apply for a Lower Deduction Certificate (LDC) from the Income Tax department before the transaction or claim a refund during your annual tax filing. The process requires navigating Form 15CA and 15CB for repatriation and ensuring your sale proceeds are credited to an NRO account. In my experience managing these exits for the Punjabi diaspora in Canada, the UK, and Australia, the biggest mistake isn't the tax itself: it's failing to plan the "white" money repatriation timeline, which typically takes 4-8 weeks post-registry.

## Why is the TDS for NRIs so high in Mohali?

When a resident Indian sells a property, the buyer deducts a symbolic 1% TDS. But when you, as an NRI, sell your plot in Aerocity or your flat in Sector 82A, the law views it differently. The Income Tax Act mandates the buyer to deduct TDS at the highest applicable rate to ensure the government doesn't lose out on tax from someone living abroad.

For 2026, if you have held the property for more than 24 months, it is classified as a Long-Term Capital Gain (LTCG). The base tax rate is 20%, but once you add the 10% surcharge (for gains above ?50 lakh) and the 4% health and education cess, the effective rate hits **22.66%**.

If you sell within 24 months, it’s a Short-Term Capital Gain (STCG), taxed at your applicable slab rate, which for most NRIs selling high-value Mohali property defaults to **30% plus surcharge**. This is why I always advise my clients in Birmingham or Brampton to wait for the 24-month mark before exiting. This holds true whether you are selling a residential flat or a commercial SCO on Airport Road. ## How do you calculate the actual Capital Gains Tax?

The "shock" of the 22.66% TDS is usually mitigated when we calculate the actual tax on profit. In India, NRIs get the benefit of **indexation** for long-term gains. This means we adjust the original purchase price of your Mohali property against inflation using the Cost Inflation Index (CII) provided by the government annually.

For example, if you bought a GMADA plot in 2015 for ?50 lakh and sell it in 2026 for ?1.5 crore, your profit isn't simply ?1 crore. We "index" that ?50 lakh to its 2026 value. If the inflation-adjusted indexed cost comes to ?85 lakh, your taxable profit is only ?65 lakh. Your actual tax liability at 20% (plus surcharge/cess) is far lower than the 22.66% TDS the buyer is forced to deduct from the ?1.5 crore total.

In many cases, I’ve seen NRIs sell inherited property in old Mohali phases. For inherited assets, the "cost of acquisition" is what the original owner (your father or grandfather) paid, indexed from the year they bought it or from 2001 (if bought earlier). This often results in a significantly lower tax burden than expected, provided your paperwork is in order. ## Can you avoid the 22.66% TDS deduction upfront?

Yes, and this is where most NRIs lose time. You can apply for a **Lower Deduction Certificate (LDC)**. This is a formal application to the Assessing Officer of the Income Tax Department, providing a detailed calculation of your actual capital gains.

Having navigated these applications for clients, I can tell you it takes about 30 to 45 days. The officer reviews your purchase deed, indexed cost calculation, and the proposed sale agreement. If satisfied, they issue a certificate allowing the buyer to deduct tax at a much lower rate, perhaps 3% or 5%, based on your actual projected profit.

Without this certificate, the buyer's lawyer will insist on deducting the full 22.66% to protect the buyer from "default" penalties. This keeps your liquidity intact rather than waiting 18 months for a tax refund from the Indian government.

## The Step-by-Step LDC Process in Mohali

1.  **Agreement to Sell:** You must have a signed agreement with the buyer. This document establishes the intent and the price.
2.  **TAN Number:** The buyer must have or apply for a Tax Deduction and Collection Account Number (TAN). This is the identifier used for depositing the TDS.
3.  **Online Application:** Your CA files Form 13 on the TRACES portal. This is a digital-first process now, though the officer may request physical documents.
4.  **Documentation:** You provide the purchase deed, all payment receipts for the original purchase (to prove the cost), and a calculation of the indexed cost.
5.  **Officer Review:** The Assessing Officer in the Chandigarh/Mohali ward reviews the case. They check for any outstanding tax dues you might have in India.
6.  **Issuance:** The certificate is issued digitally, which you then hand over to the buyer before the registry.

## What are the mandatory forms for NRI repatriation?

Once the registry is done in the Mohali Tehsil office and the funds are in your NRO account, the next hurdle is moving that money to your home country. This is governed by FEMA (Foreign Exchange Management Act) rules. You can read more about [FEMA NRI property purchase rules](/blog/fema-nri-property-purchase-india-rules) to understand the full framework of what is permitted.

To repatriate funds, your bank in India will strictly require:
1.  **Form 15CB:** A certificate from a Chartered Accountant certifying that the tax has been paid or adequately provided for. The CA will verify the Sale Deed, the TDS certificates (Form 16A), and your bank statement.
2.  **Form 15CA:** An online declaration by you (the NRI) based on the 15CB. This is your formal request to the bank to remit the funds.

Without these, no Indian bank will allow the outward remittance of property sale proceeds. I’ve seen transactions stall for weeks because the seller didn't have their tax residency certificate (from Canada/UK/USA) or old purchase deeds ready for the CA to sign off on the 15CB.

## The NRO Account: Your mandatory transit point

Under no circumstances can the buyer of your Mohali property transfer money directly to your foreign bank account or your NRE account. The sale proceeds must land in an **NRO (Non-Resident Ordinary)** account.

From the NRO account, you can repatriate up to **USD 1 million per financial year**. This limit includes the sale of up to two residential properties. If your exit from a luxury SCO on Airport Road or a large industrial plot near Rajpura exceeds this, you’ll need specific RBI permissions, though the USD 1 million limit is usually sufficient for 95% of the residential transactions I handle in Sector 82A or Mullanpur. ## Inherited Property: A Special Note for the Diaspora

Many NRIs in the UK and Canada are currently selling "kothi" (houses) or plots in Mohali that they inherited from their parents. The tax rules here are favorable but documentation-heavy.

*   **Cost of Acquisition:** You inherit the "cost" from the previous owner. If your father bought the plot in 1985 for ?50,000, we use the Fair Market Value (FMV) as of April 1, 2001, as your starting point for indexation.
*   **Holding Period:** Your holding period includes the time your father held the property. So, even if the property was transferred to your name last month, it is still a "Long-Term" asset if the family held it for over 2 years.
*   **Mutation:** Ensure the [property mutation (intkal)](/blog/property-mutation-intkal-mohali-process) is in your name before you sign an agreement to sell. You cannot sell a property based on a "Will" alone; the revenue records must show you as the owner.

## Common pitfalls for NRI sellers in Mohali

In my 10+ years across the [Mohali real estate guide](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026) landscape, I’ve seen three recurring mistakes that lead to legal or financial stress:

1.  **Accepting Cash ("Black Money"):** Never accept a portion of the payment in cash. As an NRI, you cannot repatriate cash. Every rupee must be "white" and reflected in the Sale Deed. If the buyer insists on a "cash component" to save on their stamp duty, walk away. It is a trap that leaves your money stuck in India.
2.  **Missing Old Records:** If you bought your property 15 years ago, you need the original allotment letter and payment receipts to calculate indexation accurately. If these are missing, the tax department might default your purchase price to zero or a very low value, leading to a massive, unnecessary tax bill.
3.  **POA Complications:** If you aren't flying to Mohali for the registry, your Power of Attorney (POA) must be meticulously drafted. A generic POA often gets rejected by banks for repatriation purposes. ## FAQ: Your NRI Tax Questions Answered

### What is the TDS rate for NRIs selling property in 2026?
For long-term capital gains (property held > 2 years), the TDS rate is 22.66%. For short-term gains, it is 30% plus applicable surcharges (which can be 10%, 15%, 25%, or 37% depending on the gain amount) plus 4% cess.

### Can an NRI save capital gains tax by reinvesting in Mohali?
Yes, under Section 54, you can exempt capital gains by purchasing another residential property in India within one year before or two years after the sale, or constructing one within three years. However, the new property must also be in India; you cannot use the proceeds to buy a house in Toronto or London and claim tax exemption in India.

### How long does it take to get a Lower Deduction Certificate?
In the Mohali/Chandigarh jurisdiction, it typically takes 30 to 50 days from the date of a complete application. It is vital to apply as soon as the "Agreement to Sell" is signed and not wait until the registry date.

### Is the TDS deducted on the profit or the total sale price?
By default, the buyer is legally required to deduct TDS on the **total sale consideration** (the total price), not just the profit. This is the biggest pain point for NRIs. The only way to change this is by obtaining a Lower Deduction Certificate.

### Can I move the sale proceeds directly to my NRE account?
No. Sale proceeds from property must be credited to an NRO account first. Once the taxes are cleared and Form 15CA/CB are filed, you can then transfer them to an NRE account or your foreign account.

### What if I have multiple properties to sell in one year?
The USD 1 million repatriation limit applies to the total amount you move out of India in a financial year (April to March). If you sell two properties in Mohali for ?10 crore total, you can move approximately ?8.3 crore (USD 1M) this year and the balance in the next financial year.

### Do I need to file an Income Tax Return (ITR) in India after selling?
Yes. Even if TDS has been deducted, you must file an ITR in India for the year you sold the property. This is where you finalize your tax liability and claim any refund if the TDS was higher than the actual tax.
