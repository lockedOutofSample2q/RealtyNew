# Sector 82A Mohali 2026: Price History, What Drove Appreciation, and What's Still Worth Buying

Sector 82A in Mohali has established itself as the gold standard for high-intent HNI investment in the Tricity. As of early 2026, property prices in Sector 82A have seen a staggering appreciation, with commercial units on the JLPL Airport Road corridor moving from ₹3–4 crore a few years ago to a current trading range of ₹12–16 crore. This growth is anchored by three primary infrastructure triggers: the massive expansion of the Mohali International Airport, the maturation of the IT City corridor, and the strategic connectivity provided by the Bharatmala project. For investors, the core question is whether the entry price today still offers room for growth. The answer is yes, but only for those who look beyond the headline commercial SCOs and focus on the undervalued industrial-category plots and emerging secondary corridors within the sector that are still priced for vision-investing.

## The Price Trajectory of Sector 82A: 2019 to 2026

To understand where Sector 82A is going, we must look at where it came from. Having managed project approvals and transactions in this belt for over a decade, I’ve watched this sector transform from a peripheral industrial-commercial mix into the most sought-after institutional and commercial hub in Punjab.

In 2019, the average price for commercial SCOs in the JLPL blocks of Sector 82 was in the range of ₹50,000 to ₹65,000 per square yard. Fast forward to 2026, and those same units, specifically those with direct frontage on the 200-foot Airport Road, are commanding upwards of ₹1.5 lakh to ₹2 lakh per square yard depending on the depth and frontage width.

Residential prices have followed a similar, albeit slightly more moderated, curve. Luxury flats that were launching at ₹4,500 per square foot in 2020 are now seeing resale values north of ₹8,500 per square foot. The [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026) provides a broader context for this growth, but Sector 82A remains the primary outlier in terms of percentage gains.

## What Drove the Exponential Appreciation in Sector 82A?

Many advisors talk about "location," but in Sector 82A, location is a result of infrastructure, not just a dot on a map. My experience on the developer side, handling land acquisition and documentation for approvals, taught me to look for the "trigger events" before they hit the headlines.

### 1. The Airport Expansion and International Connectivity
The Mohali International Airport is no longer just a regional facility; it is a primary cargo and passenger hub for North India. This has created a captive demand for corporate offices, transit hotels, and high-end retail in Sector 82A. When businesses want a "Mohali address" that is accessible to international clients, this is where they look.

### 2. The IT Corridor and Corporate Absorption
The proximity to IT City has driven a different class of buyer. We aren't just seeing local traders; we are seeing MNCs and high-growth startups absorbing large-format office spaces. This shift from "mom-and-pop" retail to corporate leasing has stabilized rental yields and pushed capital values higher. As noted in my [Mohali real estate investment analysis 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-investment-analysis-2026), the transition from yield-based assets to capital-gain assets is a hallmark of this sector.

### 3. The Bharatmala Project and Highway Connectivity
The Bharatmala road connectivity, linking the Airport Road to the Patiala highway and beyond, has turned Sector 82A into a logistical and commercial junction. This isn't just about local traffic; it’s about becoming a gateway for the entire Malwa belt of Punjab.

## What Remains Undervalued in Sector 82A in 2026?

While the Airport Road frontage is reaching a price plateau, there are pockets within Sector 82A that remain significantly undervalued. As an advisor who has navigated regulatory bodies like GMADA and the Industrial Departments, I see opportunities where others see "labels."

### 1. Industrial-Category Plots with Commercial Potential
There are several industrial plots in Sector 82A that are currently in a "grey zone" of transition. With the FAR (Floor Area Ratio) increases across Mohali, these plots offer a much higher built-up potential than current valuations reflect. Buyers who can navigate the [mutation and transfer process](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026) correctly can unlock massive value here.

### 2. Internal Secondary Corridors
The "second line" of plots—those not directly on the Airport Road but within 200 meters of it—are currently trading at a 40% discount to frontage prices. However, as the main road saturates, the footfall and corporate demand will inevitably spill over into these internal corridors. This is where the next 2x growth will come from.

### 3. Sector 82A vs. Sector 76/77 Price Gap
Currently, there is a noticeable price gap between Sector 82A and the [Sector 76 and 77 belt](/blog/sector-76-77-mohali-property-2026). While 82A is the "premium" choice, the overflow of demand is making the border areas of 82A extremely attractive for investors looking for a ₹2–4 crore entry point. For a complete comparison of how this fits into the broader regional growth, refer to our [Mohali sector-by-sector property guide 2026](/blog/mohali-sector-property-guide-2026).

## Why Developer Due Diligence is Critical in Sector 82A

Because the stakes are high in this sector, the risk of a "bad buy" is equally high. I have seen HNIs lose significant capital by buying into "pre-launch" projects where the developer had not even secured the Forest Department NOC or the PSPCL load sanction.

In my years as a developer and RERA company director, I learned that a project is only as good as its documentation. Before you put ₹10 crore into an SCO, you must run through our [complete developer due diligence checklist](/blog/mohali-developer-project-due-diligence-checklist). ## Frequently Asked Questions about Sector 82A Property Prices

### Is it too late to invest in Sector 82A Mohali?
No, but the "easy money" phase of buying anything and watching it double is over. In 2026, you must be surgical. Focus on the expansion corridors and industrial-to-commercial conversion opportunities rather than just buying at peak frontage prices.

For prime SCOs, the rental yield is currently between 4% and 6%. However, for "vision" investors who bought early, the yield on their original investment is often in the 12% to 15% range. This is why entry timing matters more than the property type.
