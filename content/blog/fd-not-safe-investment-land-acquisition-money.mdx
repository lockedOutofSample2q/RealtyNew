# Fixed Deposit Is Not a Safe Investment for Land Acquisition Money — Here's the Math

Fixed deposits are not a safe investment for land acquisition payouts in Punjab because they almost always result in negative real returns after accounting for taxes and inflation. While a 7% FD interest rate looks secure: a 30% tax bracket reduces your net yield to 4.9%. With inflation averaging 6%: your purchasing power actually shrinks by 1.1% every year. For a family receiving a ₹2 crore payout: choosing an FD over growth-corridor Mohali property can cost upwards of ₹1.5 crore in lost wealth over seven years. Real safety for life-changing land money comes from reinvesting in assets that outpace inflation: such as licensed plots or luxury flats in expanding sectors. In my years of closing 180+ transactions, I have seen families become \"property poor\" by staying in the perceived safety of a bank while the city expanded around them.

## Why does a \"Safe\" FD actually lose money for Punjab land sellers?

When your land is acquired for a project like the Bharatmala Road or a new GMADA sector: the money that hits your account is likely the largest sum your family has ever seen. The natural instinct is to park it in a Fixed Deposit (FD) at the bank. It feels safe. You see a monthly interest credit. The bank manager treats you like royalty.

![Amritpal Singh discussing real estate investment over chai in Mohali office](amritpal-singh-mohali-advisory.jpg)

But as someone who is AMFI and NCFM certified in capital markets: I look at the math differently. Let us look at the numbers honestly. If you park ₹1 crore in an FD at a 7% interest rate: you expect ₹7 lakh in returns. However: because this is land acquisition money: you are likely in the highest tax bracket. After paying 30% tax: you are left with ₹4.9 lakh.

Now: consider inflation. If the cost of living (seeds: diesel: school fees: construction materials) is rising at 6% per year: your ₹1 crore needs to become ₹1.06 crore just to buy the same things next year. But your bank account only grew to ₹1.049 crore. You didn't make money: you lost 1.1% of your wealth's power. Over ten years: this \"safe\" decision erodes the legacy you built from your land.

## Is property really more secure than the bank?

For a risk-averse family: the bank feels like a fortress and property feels like a gamble. But in the [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026): we see a different story. Property in growth corridors does not just \"sit there\" like an FD. It benefits from the expansion of the city.

When we talk about safety: we must talk about \"Replacement Value.\" If you sell 5 acres of land today and put the money in an FD: can that FD interest plus the principal buy back 5 acres of similar land five years from now? In Punjab: the answer is almost always no. Land prices in expanding zones outpace bank interest significantly. By staying in an FD: you are essentially \"de-growing\" your family's landholding.

## What are the three property scenarios for land sellers?

When I advise families in our [land seller reinvestment guide punjab 2026](/blog/land-seller-reinvestment-guide-punjab-2026): I suggest looking at property through three lenses of \"safety.\"

1.  **The Pure Appreciation Play:** This is buying licensed plots in sectors where infrastructure is visible but not yet fully priced in. Think of it as \"Vision Investing.\" You aren't looking for monthly rent: you are looking for the ₹2 crore to become ₹4 crore as the Bharatmala Road or the airport expansion completes.
2.  **The Yield Play:** This is buying commercial SCOs or luxury flats in established areas. Here: you get a monthly \"salary\" (rent) that is often higher than the post-tax interest of an FD: plus you still own the land underneath.
3.  **The Hybrid Play:** A mix of both. This is often the smartest move for a large payout. You secure your lifestyle with one ready property and your future wealth with a growing plot.

## How does inflation erode land money in the bank?

Inflation is a silent thief. It does not take money out of your account: it just makes the money inside the account worth less. If you have ₹1 crore today: and a plot in Sector 82A costs ₹1 crore: you can buy it. If you put that money in an FD: seven years later you might have ₹1.4 crore. But if that same plot now costs ₹2.5 crore: you have effectively lost the ability to own that land.

For land sellers: this is a generational trap. We see families who sold land in the 1990s and kept the money in the bank. Today: that money cannot even buy a 2-bedroom flat in the same area where they once owned acres. They stayed \"safe\": and they became property-poor.

## Why is the 24-month window more important than the FD rate?

As an advisor: I often tell clients that the clock is more dangerous than the bank. Under Section 54 and 54F: you have a specific window to reinvest your capital gains to save on tax. If you leave your money in an FD for too long: you might miss this window and face a massive tax bill that wipes out years of interest in a single day.

The \"Safety\" of an FD is often a mask for procrastination. It is easier to do nothing and let the money sit. But in the [mohali real estate FAQ 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-faq-2026): we constantly remind buyers that the cost of waiting is often higher than the cost of a premium property.

## How to transition from an FD mindset to an Asset mindset?

It starts with a family meeting. You need to stop looking at the monthly interest and start looking at the total family wealth. Are we richer today than we were last year in terms of what we can buy? If the answer is no: your FD is failing you.

I have seen families move from agricultural backgrounds into Mohali real estate and flourish. They didn't do it by taking wild risks. They did it by buying GMADA-approved plots: licensed colonies: and high-demand floors where the title was clear and the growth was guaranteed by the city's expansion.
