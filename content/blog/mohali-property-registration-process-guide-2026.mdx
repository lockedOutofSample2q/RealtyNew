# Mohali Property Process & Paperwork Guide 2026: Registration, Mutation, GMADA Transfer, and Everything Between

The property registration process in Mohali for 2026 follows a structured legal path involving three primary stages: execution of the Sale Deed at the Sub-Registrar's office, the subsequent Mutation (Intkal) in revenue records, and the administrative transfer in GMADA or Municipal Committee files. To register a property, you must pay 6% stamp duty and a 1% registration fee on the circle rate or agreement value. Following the registry, mutation is mandatory to establish your legal title in the 'Jamabandi.' For plots or floors in licensed colonies, additional steps include obtaining an No Objection Certificate (NOC) and updating ownership records with the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority (GMADA). Skipping any of these administrative steps can freeze your ability to sell or secure a home loan in the future.

## What is the step-by-step property registration process in Mohali for 2026?

Navigating the Tehsildar’s office in Mohali can be intimidating if you aren't prepared. Having coordinated approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, the Forest Department, and the Municipal Committee, I have seen where most buyers trip up. The process is not just about signing a paper; it is about the sequence of documentation.

![Amritpal Singh verifying property documents for a client in Mohali](amritpal-singh-document-verification.jpg)

First, you need a solid 'Agreement to Sale' that clearly defines the payment schedule and the date of registry. Once the final payment is ready, e-stamp papers must be purchased. In Mohali, the total cost for this is 7% of the transaction value. You then book an online appointment at the Sub-Registrar’s office. On the day of the appointment, the buyer, seller, and two witnesses must be present with original Aadhaar cards and PAN cards. 

If you are buying a resale property, you must ensure the [GMADA Mohali explained guide](/blog/gmada-mohali-explained-guide) has been followed for previous transfers. Any gap in the chain of documents will lead to the Sub-Registrar refusing the registration. This is a foundational principle we cover in our [complete Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026).

## How is stamp duty and registration fee calculated in Mohali?

One of the most common questions I get at Realty Holding & Management Consultants (RHMC) is about the \"actual\" cost of closing a deal. Many buyers calculate their budget based on the property price but forget the 7% government levy. 

For 2026, the [property registration Mohali 2026 stamp duty](/blog/property-registration-mohali-2026-stamp-duty) is 6% in Punjab, and the registration fee is 1%. This is calculated on the higher of two values: the circle rate (the minimum rate set by the government for that sector) or the actual agreement value. For example, if you are buying in Sector 82A, you must check the latest notification to ensure your stamp papers are of the correct value. 

Under-valuing a property to save on stamp duty is a trap I always advise my clients to avoid. It creates issues during [selling property mohali checklist process 2026](/blog/selling-property-mohali-checklist-process-2026) and can trigger audits from the Income Tax department.

## What is the property mutation (Intkal) process and why is it critical?

The most dangerous mistake a buyer can make is thinking the process ends at the Sub-Registrar’s office. The registry is just the evidence of sale; the Mutation (Intkal) is the actual update of ownership in the government’s revenue records. 

Without mutation, you are not the owner in the eyes of the Revenue Department. This means you cannot get a [home loan mohali property 2026 best rate](/blog/home-loan-mohali-property-2026-best-rate) on that property, and you certainly cannot sell it. The [property mutation intkal mohali process](/blog/property-mutation-intkal-mohali-process) involves submitting the registered deed to the Patwari or the Municipal Committee.

I’ve seen cases where buyers sat on their registry for years without doing mutation, only to find out during a resale that the property was still in the previous owner's name. Fixing this retrospectively is a bureaucratic nightmare that involves chasing old sellers who may no longer be available.

## How to handle GMADA plot transfers and ownership updates?

If your property falls under the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority, there is an additional layer of paperwork. The [GMADA plot transfer ownership process](/blog/gmada-plot-transfer-ownership-process) is a separate administrative track. 

You need to apply for transfer permission, pay the processing fee (usually between ₹4,000 and ₹5,000), and submit a certified copy of your Sale Deed. A critical detail many miss is the mobile number update. GMADA now uses OTP-based verification for records. If the seller’s number in the GMADA system is old or inactive, your transfer will stall. 

I’ve handled numerous transfers where we had to first update the seller's records before the buyer's name could be entered. It takes persistence and a clear understanding of how the GMADA back-office functions.

## Dealing with Municipal Committee property tax and NDC

In areas like Sector 82A or the newer sectors, the Municipal Committee handles the property tax and 'No Dues Certificate' (NDC). This is where things get complicated, especially in multi-floor buildings.

I recall a specific case, which we call the Property Tax MOU case. A client bought a ground floor in a multi-storey building where the seller hadn't paid property tax for years. When the buyer tried to clear it, the system showed a collective liability for the whole building. We had to coordinate with all floor owners, draft an MOU to allocate liability, and generate separate property IDs for each floor. It took three months of persistent coordination with the Municipal Committee to get a clean NDC.

Always ensure you have a separate property ID. This is covered in detail in our [Mohali real estate FAQ 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-faq-2026).

## How do you get a separate electricity connection (PSPCL)?

If you are buying a floor, do not rely on a shared electricity meter. It is a recipe for future disputes. The [separate electricity connection mohali floor PSPCL](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-floor-pspcl) process requires you to apply for a separate 5kW load (or more, depending on your needs).

In my experience navigating PSPCL approvals, having your own meter is a massive trust signal when you eventually decide to sell. We recently helped a client, Sandeep, who was facing shared meter issues. We managed the load sanction and separate meter installation, which cleared the path for his property's compliance and eventually made his resale much smoother.

## Why the FAR increase in Mohali matters for your paperwork

The Punjab government has recently allowed an [FAR increase mohali 2026 floor area ratio](/blog/far-increase-mohali-2026-floor-area-ratio). This means you might be able to build more on your plot than previously allowed. However, this requires a map revision approval from the Municipal Committee. 

If you build without this updated approval, you will never get an NDC or a completion certificate. This makes the property technically \"unauthorized\" for bank loans. Always align your paperwork with the latest building bylaws before starting any construction.

## Possession day checklist: What should you verify?

The day you get the keys is the day your paperwork must be at its sharpest. Do not sign the possession letter blindly. Use our [Mohali property possession day checklist](/blog/mohali-property-possession-day-checklist) to ensure you aren't inheriting someone else's problems.

You must verify the Occupation Certificate (OC). If a builder is offering possession without an OC, they are acting illegally, and you will face issues with electricity and water connections. Document every snag — every crack, every leaking pipe — with photos and videos before you accept the keys.
