# Luxury Flats vs Residential Plots in Mohali: Which Has Given Better Returns Since 2018?

If you are looking for the shortest possible answer to whether luxury flats or residential plots have performed better in Mohali since 2018, here it is: Residential plots in growth corridors have delivered higher pure capital appreciation, often exceeding 150% in seven years, while luxury flats have provided a balanced return of 80-100% appreciation plus a consistent 2-3% annual rental yield. For an HNI investor looking for maximum wealth creation over a 5-year horizon, a GMADA plot in an expansion zone like Sector 82A or the IT City belt has consistently outperformed the luxury high-rise. However, for those seeking immediate cash flow and a managed lifestyle asset, luxury flats in established Airport Road projects remain the more practical choice. The decision hinges on whether you value the scarcity of land or the utility and rental income of a built-up structure.

## Why the "Land is Better" Argument Isn't Always True in Mohali?

In traditional Punjabi investment circles, land is king. I hear this every single day in my office at Sector 82A. The logic is simple: they aren't making any more land. While this is fundamentally true, the real estate market in Mohali has matured to a point where "land" is no longer a monolithic category.

Having managed project approvals across PUDA, PSPCL, and the Municipal Committee, I have seen how the utility of a luxury flat can sometimes outweigh the raw potential of a plot. Since 2018, the Mohali market has shifted from being a "plot-only" investor market to a "lifestyle-driven" market. 

A plot is a passive asset. It sits there, it appreciates, and you pay minimal property tax. But a luxury flat in a gated community provides security, amenities, and a rental cheque that hits your account every month. For a corporate CXO or an NRI, the "returns" on a flat include the peace of mind that someone else is maintaining the common areas. In a plot, you are responsible for keeping it clear of encroachments and debris. 

The returns on a plot are almost entirely "back-ended" — you only see the money when you sell. With a luxury flat, you get "front-ended" returns through rent, which, when reinvested, can significantly narrow the gap between flat and plot appreciation.

## The Numbers: Appreciation Data Since 2018

Let’s look at the actual data points from transactions I have personally closed or witnessed in the Mohali corridors. 

In 2018-2019, premium residential plots in sectors like 82A or near the JLPL corridor were available at roughly ₹35,000 to ₹45,000 per square yard. By early 2026, those same plots are trading at ₹1.10 lakh to ₹1.35 lakh per square yard. That is a 200% to 250% jump in roughly seven years. 

Compare this to luxury flats. A premium 3BHK or 4BHK flat on Airport Road that was launched at ₹1.20 crore in 2018 is now valued at approximately ₹2.40 crore to ₹2.60 crore in the resale market. This is a 100% to 115% appreciation.

While the plot wins on pure percentage, the flat provides liquidity and cash flow. If you are an HNI with a ₹5 crore surplus, putting all of it into a single plot might give you a higher paper value in 2030, but it won't pay for your children's school fees today.

## Rental Yield vs Maintenance: The Hidden Cost of Luxury Flats

When people talk about returns, they often forget the "holding cost." This is where the comparison gets surgical.

A luxury flat in Mohali currently commands a maintenance charge of ₹4 to ₹8 per square foot. If you own a 3,000 sq. ft. luxury 4BHK, you are paying roughly ₹15,000 to ₹24,000 every month just to keep the lights on and the pool clean. Over five years, that is ₹9 lakh to ₹14 lakh out of your pocket. 

Then there is property tax. While the Municipal Committee has specific rules for constructed buildings, the tax on a luxury flat is significantly higher than on a vacant plot. I have handled cases, like the Sandeep MC tax dispute, where back-dated taxes and separate ID issues can eat into your net profit if not managed correctly.

However, the flat fights back with rental yield. In 2026, a well-located luxury flat on Airport Road can earn you ₹60,000 to ₹1.25 lakh per month in rent. This 2-3% yield acts as a hedge against inflation. A vacant plot earns zero rent. In fact, if you try to rent out a plot for commercial use like a nursery or a warehouse, you might run into GMADA licensing issues.

## Liquidity: Which Asset Exits Faster When You Need the Cash?

Liquidity is the "secret" return that most brokers never mention. In Mohali, the liquidity of a residential plot is generally higher than that of a luxury flat, provided the plot is GMADA-allotted or in a high-demand licensed colony.

Why? Because a plot is a "raw material." A buyer can build their own dream house, a builder can build floors to sell, or an investor can hold it with zero recurring cost. The buyer pool for a plot includes end-users, small-scale developers, and long-term HNIs. 

A luxury flat, on the other hand, is a "finished product." You are looking for a specific type of buyer who likes that specific layout, that specific floor, and that specific developer's maintenance style. In a sluggish market, a plot can be sold in 15-30 days by dropping the price by 2-3%. A luxury flat might take 3-6 months to find the right buyer even at a competitive price.

However, there is a caveat. In sectors where the "expansion principle" has played out — areas that have reached their full construction potential — plots can become illiquid because the entry price becomes too high for most buyers. This is why understanding where a sector sits in its growth cycle is critical. I explain this in detail in my [Mohali sector property guide 2026](/blog/mohali-sector-property-guide-2026).

## The Expansion Principle: How Location Type Changes the Answer

In my years of navigating project approvals and closures, I have identified what I call the "Expansion Principle." If a sector has room to grow, land (plots) will always outperform built-up space (flats). 

Take Sector 82A or the upcoming corridors near the Bharatmala Road. These are expansion zones. Every new office building, every new warehouse, and every new school added to the vicinity increases the value of the underlying land. Because you can't "manufacture" more land in these specific pockets, the scarcity drive prices up exponentially.

In contrast, in settled areas like the older phases of Mohali or Chandigarh-adjacent sectors, the appreciation has flattened. In these zones, a luxury flat might actually be a better investment because you are buying into an established lifestyle that has a "rent-ability" floor.

## When should you choose a luxury flat over a plot?

Investment is personal. As an advisor who has seen the developer side and the buyer side, I recommend a luxury flat in three specific scenarios:

1. **The NRI Buyer:** If you are sitting in Toronto or London, managing a plot in Mohali is a headache. You need someone to check it every month. A flat in a gated community is "locked and forget." The developer handles the security, and the rent is auto-credited to your NRO account.
2. **The High-Yield Investor:** If you are a CXO looking to offset a home loan or generate a monthly surplus to fund other investments, the rental yield of a flat is indispensable.
3. **The 3-Year Exit:** If you are looking to park money for a short duration, a ready-to-move luxury flat in a premium project has a predictable exit price. You aren't gambling on infrastructure growth as much as you are on the current demand for quality housing.

## When is a residential plot the superior investment?

A plot is the winner if you are looking for pure wealth creation and have a horizon of 5 to 10 years. It is the superior choice for:

1. **The HNI Legacy Builder:** If you want to build a family home or hold an asset that will appreciate 3x or 4x over a decade, land is the only answer.
2. **The "Vision Investor":** If you can see where the next Airport Road or the next IT City will be, buying a plot today is like buying a startup's equity before it goes public. The upside is uncapped.
3. **The Tax-Efficient Investor:** For land sellers who have received an acquisition payout, reinvesting in a residential plot is often the most straightforward way to manage capital gains under Section 54F, provided they intend to build or meet the 24-month window.
