# How to Get a Separate Electricity Connection for Your Floor in a Multi-Storey Mohali Property

To obtain a separate electricity connection for an individual floor in Mohali, you must apply to the Punjab State Power Corporation Limited (PSPCL) via their online consumer portal. The process involves submitting a registered sale deed or allotment letter to verify ownership, an Aadhar card, and a load sanction request: typically 5kW for a standard 3BHK residential floor. After paying the Security Consumption and Security Meter (SC/SM) fees, a field inspection is conducted by a Junior Engineer (JE) to verify wiring and load feasibility. An independent meter is essential for obtaining a No Dues Certificate (NDC) during resale and ensures your utility liability is decoupled from other owners in the building. Having personally managed project approvals across PSPCL, PUDA, and the Forest Department, I know that getting this paperwork right early is the difference between a functional asset and a legal headache.

## Why is a separate meter essential for Mohali floor owners?

When you buy an independent floor in sectors like 82A, 76, or 77, the building often starts with a single high-load connection used by the developer during construction. If you do not transition to a separate meter in your own name, you remain tethered to a collective bill. This is not just an administrative nuisance; it is a significant financial risk.

In my years of navigating project approvals and handling [Mohali property registration](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026), I have seen many buyers ignore this until they decide to sell. At the time of resale, the buyer's bank will demand an NDC (No Dues Certificate) from the Municipal Committee. If your electricity bill is shared and there is a dispute on any floor, your NDC will be blocked. A separate meter ensures that your account with the state is clean, independent, and ready for transfer.

## What documents do you need for a new PSPCL connection?

The paperwork for PSPCL is precise. They do not accept blurred copies or incomplete sets; they require verified documentation. Based on my experience coordinating with PSPCL sub-divisions in Mohali, here is what you must have ready:

1.  **Proof of Ownership:** A certified copy of your Registered Sale Deed or the Allotment Letter from GMADA/PUDA.
2.  **Identity Proof:** Aadhar Card is the standard. Ensure the name matches the Sale Deed exactly.
3.  **No Objection Certificate (NOC):** If the building is still under the developer's name, you need an NOC from the developer.
4.  **Test Report:** A certificate from a licensed electrical contractor certifying that the internal wiring of your floor is safe and meets the requested load.
5.  **Photograph:** Recent passport-size photograph of the applicant.

## What is the step-by-step process for application?

The process has moved significantly online, but on-ground follow-up remains a reality of the Punjab real estate landscape. 

*   **Step 1: Online Registration:** Visit the PSPCL Consumer Portal and register using your mobile number. Select 'New Connection'.
*   **Step 2: Load Selection:** For most 3BHK floors in Mohali, a 5kW load is sufficient. If you have multiple heavy appliances, consider 7kW.
*   **Step 3: Fee Payment:** Once the application is submitted, you will receive a demand note for security deposits. These fees are regulated and based on the kilowatt load requested.
*   **Step 4: Field Inspection:** A Junior Engineer (JE) from the local PSPCL office will visit the site. They check if the meter box location is accessible and if the load requested matches the wiring.
*   **Step 5: Meter Installation:** Post-approval, the meter is usually installed within 7 to 15 days.

I recall a case involving a client, Sandeep, who purchased a ground floor in a multi-storey building. While the upper floor owners were content using a sub-meter connected to the main line, Sandeep followed my advice and insisted on a separate 5kW connection immediately after his [property mutation](/blog/property-mutation-intkal-mohali-process).

Because Sandeep had his own independent meter and account ID with PSPCL, his power remained unaffected. ## Sub-meter vs. Direct PSPCL Connection: A Financial Comparison

Many investors ask me why they should spend ₹25,000 to ₹35,000 on a new connection when they can just install a ₹2,000 sub-meter. The answer lies in the slab-based billing system of Punjab. PSPCL uses a progressive tariff where the cost per unit increases as your consumption crosses specific thresholds. 

When three floors share one main meter, the total consumption often hits the highest tariff slab within the first 10 days of the month. Even if you use a sub-meter to split the bill, you are likely paying for every single unit at the highest possible rate. In contrast, with a separate connection, each floor gets its own fresh start at the lowest slab. Over a 12-month period, the saving on electricity bills often exceeds the initial cost of the connection. For an NRI landlord managing a property remotely, this also eliminates the monthly calculation friction with tenants.

## The Liaisoning Reality: Navigating the PSPCL Sub-Division

Having managed these approvals across various Punjab government regulatory bodies: including PUDA, the Forest Department, and the Municipal Committee: I can tell you that the PSPCL office in Mohali operates on a document-first principle. 

Liaisoning is not about shortcuts; it is about anticipation. For example, the Junior Engineer is not just looking at your meter; they are looking at the Distribution Transformer (DT) capacity in your area. If the transformer is overloaded, your connection might be delayed until a capacity upgrade is approved. This is why I always check the local infrastructure status before a client commits to a high-load requirement. When we represent a client at the sub-division office, we ensure the Service Connection Order (SCO) is issued promptly. Tracking the file from the Revenue Superintendent to the JE is what reduces the timeline from 45 days to under 15 days.

## Technical Specifications: The Meter Box and Wire Gauge

PSPCL Mohali has strict guidelines on where the meter is placed. It must be at the entry point of the property. For multi-floor buildings, there should be a centralized meter cabinet on the ground floor. 

I have seen possession delays in projects simply because the developer used 4sq mm wire instead of the required 6sq mm or 10sq mm for the main incoming line. When the JE visits for inspection and finds the wire gauge insufficient for the sanctioned load, the file is returned with a technical deficiency note. As a buyer, always verify the electrical map of your floor against the PSPCL requirements before the walls are plastered. It saves you from the nightmare of tearing down a finished wall to upgrade a conduit.

## Common Reasons for PSPCL Application Rejection

Rejections usually happen due to technicalities, not lack of merit. Having navigated these approvals personally, I have seen three recurring issues:

1.  **Name Mismatch:** If the name on your Aadhar does not match the Sale Deed exactly, the JE will flag it.
2.  **Unpaid Dues on Land:** If the developer or the previous owner has unpaid bills on the parent connection for that plot, PSPCL will refuse new connections until the old dues are cleared.
3.  **Inaccessible Meter Box:** In Mohali, PSPCL requires the meter to be outside the main gate or in a common area where the reader can access it without entering the house.
