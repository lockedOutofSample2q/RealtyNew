## Pre-Requisites for PSPCL Application in Mohali

Before initiating the online application on the PSPCL My Account portal, ensure you have the following documentation ready. PSPCL is strict about documentation, and any discrepancy between the name on the property title and the applicant's ID can lead to immediate rejection.

### 1. Proof of Ownership
You must provide a registered Sale Deed, an Allotment Letter from GMADA, or a Conveyance Deed. If the property is still under an agreement to sell, a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from the developer or the original allottee might be required, though PSPCL primarily prefers registered ownership documents.

### 2. The Test Report (B-Form)
This is a technical document issued by a licensed electrical contractor. It certifies that the internal wiring of your floor is safe and complies with PSPCL standards. It includes a detailed list of points (lights, fans, ACs, geysers) and the total calculated load in Kilowatts (KW).

### 3. Identity and Address Proof
Aadhaar Cards are mandatory for individual applicants. For NRIs owning floors in Mohali, a copy of the passport and a local representative's details may be necessary during the physical verification stage.

### 4. Sanctioned Building Plan
PSPCL often requires the sanctioned building plan to verify that the floor is a legal construction. This is particularly relevant in areas like Sector 123 to 125 (Sunny Enclave) or other private developer colonies where unauthorized constructions are occasionally flagged.

## Step-by-Step Guide to the PSPCL Application Process

The Punjab government has streamlined the process through the "Single Window" system, but the execution still requires meticulous follow-up.

### Step 1: Online Registration and A&A Form
Visit the official PSPCL website and navigate to the "New Connection" section. You will need to fill out the Application and Agreement (A&A) form. Select the "Domestic Supply" (DS) category for residential floors. Ensure the address matches the Registry exactly.

### Step 2: Load Calculation
For a standard 3BHK floor in Mohali, the load usually ranges between 5KW to 10KW. If you plan to install multiple 2-ton air conditioners or electric vehicle (EV) charging points, ensure your sanctioned load reflects this. Underestimating the load can lead to frequent tripping and penalties during PSPCL audits.

### Step 3: Payment of Security and Initial Charges
Once the application is submitted, you will receive a demand notice or an online payment link. This includes:
* **Security (Consumption):** A refundable deposit based on the sanctioned load.
* **Security (Meter):** The cost of the meter equipment.
* **Service Connection Charges:** Costs for the cable and infrastructure.

### Step 4: Physical Verification by the Junior Engineer (JE)
This is the most critical stage of liaisoning. The JE from the local Mohali subdivision (e.g., Phase 7 or Phase 1 division) will visit the property. They check:
* The availability of a main distribution board.
* The presence of an Earth Leakage Circuit Breaker (ELCB).
* Whether the meter box is accessible from the outside (a mandatory requirement in newer Mohali sectors).

### Step 5: Meter Installation
After the JE's report is uploaded to the system, the SDO (Sub-Divisional Officer) approves the release of the connection. A technician will then install the physical meter and seal it.

## Technical Considerations: KW vs. kVA

While most domestic users deal in Kilowatts (KW), larger multi-storey units with a sanctioned load above 20KW are often billed on kVA (Kilo-Volt-Ampere) basis, which includes power factor considerations. For a single floor owner, staying within the KW limit is usually more straightforward. However, if you are the developer of the entire stilt plus 4 building, you must ensure the "Total Connected Load" of the building is sanctioned with the local subdivision to avoid heavy fines once all residents move in.

## Common Reasons for Application Rejection

1. **Incomplete Wiring:** If the internal wiring is not complete up to the meter board, the JE will reject the report.
2. **Arrears on the Plot:** If the parent plot had an old connection with unpaid bills, PSPCL will not release new connections for individual floors until the dues are cleared.
3. **Mismatched Documents:** Differences in names between the Aadhaar card and the Sale Deed.
4. **Encroachment:** If the meter box is placed on public land or a GMADA green belt area, the connection will be denied.
