# Prelaunch Property in Mohali: The Real Risk-Reward Calculation With Actual Numbers

Is buying a prelaunch property in Mohali worth the risk? The short answer is yes, but only if the price discount is at least 15–20% below the current market rate for ready-to-move-in inventory in the same micro-market. For a prelaunch project to be a "rewarding" investment, the entry price must compensate you for the 36–48 month construction timeline, potential developer default, and approval uncertainties. In Mohali’s current 2026 market, a successful prelaunch play typically involves a 25% upfront payment with a construction-linked plan that ensures your capital is deployed only as the structure rises. If the "prelaunch discount" is merely 5% or if the developer demands 50% upfront before RERA registration, the risk far outweighs the reward.

## What is the actual economics behind a Mohali prelaunch price?

When a developer offers you a "prelaunch" rate, they aren't doing you a favour. They are looking for interest-free capital to fund initial land payments and approval costs. From my time on the developer side, I can tell you exactly how this is calculated. A developer’s cost of capital from banks is typically 12–18%. By offering you a discount, they are "borrowing" from you at a lower rate while de-risking the project by showing "sales velocity" to lenders. If the market rate in Sector 82A is ₹8,500 per sq. ft., and a developer offers a prelaunch at ₹6,800, that ₹1,700 gap is your "risk premium."

However, many buyers forget the "all-in" cost. You must add EDC, IDC, PLC, and GST. In many cases, I’ve seen buyers enter a prelaunch thinking they got a steal, only to realize later that after 4 years of waiting and paying loan interest, their "discounted" property costs the same as a ready unit did 4 years ago. ## Why do Sector 82A and Sector 76 show such different prelaunch trajectories?

In **Sector 82A**, a luxury project launched its prelaunch phase in 2022 at a base price of ₹5,200 per sq. ft. At that time, ready inventory was trading at ₹6,500. By 2024, the developer’s price climbed to ₹7,800. Today, as they approach possession in 2026, resale is touching ₹10,500. The early buyer saw a 100% appreciation on their base price, despite two years of approval delays. 

In contrast, a **Sector 76** project launched at ₹4,800 when the market was ₹5,500. Four years later, it’s only at ₹6,200. Factor in the 5% GST and 7% stamp duty, and that buyer has barely beaten a Fixed Deposit. The difference? The first was in an [expansion corridor](/blog/mohali-property-appreciation-vs-stagnation-zones) where vision investing paid off. The second was a "me-too" project with no unique value.

## What are the three non-negotiable risks you take with a prelaunch?

Before signing that allotment letter, be blunt about the risks:

1.  **Approval Risk:** A "prelaunch" often means a project is still obtaining CLU or RERA registration. If GMADA raises an objection, your money is stuck. I've seen an 8-month plot recovery start because of a surprise cancellation.
2.  **Payment Plan Risk:** If a developer asks for 50% upfront, walk away. They are solving their liquidity crisis with your money. A safe prelaunch always follows a construction-linked plan. Check our [buyer protection guide](/blog/mohali-property-buyer-protection-guide) for safe structures.
3.  **Developer Default Risk:** In Mohali, local developers often launch three projects at once, using Project C's funds to finish Project A. If Project C's sales slow, Project A stalls.

## What does RERA Punjab actually protect you from in a prelaunch?

A developer cannot legally market or sell before RERA registration. Pre-booking is a grey area. If you pay before a RERA number is issued, you are an "unsecured creditor." Once registered, the developer must put 70% of funds into an escrow account, preventing fund diversion. If a project lacks a RERA number, you are "speculating," not "investing." For [prelaunch pricing calculations](/blog/prelaunch-pricing-mohali-real-cost-calculation), always wait for the RERA number to appear on the portal.

## When does a prelaunch project actually make sense?

Despite risks, prelaunch is where wealth is created. It makes sense in three scenarios: the expansion argument (first mover in a growth corridor near PR-7), the branded premium (developer with a 100% delivery record), or the liquidity play (locking in today's price while paying over 3 years from cash flow). In these cases, the reward justifies the construction wait.
