# Rental Yield Reality in Mohali 2026: What ₹3 Crore Worth of Property Actually Earns Per Month

In Mohali’s 2026 real estate market, a ₹3 crore investment generates vastly different monthly returns depending on the asset class you choose. For a high-end luxury flat, you should realistically expect a rental yield of 2% to 3%, translating to ₹50,000 to ₹75,000 per month. If you shift that same ₹3 crore into a commercial SCO (Shop-cum-Office), the yield climbs to 4%–6%, or ₹1 lakh to ₹1.5 lakh monthly. Independent floors sit in the middle at 3%–4% (₹75,000–₹1 lakh), while industrial sheds in emerging corridors like the Rajpura belt offer the highest yields at 6%–8%, potentially earning you up to ₹2 lakh per month. While appreciation often grabs the headlines, these rental figures represent the "hard cash" reality that sustains long-term portfolios.

When you are sitting in a CXO suite or managing a large business, you don’t look at property as just "bricks and mortar." You look at it as a capital allocation decision. Is your money working harder here than it would in a Fixed Deposit or a balanced equity fund? To answer that, we have to look past the developer brochures and into the actual bank statements of owners across Sector 82A, Aerocity, and the Airport Road.

## What is the realistic rental yield for luxury flats in Mohali?

If you are buying a premium 3BHK or 4BHK in a gated community with a ₹3 crore price tag, you are primarily buying for lifestyle and capital appreciation, not pure yield. The math is simple but often sobering for first-time investors. In the current market, these units rent for roughly ₹50,000 to ₹75,000. 

After you factor in the annual maintenance charges (which can be ₹5–₹8 per square foot), property tax, and the occasional vacancy, your "net" yield often hovers around 2.2%. This is standard for luxury residential globally. The value here isn't the monthly cheque; it’s the quality of the tenant (often corporate executives or NRIs) and the fact that these properties in growth corridors like the JLPL belt or IT City tend to appreciate faster than smaller, high-yield assets.

## Why do Commercial SCOs offer 4–6% yield?

Commercial property in Mohali is a different beast entirely. A ₹3 crore investment might get you a mid-sized SCO or a large commercial floor in a developing sector. The yield is higher—usually 4% to 6%—because you are serving businesses, not families. 

On Airport Road, we’ve seen commercial units that were bought for ₹3–4 crore a few years ago now commanding rentals of ₹1 lakh per month or more. However, the risk here is vacancy. A residential flat is easy to rent out in 30 days. A commercial unit might sit empty for six months while you wait for the "right" brand or bank to sign a 9-year lease. But once they do, the escalations (usually 15% every three years) make the yield much more attractive than residential.

## Are independent floors the "Middle Ground" for yield?

Independent floors in Mohali, particularly in sectors like 76, 77, or the newer GMADA plots, have become a favorite for mid-tier investors. At a ₹3 crore price point, you might be looking at multiple units or a very high-end joda-plot floor.

The yield here typically ranges from 3% to 4%. These are easier to manage than commercial units but offer better cash flow than luxury apartments. Families relocating from rural Punjab or smaller towns often prefer these over high-rises, leading to very low vacancy rates.

## What drives the 6–8% yield in Industrial property?

The biggest secret among HNI investors in Mohali right now is the industrial shed market. With the Bharatmala project and the Rajpura-Banur corridor expanding, there is a massive shortage of "Grade A" warehousing and light industrial space.

A ₹3 crore investment in an industrial shed can realistically yield ₹1.5 lakh to ₹2 lakh per month. Why? Because industrial tenants (logistics companies, manufacturing units) value utility over aesthetics. They sign long-term leases and often handle the maintenance themselves. If you are focused purely on monthly cash flow and don't care about "showing" a fancy property to your friends, this is where the smart money is moving.

## How does vacancy reality impact your "Expected" income?

One mistake I see investors make constantly is calculating yield as `(Monthly Rent * 12) / Purchase Price`. That is "Gross Yield," and it is a fiction.

In the real world, you must account for:
1. **The Gap:** Every 2-3 years, you will have a 1-month vacancy.
2. **The Brokerage:** You pay 1 month's rent to the broker to find a new tenant.
3. **The Upkeep:** Painting, repairs, and electrical work between tenants.
4. **The Tax:** Property tax in Mohali is manageable, but it exists.

When you subtract these, your 3% yield often becomes 2.5%. This is why we always emphasize looking at the [/blog/mohali-real-estate-investment-analysis-2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-investment-analysis-2026) to see the total return—appreciation PLUS yield.

## Comparing Yield: Mohali Property vs. Fixed Deposits vs. Equity

As someone with an AMFI/NCFM background, I often have to remind clients that you can't compare a 7% FD rate directly to a 3% rental yield.

| Asset Class | Typical Yield (2026) | Tax Implications | Appreciation Potential |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Fixed Deposit** | 6.5% - 7.5% | Taxed at Slab (30% for CXOs) | Zero (Inflation eats principal) |
| **Mohali Luxury Flat** | 2% - 3% | 30% standard deduction + interest offsets | High (8-12% p.a.) |
| **Mohali SCO** | 4% - 6% | Higher maintenance/tax costs | Moderate to High |
| **Industrial Shed** | 6% - 8% | Simple management | High in growth corridors |

The "Real Return" on a Mohali property is the Rent (Yield) + Price Increase (Appreciation) - Inflation. In a growth sector like Sector 82A, that total return has historically outperformed FDs by a wide margin.

## The "Airport Road" Case Study: When Vision Meets Yield

Let's talk about the Airport Road commercial trajectory. A few years back, I saw people hesitating to buy commercial units at ₹4 crore. They thought the "rent wasn't there yet." Today, those same units are not only worth ₹12 crore+, but they are also fetching ₹1 lakh to ₹1.2 lakh in monthly rent. 

The yield on their *original* investment is now over 15%. This is the power of "Vision Investing." If you wait for the yield to be high before you buy, you will pay a premium price that pushes the yield back down to the market average. To make real money, you buy where the infrastructure is coming, but the tenants haven't arrived yet.

## Who rents at these rates in Mohali?

In 2026, the tenant profile in Mohali has shifted. We no longer rely just on local demand.
- **IT Professionals:** The expansion of IT City has brought in thousands of high-earning engineers who prefer luxury rentals.
- **Corporate Guests:** MNCs are renting luxury flats as guest houses for visiting directors.
- **The "Relocation" Crowd:** Families from Ludhiana, Jalandhar, and Amritsar are moving to Mohali for better schooling and healthcare (PGIMER proximity), preferring to rent before they buy.
- **International Brands:** Retailers are now aggressively looking for SCO space in Aerocity and Sector 82 to tap into the high-net-worth crowd in the Tricity.

## The Net Yield Reality: Taxes, Maintenance, and Management

To understand what ₹3 crore actually puts in your pocket, we have to talk about the "net" yield. In my experience, most brokers will tell you a property rents for ₹1 lakh and call it a 4% yield. But as someone who has managed project approvals and property documentation for a decade, I look at the numbers after the Government and the maintenance society take their share.

In India, the Income Tax Act provides a 30% standard deduction on rental income for repairs and maintenance. This is a significant benefit for high-earning CXOs. If you are earning ₹12 lakh a year in rent from a commercial SCO, you only pay tax on ₹8.4 lakh. If you have a home loan on that property, the interest can further offset this income.

However, the out-of-pocket costs are real. Luxury projects in Mohali Sector 82A or Aerocity often have maintenance charges ranging from ₹3 to ₹8 per square foot. On a 2,500 sq. ft. luxury flat, that’s ₹1.5 lakh to ₹2 lakh gone every year before you even pay for your own repairs. When you add the 1% annual property tax and the cost of a property manager (especially if you are an NRI), your 3% gross yield is realistically a 2.1% net yield. This is why I always tell my clients: "Buy for the appreciation, but ensure the yield covers your holding costs."

## Who should (and should not) invest for yield in Mohali?

Yield-focused investing isn't for everyone. Based on the hundreds of transactions I’ve handled, here is how I break down the decision framework:

### The "Yield Hunter" (Industrial and Commercial)
If you are a retired business owner or a Land Seller who has just received a large acquisition payout, you need monthly cash flow to replace your previous income. In this case, you should avoid luxury flats. Your ₹3 crore should go into an industrial shed or a high-footfall commercial unit. You are trading off "prestige" for a steady ₹1.5 lakh monthly cheque.

### The "Growth Investor" (Luxury Residential)
If you are a CXO at an MNC with a high monthly salary, you don't actually *need* an extra ₹50,000 a month that will just get taxed at your 30% bracket. You should prioritize "Vision Investing" in upcoming corridors. You want a property that might only yield 2% today but will be worth ₹6 crore in five years. The rental income is just a bonus that pays for the property’s own upkeep.

### The "Safe Haven" Investor (Independent Floors)
If you are an NRI looking for a safe place to park capital with minimal headache, independent floors in GMADA-approved sectors are your best bet. They offer a stable 3.5% yield and are the easiest to liquidate if you ever need to pull your capital back to the UK or Canada.

## How to maximize your yield in the current market?

If you want to push your ₹3 crore investment toward the higher end of the yield bracket, follow these three rules:

1. **Frontage is Everything (Commercial):** A wider SCO frontage always commands a higher rental premium than a deep, narrow one.
2. **Furnish for the CXO (Residential):** A semi-furnished flat might rent for ₹50,000. Spend ₹15 lakh on high-end interiors, and it could jump to ₹85,000. The yield on that incremental interior spend is often 20%+.
3. **Verify the Maintenance:** High maintenance charges in "over-amenitized" projects can kill your net yield. Sometimes, a project with fewer "lifestyle" features but better location offers a better net return.
