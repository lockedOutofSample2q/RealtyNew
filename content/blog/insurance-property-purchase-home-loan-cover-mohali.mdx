# Group Insurance and Life Insurance in Real Estate: Why Your Property Purchase Should Trigger a Coverage Review

Every property purchase, especially one funded by a home loan, fundamentally changes your liability profile. If you are buying a luxury flat or an SCO in Mohali, you aren't just acquiring an asset: you are often taking on a multi-crore liability. A ₹2 Crore mortgage is a ₹2 Crore debt that your family inherits if you are no longer there to service it. An insurance review ensures that your life cover is adequate to clear the outstanding debt and that the asset itself is protected against physical risks.

In my years working within the insurance sector before moving into real estate development and advisory, I saw too many families lose their "dream home" because they treated insurance as a bank-mandated formality rather than a structural necessity. When you sign those registry papers in Mohali, your very next call should be to your insurance advisor.

## Is home loan protection insurance mandatory for Mohali property buyers?

When you sit down with a banker to finalize your [home loan for a Mohali property](/blog/home-loan-mohali-property-2026-best-rate) to finalize your [home loan for a Mohali property](/blog/home-loan-mohali-property-2026-best-rate), you will almost certainly be pitched a "Home Loan Protection Plan" (HLPP). Let me be direct: It is not legally mandatory.

Banks in Mohali push these plans because they earn significant commissions. These plans are usually Mortgage Reducing Term Assurance (MRTA) policies, where the sum assured decreases as you pay off your loan. While they serve a purpose, they are often more expensive than a standalone Term Insurance plan and offer significantly less flexibility. One major disadvantage is that the premium is often added to your loan amount, meaning you pay interest on your insurance premium for years.

## Why term insurance is often superior to the bank’s "Group Insurance" plan

As someone who understands the math behind both [capital markets](/blog/rhmc-amritpal-singh-mohali-real-estate-consultant) and insurance, I always advise my clients to look at the numbers. A standalone term insurance policy stays with you even if you transfer your loan to another bank or pay it off early. A bank-linked group plan often lapses the moment the loan is closed or moved.

Furthermore, term insurance offers a fixed sum assured. If you take a ₹2 Crore loan and buy a ₹2 Crore term plan, and something happens when the loan is down to ₹50 Lakhs, your family gets the full ₹2 Crore. They can clear the loan and still have ₹1.5 Crore for their future. With a reducing cover, they only get enough to pay the bank.

## How much should your life cover increase after a Mohali property purchase?

If you are a CXO or a senior corporate executive buying in the premium segments of Mohali, your life insurance calculation needs to be surgical. In my advisory practice, I use a simple "Liability + Need" framework. If your family requires ₹5 Crore to maintain their lifestyle and you have just taken a ₹2 Crore loan for a property in Sector 82A, your total cover needs to be ₹7 Crore. 

In the [master Mohali real estate guide](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), I talk about the appreciation of assets, but insurance is about protecting against the depreciation of your family’s financial security. Don't let a "paper asset" like a luxury flat become a "stone weight" around your family's neck.

## Does your employer's Group Term Insurance (GTI) cover your mortgage?

Many corporate professionals assume their company-provided cover is enough. It isn't. Employer-provided Group Term Insurance (GTI) is a perk, not a plan. It exists only as long as you are employed by that specific company. More importantly, for someone buying a luxury property in Mohali, a ₹1 Crore cover is often exhausted just by the children’s international education fund or the spouse’s retirement needs. Buy your own independent term cover that covers your mortgage independently.

## Property Insurance for SCOs: Commercial Risks You Cannot Ignore

If your purchase isn't a residential flat but a [commercial SCO on Airport Road](/blog/aerocity-mohali-commercial-property-2026), your insurance needs shift entirely. 

1.  **Loss of Rent Insurance:** If your SCO faces a fire and the tenant has to vacate, you lose your rental income while your loan EMIs continue. Loss of Rent insurance covers this gap.
2.  **Public Liability Insurance:** If a customer slips and falls in your SCO, you could be facing a massive legal claim. 
3.  **Plate Glass and Neon Sign Cover:** In a premium corridor like Aerocity, the glass frontage of an SCO can cost lakhs. 

## Property Insurance: Structure vs. Contents – What do you actually need?

While life insurance covers the *buyer*, property insurance covers the *brick and mortar*. In Mohali, we are seeing a surge in high-rise luxury apartments. Many buyers assume the society’s "Master Insurance" covers everything. It doesn't.

*   **Structure Insurance:** Covers the external walls and roof. 
*   **Contents Insurance:** This covers your internal fit-outs, expensive Italian marble, and high-end woodwork.
*   **Burglary and Theft:** If you are an NRI or a frequent traveler, your flat in Mohali might be empty for weeks. Contents insurance is essential.
