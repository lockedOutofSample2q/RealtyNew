# Sunny Enclave Sector 105–109 Mohali: What Buyers in This Belt Are Actually Getting in 2026

In 2026, the Sunny Enclave belt (Sectors 105 to 109) stands as the most practical entry point for buyers who want the Mohali address without the ₹2–4 crore price tag of the core city. For a Land Seller reinvesting an acquisition payout, this area offers the perfect balance of immediate livability and high resale liquidity. Currently, residential plots here trade between ₹45,000 and ₹75,000 per sq. yard, while 3BHK independent floors range from ₹65 Lakh to ₹95 Lakh. It is an established, bustling township that provides a "soft landing" for families moving from rural Punjab. While it may not offer the high-end aesthetic of the [Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), its functionality as a residential hub is unmatched in the mid-budget segment. The belt offers a genuine community feel, high density of essential services, and a price point that allows for diversification of capital—something critical for families managing life-changing acquisition wealth.

When a family sells agricultural land in areas like Banur, Rajpura, or along the Ludhiana highway, the sudden influx of ₹1 crore to ₹5 crore can be overwhelming. In my experience advising these families, the first instinct is often to buy where they feel "at home." They aren't just looking for an asset; they are looking for a transition.

Sunny Enclave Sector 105 feels like a natural extension of the Punjab heartland. Unlike the corporate-heavy environment of IT City or the high-rise culture of Sector 82A, this belt is built for horizontal living. You see wide porches, community parks filled with familiar faces, and local markets that cater to every household need from fresh produce to hardware. From a financial perspective, it’s a safe harbour. If you’ve received a payout and are following the [land seller reinvestment guide punjab 2026](/blog/land-seller-reinvestment-guide-punjab-2026), Sector 105 offers immediate utility. You can buy a plot, build a floor for yourself, and rent out the others to generate a steady monthly income that beats any FD return.

### What makes the "Sunny Enclave Psychology" different?
Most buyers here aren't looking to flip a property in six months. They are looking to park capital for 10 years. They value the fact that their neighbor is likely another family from a similar background—perhaps a retired officer or a business owner from a nearby town like Kharar or Kurali. This social cohesion makes the area resilient to market volatility. When core Mohali prices stagnate, Sunny Enclave often keeps moving because its demand is driven by people who *need* to live there, not just speculators.

## Is the connectivity to core Mohali and Chandigarh actually good?

For years, the "Sunny Enclave bottleneck" was a real deterrent. The single entry-exit point near the Kharar flyover caused massive delays. In 2026, that narrative has changed. The PR-7 (Airport Road) extension has bypassed the old Kharar congestion, linking Sector 105 directly to the IT corridor and the airport.

However, be realistic. If your office is in Chandigarh Sector 17 or the Phase 8B Industrial Area, your commute during peak hours (9:00 AM and 6:00 PM) will still involve some traffic. The advantage here is the highway access. If you have family back in Ludhiana or Jalandhar, being on this side of Mohali saves you 30 minutes of cross-city driving every single trip. For a Land Seller who still has interests in rural Punjab, this "edge of the city" location is often a feature, not a bug.

### How has the PR-7 Extension changed the game?
The PR-7 extension has essentially turned Sunny Enclave from a "suburb of Kharar" into a "sector of Mohali." You are now 20 minutes from the airport and 15 minutes from the PCA Stadium. This infrastructure shift is the primary reason why resale prices in Sector 105 have held firm while other outskirts have struggled.

As a consultant, I believe trust is built by saying "no" as often as "yes." Sector 105–109 is a fantastic residential hub, but it isn't for everyone. My job isn't to sell you a property; it's to ensure you don't buy the wrong one.

If you are an HNI (High Net Worth Individual) looking for a "gated community" feel with concierge services, clubhouses, and 24/7 manicured landscaping, you might find Sunny Enclave too "busy." This is a township, not a resort. It has the energy of a growing city—commercial shops integrated into residential blocks, bustling evening markets, and high density. ## What should you verify before signing the agreement?

Even in an established area like Sector 105, due diligence is non-negotiable. I've seen too many buyers get blinded by a "good deal" only to find out the paperwork is a mess.

### 1. Mutation (Intkal) Status
Ensure the seller's name is updated in the revenue records. I have handled cases where the registry was done, but the mutation was pending for 10 years, making the bank loan impossible for the next buyer. If the Intkal isn't clear, walk away—no matter how cheap the plot is.

### 2. NOC from Developer
Sunny Enclave is a private township. You need a clear No Objection Certificate from the developer (Bajwa Developers) to ensure all dues—including EDC (External Development Charges) and maintenance—are paid up to date. Without this, GMADA or the MC might refuse to transfer the property in your name later.

### 3. Electricity and Water Connections
Check if the individual meters are installed. In some newer pockets of Sector 108/109, developers sometimes provide a "shared" connection. This is a red flag. You want an independent PSPCL meter in your own name. I've seen how shared meters lead to disputes between floor owners during the [mohali sector property guide 2026](/blog/mohali-sector-property-guide-2026) transition.

### 4. Road Width and Parking
Brochures often show 40ft roads, but on the ground, they might be 30ft due to encroachment or planning changes. Measure it yourself. In high-density areas like Sunny Enclave, a 10ft difference in road width can affect your resale value by 15%.
