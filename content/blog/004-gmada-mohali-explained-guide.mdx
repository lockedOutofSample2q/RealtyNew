# GMADA Explained: What It Controls, What It Approves, and Why Every Mohali Buyer Must Understand It

If you are buying property in Mohali, you are essentially entering a contract with the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority (GMADA). GMADA is the apex planning and development body that controls land use, infrastructure, and colony licensing across the Mohali district. It is the authority that decides where a residential sector will be carved out, where the commercial hubs will sit, and which private developers are legally permitted to build. For a buyer, GMADA approval is the ultimate certificate of title clarity and safety. This authority is the foundation of the entire [Mohali real estate guide](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026). Without it, your property is effectively an "unauthorised colony," which means no bank loans, no registered resale value, and a permanent risk of demolition. Understanding GMADA isn't just about knowing a government department: it is about protecting your capital from the legal "grey zones" that still exist in the Punjab real estate market.

## What Exactly is GMADA and What is Its Mandate?

GMADA, or the Greater Mohali Area Development Authority, was established under the Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995. Its jurisdiction isn't limited just to Mohali city: it extends across Zirakpur, Derabassi, Kharar, Mullanpur (New Chandigarh), Banur, and even parts of Fatehgarh Sahib.

Think of GMADA as the architect and the landlord of the region. Their mandate includes:

1.  **Master Planning:** Creating the 20-year vision for the city, including the [New Chandigarh Master Plan 2031](/blog/new-chandigarh-master-plan-2031-property).
2.  **Land Acquisition:** Buying agricultural land from farmers to develop residential sectors or industrial corridors.
3.  **Infrastructure Development:** Building the 200-foot wide Airport Road, the internal sector roads, and the primary sewage and water lines.
4.  **Colony Licensing:** Issuing licenses to private developers like JLPL, Homeland, or Emaar. A developer cannot dig a single inch of soil without a GMADA-issued License to Develop a Colony (LDC).

In my years of navigating project approvals from the developer side, I have seen that GMADA is the most rigorous filter in the system. If a project has their stamp, it means the land title is clear, the Change of Land Use (CLU) is paid for, and the layout plan is sanctioned.

## GMADA vs. PUDA vs. Municipal Committee: Who Does What?

One of the most common points of confusion for NRIs and first-time buyers is the alphabet soup of Punjab's regulatory bodies. Having worked with all five major departments—PUDA, PSPCL, Forest, Municipal, and Industrial—I can tell you exactly where the boundaries lie.

| Authority | Role in Your Property | When You Deal With Them |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **PUDA** | The "Parent" body for Punjab. | Usually at the policy level or for areas outside GMADA's specific Mohali zone. |
| **GMADA** | The "Owner" and "Planner" of Mohali. | During allotment, transfer of ownership, and checking project licenses. |
| **Municipal Committee (MC)** | The "Administrator." | For property tax (NDC), building plan sanctions in specific zones, and [separate electricity connections](/blog/separate-electricity-connection-mohali-pspcl). |
| **PSPCL** | The Power Utility. | When you need a meter in your name or a load sanction. |

If you buy a plot in a GMADA-developed sector (like Sector 76–80), your "landlord" is GMADA. If you buy a flat in a private project, the developer deals with GMADA for the license, but you deal with the Municipal Committee for your property tax IDs once the building is constructed. I’ve handled cases, like the [Sandeep MC tax dispute](/blog/property-tax-dispute-multi-floor-building-mohali), where a buyer was caught between these departments because the property IDs weren't separated. Knowing which door to knock on saves months of frustration.

## How the GMADA Plot Allotment Process Works

GMADA releases residential and commercial inventory through two primary routes: Draws and Auctions.

### 1. The Draw of Lots (The Lottery)
This is typically for "Affordable" or "EWS" housing and certain residential plot schemes in new sectors. You apply with an earnest money deposit (usually 10% of the price), and if your name comes up in the computerized draw, you get the allotment. The price here is fixed by GMADA. 

### 2. The E-Auction (The Bidding War)
For prime commercial SCOs or premium residential plots, GMADA uses an e-auction portal. There is a "Reserve Price"—for example, I’ve seen 3BHK units with a reserve price of ₹1.15 Crore—and the highest bidder wins. 

From my experience in these auctions, the "hidden" cost is the payment timeline. Once you win, you have to pay 25% of the bid amount within a very short window (usually 30 days) to get the Allotment Letter. If you default, your earnest money is forfeited. I always advise my HNI and NRI clients to have their [home loan eligibility](/blog/home-loan-mohali-property-2026-best-rate) or liquid funds ready *before* the hammer falls.

## The Critical Distinction: Licensed vs. Unauthorised Colonies

This is where most buyers lose their money. A "Licensed Colony" is one where the developer has gone through the full GMADA drill:
- They have proven ownership of the land.
- They have paid the External Development Charges (EDC) and Internal Development Charges (IDC).
- They have obtained the Change of Land Use (CLU).

An "Unauthorised Colony" is simply a piece of agricultural land that a local "builder" has divided into small plots without any government permission. They might promise you "registry" (sale deed), but in Mohali, a registry does *not* mean the colony is legal. 

If you buy in an unauthorised colony, you will never get a [GMADA plot transfer](/blog/gmada-plot-transfer-ownership-process). When you try to sell that property five years later, you will find that no bank will fund the buyer, and your resale value will be 40% lower than the licensed plot across the road. I’ve seen families relocate from rural Punjab to Mohali, putting their life savings into these "cheap" plots, only to find they can't even get a legal water connection.

## Why the GMADA Transfer (Intkal) is Your Real Proof of Ownership

In most of India, the "Registry" at the Tehsil office is the end of the road. In GMADA sectors, it is only the halfway mark. After the registry, you must apply for a "Transfer of Ownership" in GMADA's records. 

This process generates a "Transfer Letter" or a "Permission to Transfer." This is the document that banks look for. In my practice, I’ve seen transfers get stuck for eight months because of small issues—like a missing certified copy or an OTP going to the previous owner's old phone number. We once handled a [plot recovery case](/blog/mohali-plot-cancelled-booking-recovery-process) where the builder had cancelled the allotment for non-payment by a previous owner, and it took persistent follow-up with GMADA officials to get the property reinstated and reallocated. 

Do not consider a deal closed until the property is mutated (Intkal) in the government records. You can read my full guide on the [Mohali property registration process](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026) for the step-by-step sequence.

## How to Verify GMADA Approval Status Before You Pay

Never take a broker’s word or a glossy brochure as proof. Use these three professional verification steps:

1.  **Check the RERA Punjab Website:** Every GMADA-licensed project must be registered on RERA. Search for the project at [rera.punjab.gov.in](/blog/how-to-check-rera-punjab-property). If it isn't there, walk away.
2.  **Ask for the License to Develop a Colony (LDC):** Ask the developer for a copy of the LDC issued by GMADA. Check the validity date.
3.  **Check the Allotment Letter:** If you are buying a resale plot, ask for the original Allotment Letter issued by GMADA and the subsequent Transfer Letters. 

In my years as a developer, I had to ensure every single one of these documents was water-tight before we could even announce a project. If a developer is hesitant to show you the original GMADA-approved layout plan, it is a massive red flag.

## Why GMADA Property Commands a Premium in Resale

Buyers often ask me: "Amritpal, why should I pay ₹1.5 Crore for a GMADA plot when I can get the same size in a local colony for ₹90 Lakh?"

The answer lies in liquidity and the [expansion principle](/blog/mohali-property-appreciation-vs-stagnation-zones). GMADA sectors are designed with wider roads, planned parks, and designated spaces for schools and hospitals. Most importantly, GMADA properties have "Title Absolute." This means a bank like SBI or HDFC will approve a loan in 7 days. 

When you want to exit your investment, a GMADA-approved property sells in weeks. An unauthorised one can take years to find a buyer who is willing to pay 100% cash because they can't get a loan. As someone with a capital markets background (AMFI/NCFM), I view real estate through the lens of IRR and liquidity. A 20% "saving" at the time of purchase is often a 50% loss in potential appreciation and exit speed.

## Frequently Asked Questions About GMADA Mohali

These are some of the most common questions I hear during site visits and consultations. For a more exhaustive list of 50+ property questions, you can browse our complete [Mohali real estate FAQ](/blog/mohali-real-estate-faq-2026).

### Does a Registry mean my plot is GMADA approved?
No. A registry (Sale Deed) is a record of a transaction between two parties at the Sub-Registrar's office. It does not certify that the land has been approved for residential use or that the developer has a license. Always verify the GMADA license or allotment separately.

### What are the GMADA transfer fees for a plot?
For a standard residential plot, the GMADA transfer fee typically ranges between ₹4,000 to ₹5,000, excluding the stamp duty and registration charges paid to the revenue department. However, this varies depending on the property type and size.

### Can NRIs buy GMADA plots directly?
Yes, NRIs can participate in GMADA e-auctions and buy resale GMADA properties. However, you must follow FEMA guidelines, ensuring the payment comes from an NRE/NRO account. For a detailed walkthrough, refer to our [NRI property guide for Mohali](/blog/nri-property-guide-mohali-2026).

### What happens if I build without GMADA map approval?
If your sector falls under GMADA jurisdiction, you must get your building plan (map) sanctioned before construction. Building without approval can lead to demolition notices, heavy penalties, and the refusal of a Completion Certificate (CC), which is mandatory for legal electricity and water connections.
