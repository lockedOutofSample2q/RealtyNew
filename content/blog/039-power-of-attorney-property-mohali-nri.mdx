# Power of Attorney for Property in Mohali: What It Should Say, What It Shouldn't, and Who to Trust With It

For an NRI buying or selling property in Mohali, a Power of Attorney (POA) is not just a convenience—it is often a necessity. To answer the core question: a property POA in Punjab must be a 'Special Power of Attorney' (SPA) rather than a General one to be safest, it absolutely must be registered with the Sub-Registrar if it involves the sale or transfer of immovable property, and if executed abroad, it requires an Apostille or Embassy attestation followed by adjudication in India. You should never grant a POA that allows the holder to receive sale proceeds into their personal account; all financial transactions must flow through your own NRE/NRO accounts.

In my decade of navigating Mohali’s real estate landscape—from the developer side to resolving complex title disputes—I have seen the POA be both a bridge to a successful transaction and a trap for the unwary. When you are sitting in Toronto, London, or Dubai, and your property is in Sector 82A or Aerocity, the person holding your POA is essentially "you" in the eyes of the law. This guide breaks down how to structure that power so it protects your interests rather than compromising them.

## What is the difference between a General and Specific Power of Attorney in Mohali?

The first decision you will face is whether to grant a General Power of Attorney (GPA) or a Special Power of Attorney (SPA). In the context of our [complete Mohali real estate guide 2026](/blog/mohali-real-estate-guide-2026), understanding this distinction is the foundation of your security.

A **General Power of Attorney (GPA)** gives the holder wide-ranging powers to act on your behalf across multiple domains—banking, legal cases, and property management. While it sounds efficient, it is unnecessarily risky for a single property transaction. In Punjab, a GPA is often viewed with extra scrutiny by authorities because of historical misuse.

A **Special Power of Attorney (SPA)**, on the other hand, is restricted to a specific task or property. For instance, if you are buying a luxury flat in Mullanpur, the SPA will specify the exact unit number, the developer’s name, and the specific actions allowed (e.g., signing the allotment letter, appearing before the Sub-Registrar for registration). For NRIs, I always recommend the SPA. It limits your exposure. If the document is only valid for "Property X," the holder cannot suddenly decide to use it to sell "Property Y" or withdraw funds from your fixed deposits.

## Does a Power of Attorney have to be registered in Punjab?

This is where many NRIs make a fatal mistake. Under the Registration Act, 1908, any Power of Attorney that authorizes the holder to sell, gift, exchange, or lease immovable property for a period exceeding one year **must be registered**.

If you send a signed piece of paper from Canada to your cousin in Mohali, and he takes it to the Sub-Registrar’s office to sign a Sale Deed, it will be rejected. Even worse, if a transaction is somehow pushed through on an unregistered POA, it remains legally vulnerable. I have dealt with [real estate disputes in Punjab](/blog/real-estate-disputes-legal-rights-punjab-2026) where the entire basis of the lawsuit was a "defective POA."

For an NRI, the registration process involves two steps:
1. **Execution Abroad:** You sign the document in the presence of a Notary Public or a Consular Officer at the Indian Embassy/Consulate.
2. **Adjudication in India:** Once the document arrives in India, it must be 'adjudicated' or stamped by the Financial Commissioner or the Divisional Commissioner within three months of its receipt in India. Only after this process does the POA become a valid legal instrument for property registration in Mohali.

## What should a safe NRI Power of Attorney actually say?

Specificity is your best defense. A well-drafted POA for Mohali property should be surgical. It should not say "the holder can do everything." It should list specific permissions:

1.  **The 'To Represent' Clause:** Permission to appear before GMADA, PUDA, or the Municipal Committee for signatures and document collection.
2.  **The 'Registration' Clause:** Specific authority to present documents for registration at the Sub-Registrar’s office.
3.  **The 'Financial' Limit:** This is critical. The POA should explicitly state that any payments made or received must be via the NRI’s own NRE/NRO accounts. The holder should have no power to receive cash or have cheques issued in their name.
4.  **The 'No-Substitution' Clause:** Ensure the holder cannot "delegate" this power to a third party you don't know.
5.  **The 'Expiry' Date:** If the POA is for a specific purchase, have it expire once the Registry is complete and Mutation is filed.

If your POA doesn't include these safeguards, you are essentially handing over a blank cheque. In my experience, the [NRI property guide for Mohali](/blog/nri-property-guide-mohali-2026) always emphasizes that documentation is the only thing that speaks when you are not physically present.

## What are the risks of granting a Power of Attorney to a relative or friend?

Most NRIs grant POAs to family members out of trust. While this is natural, it often leads to "informal" handling of formal assets. I have seen cases where a relative, holding a broad POA, has mortgaged an NRI's property to take a personal loan, or has settled a boundary dispute with a neighbor without the owner's consent.

The biggest risk is not just intentional fraud; it is incompetence. If the POA holder doesn't understand the [title dispute risks in Punjab](/blog/title-dispute-punjab-property-how-to-avoid), they might sign a document that unknowingly creates a lien on your property.

## How do you revoke a Power of Attorney if things go wrong?

A Power of Attorney is not permanent. If you lose trust in the holder, or if the task is completed, you must formally revoke it. 

The revocation process for a registered POA in Mohali requires:
- A formal **Deed of Revocation**.
- Registration of this revocation at the same office where the original POA was (or would have been) used.
- A public notice in at least two local newspapers (one English, one Punjabi) to inform the general public that the holder no longer represents you.
- Direct intimation to the Sub-Registrar, GMADA, and any relevant banks.

Simply "telling" the person that they no longer have power is not enough. Until the revocation is formal, their signature on a Sale Deed could still be legally binding on you.

## Why is 'Apostille' or Consulate attestation mandatory for NRIs?

If you are in a country that is part of the Hague Convention (like the UK, USA, or Australia), your POA must be **Apostilled**. This is an international certification that makes the document legally acceptable in India. If you are in a non-Hague country (like the UAE), you must get it attested by the Indian Embassy or Consulate.

Without this, the document is just a piece of paper. I’ve seen transactions in Sector 77 and Aerocity stall for months because the NRI sent a "notarized" document that wasn't Apostilled. By the time the correct paperwork arrived, the circle rates had changed or the seller had walked away.

## Who should you trust with your Power of Attorney in Mohali?

This is a question of "skin in the game." While family is the default choice, I often advise NRIs to consider the following:
- **Professional Advisors:** Not for the 'Sale' power, but for the 'Representation' power. An advisor can handle the legwork at GMADA or the Municipal Committee, while you keep the 'Sale' power with a trusted family member or reserve it for a personal visit.
- **Limited Scope Holders:** Give the power to sign utilities or maintenance to one person, and the power to register property to another.
- **The 'Visit' Rule:** For high-value transactions (SCOs on Airport Road or luxury floors), try to visit personally for the final Registry. Use the POA only for the initial Agreement to Sale and the "chasing" of NOCs.

In my years of [answering Mohali real estate FAQs](/blog/mohali-real-estate-faq-2026), the concern about trust is the most common. Trust is built on transparency, and transparency is enforced by a well-drafted legal document.

## Checklist for an NRI Power of Attorney in Mohali:
- [ ] Is it a Special Power of Attorney (SPA)?
- [ ] Does it clearly describe the property (Plot No, Sector, Dimensions)?
- [ ] Is the holder’s ID proof (Aadhar/Passport) attached or referenced?
- [ ] Are financial powers limited to your own bank accounts?
- [ ] Has it been Apostilled or Attested by the Indian Consulate?
- [ ] Have you planned for its adjudication in India within 90 days?
