## The Legal Finish Line: Possession vs Registry

**Are you the legal owner of a flat in Mohali once you have the allotment letter and transfer letter?**

No. In Punjab, legal ownership only transfers when the **[Registered Sale Deed](/blog/mohali-property-registration-process-guide-2026)** is registered at the Tehsildar's office. Understanding the **property transfer process in GMADA** or with private builders is critical. Whether you are buying [luxury flats in Mohali](/properties/flats) or [independent houses](/properties/houses), an allotment letter, a transfer letter, and a possession letter are all intermediate documents. They acknowledge a transaction. They do not confer title. Until the registry is done in your name at the Sub-Registrar office, the builder retains legal standing over that property. 

This is not a technicality buried in fine print. It is the difference between holding a receipt and holding ownership. Many first-time buyers exploring our [featured properties](/properties) do not understand this distinction until something goes wrong. By then, the damage is already underway.

## Case Study: How We Resolved a 3-Month Builder Payment Discrepancy

Gurpreet Singh, a working professional based in Chandigarh, decided to buy a flat in Mohali. It was a resale unit in a builder project with multiple active phases across the city. The original allottee had taken the flat during a pre-launch phase, paying a mix of cheque and cash.

Gurpreet did what most buyers would consider the right thing. Before paying the transfer fees, he went directly to the builder's office with the seller to verify the payment status on the unit. The builder's team reviewed the account. They said: all clear.

So Gurpreet proceeded.

He submitted all documents. He signed all forms. He paid the full purchase price to the seller. He paid the builder's transfer fees. He received a transfer letter in his own name. He received a possession letter. On paper, everything was in order.

**Then the registry date arrived.**

The builder refused to process the registry. The reason given: a payment discrepancy from the previous owner.

### What the Investigation Revealed: How to Verify Property Documents in Punjab

Resolving this took approximately 80 to 90 days. When the full picture came out, the payment discrepancy the builder had flagged was not the previous owner's problem. It was the builder's own internal accounting error. A cheque payment made by the original buyer had been realised and encashed. The funds had cleared. But the builder's team had never updated their internal ledger to reflect it.

From the builder's side: the file showed a pending balance. From the bank records: the payment had gone through months earlier. The builder had confirmed "all OK" at the time of verification because their own team had not cross-checked properly.

## The RERA Lever: How to Use It Effectively

Most first-time buyers in Mohali and Chandigarh know **[RERA Punjab](https://rerapunjab.gov.in)** exists. Very few know how to use it effectively, or how to use the threat of it before filing.

RERA Punjab, administered through rerapunjab.gov.in, gives buyers legal standing to file complaints against registered developers for delays, misrepresentation, and failure to execute conveyance. But RERA's more immediate power is in its credibility as a threat. 

A properly drafted complaint notice, referencing the project's RERA registration number and the specific contractual obligations in default, is often enough to unlock movement from a builder who has been stalling. It signals that the buyer has documentation, counsel, and intent.

### Frequently Asked Questions

For more detailed answers regarding specific sectors or financial queries, visit our main [Real Estate FAQ Hub](/faq).

#### Does an allotment letter make me the legal owner of a flat in India?
No. An allotment letter is a builder's internal document acknowledging your booking or transfer. It does not transfer legal title. Legal ownership in Punjab requires a Sale Deed registered at the Sub-Registrar's office.

#### Is a transfer letter sufficient proof of flat ownership in Chandigarh or Mohali?
No. A transfer letter records that the builder has updated their internal records to show your name. It is not a government-registered document and does not confer legal title to the property.

#### What documents are required for flat registry in Punjab?
The primary documents are the Sale Deed (executed between seller and buyer), original allotment letter chain, proof of identity, builder NOC, payment receipts, and stamp duty payment. A legal professional should verify the complete set for your specific transaction.

#### What can I do if a builder refuses to process my flat registry in Mohali?
First, obtain written documentation of the specific reason for refusal. If the project is RERA-registered, you can file a complaint at rerapunjab.gov.in. A legal notice through a practising advocate, combined with a RERA complaint filing, is the standard escalation path. In most cases, the threat of filing is enough to unlock movement.

#### How do I check if a builder's project is registered under RERA Punjab?
Visit rerapunjab.gov.in and search by project name or promoter name. All RERA-registered projects in Punjab are listed with their registration numbers, completion timelines, and complaint history.

#### Can a builder cancel my flat allotment after I have paid in full?
Yes, builders in Punjab can cancel allotments citing reasons such as non-payment of dues, payment discrepancies in their records, or violation of the Buyer's Agreement terms. This is why maintaining clear payment documentation and getting written confirmation of zero dues before each major milestone is critical. Once a RERA complaint is filed against an unjustified cancellation, the burden shifts to the builder to prove the default.

#### Can I sell my flat in Mohali if the registry has not been done yet?
You can execute another transfer through the builder, passing the allotment or transfer letter to a new buyer. However, without a registered Sale Deed, you are selling a builder-recorded entitlement, not a legally titled asset. The new buyer faces the same ownership risks you do. Most serious buyers and all home loan banks will require a registered Sale Deed before completing a purchase.

#### Can a builder legally withhold registry due to pending maintenance dues?
A builder cannot indefinitely block registry solely over maintenance dues, but they can and often do use it as leverage. Maintenance dues are a valid concern in the conveyance process, but they do not override your right to registry once the purchase price and applicable charges have been paid. If a builder is withholding the conveyance appointment over maintenance, RERA Punjab has clear provisions against this practice.

#### What is an NDC from a builder and why does every flat buyer need one?
An NDC (No Dues Certificate) is a written statement from the builder confirming that no outstanding amount is owed on a specific unit. In any resale flat transaction, obtaining the NDC from the builder before paying the seller is the single most important pre-purchase step. Without it, you have no documented proof that the unit is clear of liabilities at the time you bought it. It is the written record that protects you if the builder later claims a payment gap.

#### How long does flat registry take in Punjab after all documents are submitted?
Once all documents are in order and the Sub-Registrar appointment is confirmed, the registration itself is completed on the day of the appointment, typically within a few hours. The challenge is almost never the registry process itself. It is the pre-registry stage: clearing builder dues, obtaining NOC, resolving any ledger discrepancies, and coordinating between buyer, seller, and builder. In clean transactions, the pre-registry process takes 15 to 30 days. In disputed ones, it can stretch to 3 to 6 months.

#### Is it safe to buy a flat in a pre-launch project in Mohali?
Pre-launch purchases carry risks that RERA-registered projects do not: no RERA registration at the time of booking, no fixed completion timeline enforceable by law, and payment structures that often involve informal modes. If you choose to buy pre-launch, every payment should be through banking channels, you should obtain a written Buyer's Agreement before paying, and you should verify that the developer has clear land title and all CLU or zoning approvals in place. Pre-launch pricing can be attractive, but the documentation requirements are higher, not lower.

#### What happens to my flat if the builder goes bankrupt or the project stalls in Punjab?
If the project is RERA-registered, buyers have legal standing to approach the RERA authority for remedies including refund with interest or forced completion through an appointed agency. If the project is not RERA-registered (common in older or pre-2017 projects), buyers must approach consumer courts or civil courts, which is slower and more expensive. This is one of the primary reasons to verify RERA registration before paying anything beyond a token booking amount.

#### What is the stamp duty on a resale flat in Mohali in 2025?
In Punjab, stamp duty is 6% of the circle rate or the agreement value, whichever is higher. Registration charges are an additional 1%. When calculating **flat registry charges in Mohali 2026**, the applicable circle rate is set by the Punjab government and varies by sector. You can use our [Stamp Duty & Loan Eligibility Tool](/tools/loan-eligibility) to estimate your total acquisition cost. Always calculate stamp duty on the circle rate, not just the deal value, when budgeting for a purchase. You can also track [property price trends here](/tools/price-trend).

#### Can I get a home loan on an allotment letter or transfer letter in Mohali?
Most banks and housing finance companies will not disburse a home loan solely on the basis of an allotment or transfer letter from a builder. They typically require a registered Sale Deed or, in the case of under-construction projects, a Tripartite Agreement between the buyer, builder, and bank. You can calculate your monthly payments using our [Mortgage Calculator](/tools/mortgage-calculator). Some lenders will process partial disbursements against a BBA (Builder Buyer Agreement) in RERA-registered projects, but the final disbursement is almost always linked to possession and a registered document. Confirm with your lender before assuming the allotment letter is sufficient collateral.
 
#### If you are selling a property:
If you are a seller looking to move your asset efficiently, you can [list your property with us](/list-your-property) to ensure a smooth, technically-verified transfer for your buyer.
 
#### If the builder's employee verbally confirmed no dues, am I protected?
No. Verbal confirmations from builder staff carry no legal weight. The only confirmation that protects you is a written, signed, and ideally stamped statement from an authorised representative of the builder specifying the unit number, the buyer's name, and a zero-dues status as of a specific date. Always get this in writing before paying transfer fees.
