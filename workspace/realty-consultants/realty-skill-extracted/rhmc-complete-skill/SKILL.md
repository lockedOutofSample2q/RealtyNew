## REAL CASE STUDIES (from transcripts — use anonymised exactly as described)

### Case Study 1: The 8-Month Plot Recovery
**Situation:** Sold two plots in Mohali. Seller had not disclosed that builder had cancelled both plots due to non-payment of installment by the seller. Client had already paid 65% of the purchase price. Discovery came only when attempting to initiate transfer.
**Action:** Followed up persistently with builder to reinstate the plots. Process required 8 months of persistent chasing. One plot number had to be changed in the process (joda plots — adjacent plots). Reallocated to adjacent direction and location. Transfer finally completed.
**Lesson for buyers:** Verify at point of purchase that plots are not already cancelled or under any dispute before making any payment. Always chase transfer immediately after booking.
**Use in:** Buyer protection content, post-sale advisory content, trust-building copy, disputes category blogs

### Case Study 2: The Property Tax MOU (3 months)
**Situation:** Seller had not paid Municipal Committee property tax for several years on a constructed multi-floor building. Sold a ground floor. Back-dated tax appeared as collective liability across all floors when buyer tried to clear it.
**Action:** Contacted all upper floor owners. Drafted MOU between all three floor owners to allocate liability and settle terms. Generated separate property IDs for each floor. Transferred electricity bill to buyer's name. Completed GMADA transfer. Resolved in 3 months of persistent coordination.
**Lesson for buyers:** Always get separate property tax IDs for multi-floor buildings. Verify property tax clearance and electricity meter status before completing purchase.
**Use in:** Process blogs, MC tax content, post-sale support content, trust-building copy

### Case Study 3: The Rajpura Industrial Land Appreciation
**Situation:** 62 vigha purchased in an industrial zone near Rajpura at Rs 18.70 lakh per vigha.
**Outcome:** Six months later at time of registration, buyers approached offering Rs 33 lakh per vigha. Post-registration, rate was Rs 45 lakh per vigha. Held for further appreciation.
**Factors:** National highway proximity, declared industrial zone, Bharatmala road connectivity incoming, railway and road junction location.
**Use in:** Vision investing content, industrial investment blogs, investment thesis, HNI advisory

### Case Study 4: The Airport Road Commercial Trajectory
**Situation:** Commercial units on Airport Road in and around Sector 82 (JLPL area) were available at Rs 3-4 crore.
**Outcome:** Seated a party at Rs 11 crore when market rate was Rs 6.5-7 crore. Today those same units trade at Rs 12-16 crore.
**Factors:** Airport expansion, IT corridor growth, Bharatmala, infrastructure visibility arriving after the buy opportunity had passed.
**The insight:** "Je aapaan vision ton bina challaange taan aapaan mehngi cheez khareeddaange." If we move without vision, we will buy at the wrong price.
**Use in:** Vision investing content, sector intelligence, investment thesis, HNI advisory copy, YouTube hooks

### Case Study 5: Transcript Insights on Buyer Psychology
**From negotiation transcript:** Amritpal assessed that a prospective buyer had likely already purchased elsewhere before anyone said it explicitly. He read her engagement level, follow-through pattern, and response to pricing. Quote: "Banda jisne khareedna hota hai na vo thoda bahut interest show karta hai." ## COPYWRITING DOCTRINE

For complete 6-step copy process, format-specific rules, and the 10 unbreakable rules: read `references/voice.md`

**The one reader test:** Before writing anything, name the ICP, their dominant emotion, the one action this copy must produce, and the opening weapon.

**The universal body arc:**
Pain (named precisely) then Amplify (cost of staying here) then Shift (possibility) then Solution (with value) then Proof (specific data or case study) then Future State (reader already in outcome).

**The standard CTA template:**
"If what you read describes your situation, one 15-minute call. I will tell you directly what I would do in your position. No pitch. No pressure. Just the answer."

**Never:** Em dashes. AI filler phrases. Vague return claims. Manufactured scarcity. Two CTAs in one piece.
