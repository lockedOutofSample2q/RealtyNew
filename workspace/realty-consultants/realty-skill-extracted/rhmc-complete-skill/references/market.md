### The 5 Regulatory Bodies (direct liaisoning experience with all five)

**GMADA (Greater Mohali Area Development Authority)**
Controls: plot allotment (draw and auction), colony licensing in GMADA jurisdiction, property transfer records (mutation in GMADA areas), project oversight
Key process: transfer requires certified copy with Tehsildar stamp — WhatsApp photos not accepted. Fee approximately Rs 4,000-5,000 for basic transfers. OTP goes to last registered buyer. Takes 4-8 weeks when documents are complete.
URL: gmada.gov.in

**PUDA (Punjab Urban Development Authority)**
Controls: colony licensing outside GMADA limits, layout plan approvals, CLU (Change of Land Use), building plan sanctions in PUDA areas
Key distinction: "applied for PUDA approval" is not "PUDA approved." CLU and layout approval are separate processes. Both must be complete before a project is legitimately approved.

**PSPCL (Punjab State Power Corporation Limited)**
Controls: electrical connection sanction, load approval, metering
Key process: separate metering for each floor of a multi-floor building requires a new application, load sanction, and connection. A few weeks process. Having a separate meter is critical for NDC at resale.

**Municipal Committee**
Controls: building plan sanction, completion certificate (OC/CC), property tax IDs, mutations in Municipal Committee areas, map revision approvals
Key issue: property tax IDs can be created at building level, not floor level, causing shared liability. Getting separate IDs requires application and MOU between floor owners. FAR has increased across Mohali — map revision for additional construction goes through Municipal Committee.

**Industrial Departments, Punjab**
Controls: industrial plot permissions, factory establishment, zone classification
Zone types:
- Red zone: high-pollution industries (chemicals, pharma, heavy manufacturing, meat processing). Need nala proximity. Cannot be near residential. Large plots required.
- Green zone: low-impact industries
- Orange zone: medium-impact
Red zone investment opportunity: genuine industrial demand, larger plots required, appreciation tied to industrial corridor growth. Key areas: Rajpura, Lalru, Derabassi, Kurali, Morinda.

### RERA Punjab

URL: rera.punjab.gov.in
What to check: project registration number, promoter details and past project history, project status, complaint count and type, completion certificate status.
Complaint filing: types are possession delay, deficiency in service, false information. Documents needed: booking letter, payment receipts, agreement to sell, possession letter. Adjudicating officer process then RERA authority. Timeline target 60 days, reality 6-12 months.

### Key Infrastructure Corridors and Appreciation Drivers

**Bharatmala Road:** National highway connecting Airport Road toward Patiala, bypassing Rajpura/Banur. Reduces distance from Mohali airport to Rajpura industrial zone from approximately 30km to 16-17km. Properties along this corridor expected to appreciate significantly at completion.

**Airport expansion:** Chandigarh International Airport expanding capacity and routes. Captive demand driver for Airport Road commercial. Hotel, transit, and corporate demand all tied to airport growth.

**IT City / Kharar IT Corridor:** Growing IT sector presence beyond core Mohali. Price gap vs Sector 82A presents potential appreciation opportunity. Corporate tenant demand increasing.

**New Chandigarh / Mullanpur:** Government master plan 2031. Zoned for residential and mixed-use expansion. Infrastructure still catching up to marketing — verify OC and connectivity before buying.

### Stagnation vs Appreciation Principle

From transcript: areas where expansion is possible appreciate. Areas where expansion is not possible stagnate. Old established commercial corridors (3B2 market, Phase 7 market) — stagnation, price fixation, rental value down. New expanding corridors — new companies, new trends, new projects, all arriving. When old market stagnates, it is because everything that was going to come has already arrived.

### Capital Gains Laws (Land Sellers)

**Section 10(37):** Capital gains on agricultural land compulsorily acquired by government — exempt for individuals and HUFs if land used for agricultural purposes. Most Punjab road/infrastructure acquisitions qualify.

**Section 54F:** Long-term capital gain from any asset reinvested in residential property — exempt if reinvested within 2 years (purchase) or 3 years (construction).

**Capital Gains Account Scheme:** Proceeds parked in designated bank account to preserve exemption while reinvestment decision is made.

**Practical note:** The 24-month window is the most important frame for Land Seller conversations. Not unlimited time. The money should be parked in the Capital Gains Account Scheme, not in an FD.

### FEMA (NRI Property Purchase)

Residential property purchase permitted without RBI approval. Agricultural land, farmhouse, plantation require RBI permission. Payment must route through NRO or NRE account. Repatriation: up to USD 1 million per year through NRO account, requires Form 15CA and 15CB.

### Section 138 NI Act (Cheque Bouncing)

Dishonoured cheque is a criminal offence. Payee must send 30-day written notice before filing complaint. Complaint at magistrate court within 30 days of notice expiry. Outcomes: settlement (most common), conviction (fine plus imprisonment up to 2 years).

### RTI (Right to Information Act 2005)

Application to Public Information Officer of relevant authority. Fee: Rs 10 application plus Rs 2 per page. Timeline: 30 days. Useful for: PUDA approval status, GMADA allotment records, Municipal Committee sanctions, forest clearance status, revenue department land records.
