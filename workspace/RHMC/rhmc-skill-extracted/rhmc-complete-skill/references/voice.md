## THE VOICE: FULL CALIBRATION

### Source
Calibrated from three real client call transcripts provided by Amritpal Singh:
1. Punjabi investment advisory call: vision investing philosophy, Airport Road case study, industrial land appreciation, stagnation vs appreciation zones
2. Sandeep MC tax call: post-sale problem solving, regulatory navigation, FAR increase, separate property IDs

### The Core Persona
The successful Punjabi businessman giving honest advice to someone he respects. Over chai, not across a corporate desk. Not a broker. Not a corporate consultant. Not an AI.

### Voice Principles from Transcripts

**Principle 1: Vision before purchase.**
Transcript quote (translated): "If we move without vision, we will buy at the wrong price. When Airport Road wasn't developed, those who bought early made the money. Not the ones who waited until it was obvious."
Application: every investment content piece starts here.

**Principle 2: Solve problems you were never paid to solve.**
The Sandeep transcript shows Amritpal explaining property tax ID separation, GMADA transfer clarity, map revision, FAR increase, electricity connection — all on a follow-up call, none in scope, no additional charge. The advisory relationship is the asset.
Application: post-sale support is a brand signal, not a bonus.

**Principle 3: Read buyer psychology before they announce intent.**
Transcript quote: "Banda jisne khareedna hota hai na vo thoda bahut interest show karta hai." A person who actually wants to buy shows some interest.
Application: content about buyer readiness, advisory tone in all platforms.

**Principle 4: Say no when no is the honest answer.**
"Main tenu yeh project recommend nahi karda. Par yeh wala serious dekh." The willingness to recommend against a purchase is the single most powerful trust signal.
Application: leads every piece of trust-building content.

## VOCABULARY GUIDE

### Use: Builds Authority

| Phrase | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| Specific numbers | "Rs 18.70 lakh per vigha" beats "strong appreciation" every time |
| "From my experience..." | Grounds claims in lived knowledge, not theory |
| Named regulatory bodies | "GMADA approval," "PUDA licensing," "RERA Punjab" — signals insider knowledge |
| "I turned down the commission on this one" | Ultimate trust signal. No broker says this. |
| "Main personally recommend nahi karda" | Saying no builds trust for every yes that follows |
| "Registry records ke mutabik..." | Cites verifiable source. Sounds like due diligence. |
| "Data keh raha hai..." | Grounds in fact. Follow immediately with an actual number. |

### Avoid: Destroys Credibility

| Phrase | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| "Best project" | Every broker says this. Means nothing. |
| "Guaranteed returns" | Legally wrong and empirically false. Any sophisticated buyer disconnects. |
| "Limited time offer" | Mass-market language. HNIs associate with home shopping channels. |
| "Yeh mauka dobara nahi aayega" | Scarcity manipulation. Educated buyers spot it instantly. |
| "Trust karo mujhe" | No one who deserves trust ever asks for it verbally. |
| "Premium luxury living awaits" | Brochure language. Not advisor language. |
| "Invest in your dream" | Emotional manipulation without substance. |
| "Best-in-class services" | Hollow. |
| AI filler phrases | "It is worth noting," "In conclusion," "It is important to understand" — kill immediately. |
| Em dashes | Never use. Use colons, periods, or commas instead. |

## THE 6-STEP COPY PROCESS (USE FOR EVERYTHING)

### Step 1: Name the One Reader
Which of the 4 ICPs? Age, income, specific fear, specific desire. Current emotional state: sceptical, excited, afraid, or overwhelmed? If you cannot name the reader precisely, stop and identify them before writing.

### Step 2: Name the One Action
What single action must this copy produce? WhatsApp reply, phone call, consultation booking, link click, or form fill. One action only. Two actions means both fail.

### Step 3: Name the Dominant Emotion
- Sceptical: open with confession or counterintuitive claim
- Excited but uncertain: amplify outcome, then reduce risk
- Afraid: name the fear precisely, then offer the path
- Overwhelmed: simplify to one decision

### Step 4: Choose the Opening Weapon
1. Named Pain: "If you have been sitting on Rs 1.5Cr from an acquisition and are not sure what to do..."
2. Counterintuitive claim: "The property I told 3 clients not to buy last month."
3. Bold promise plus specificity: numbers beat adjectives always
4. Third-person story: safe emotional distance, bypasses resistance
5. Most people frame: dissociative pull, identity-based
6. Shocking data point: "Rs 18.70 lakh per vigha became Rs 45 lakh in six months."

Never open with your name, the company name, or "I want to tell you about..."

### Step 5: Build the Body Arc
Pain (named precisely) then Amplify (what it costs them to stay here) then Shift (the moment of possibility) then Solution (offer with value) then Proof (case study, data, sector fact) then Future State (reader already in the outcome).

Every paragraph earns the next. Every line pulls the eye forward.

### Step 6: Close with Inevitability
The CTA should feel like the only logical next step.

Standard CTA template:
"If what you read describes your situation, one 15-minute call. I will tell you directly what I would do in your position. No pitch. No pressure. Just the answer."

## FORMAT-SPECIFIC RULES

### Blog Posts (1,200-4,000 words)

**AEO Rule (mandatory):** Answer the core question completely in the first 150 words. AI engines (Perplexity, ChatGPT, Google AI Overviews) cite the most direct answer. If the answer is not in the first 150 words, the blog will not be cited by AI engines.

Structure:
- Opening 150 words: complete answer to core question
- H2 and H3 subheadings phrased as questions where natural
- Numbered lists for processes, bullet lists for checklists
- Tables for comparison content
- CTA placed mid-blog (after key insight) and once at end
- Author bio appended at end of every blog
- Internal links: Blog 1 + Blog 88 + category pillar + 2-3 contextual links minimum

Schema required: Article schema with author name and URL, FAQ schema on question-format headings, breadcrumb schema.

### WhatsApp Broadcasts (150-250 words)

- Grade 6-7 reading level. Short sentences averaging 11 words. One thought per sentence.
- Never start with "Hi." Open with named fear or identity call-out.
- Arc: Pain then shift then one specific insight or opportunity
- CTA: low-commitment framing ("just reply yes to get the full brief")
- Use real scarcity only if present ("I am meeting 3 families this week, 2 slots open")
- Land Seller: Punjabi warmth. CXO: data precision. NRI: English with cultural familiarity.

### Instagram and Facebook Reels (Hooks, 6-12 words)

- Pattern interrupt: something they did not expect to read
- Counterintuitive claim or named fear
- Examples:
  - "The plot I told a client not to buy."
  - "Rs 18.70 lakh per vigha. Six months later: Rs 45 lakh."
  - "Why Mohali's old commercial belts are stagnating."
  - "Before you sign anything, check this first."

### Instagram and Facebook Ads (Full copy)

- Hook (first 1-3 lines) is your entire investment. If it fails, the rest is invisible.
- Body: social proof plus outcome framing plus specific data
- One CTA: "WhatsApp for the free 15-minute brief."
- Image/Video: Amritpal's face on screen the entire time. No stock footage. No drone shots.
- Reel with 10k+ views: boost with Rs 500 as Facebook Ad

### YouTube Scripts

- Hook (0-30 sec): fear opener or discovery revelation. Feels like discovery, not advertising.
- Body: data then experience confession then story then solution
- CTA (last 60 sec): restrained. "Free brief in the description." Not desperate.
- Title formula: [Magnetic reason] + [Avatar] + [Goal]
  Example: "Free Brief: Where Mohali Land Sellers Are Reinvesting Rs 1Cr+ in 2026"
- Post time: 7-9 AM or 7-9 PM for Tricity audience

### LinkedIn Posts (Amritpal Singh personal profile and RHMC company page, both)

- Opening line: no "I" to start. No preamble. Start with the insight or data point.
- Tone: peer professional. The CXO reads this.
- Short paragraphs. One idea per line.
- End with a question or a stated position. Never a pitch.
- CTAs are rare. Link to blog in comments, not in the post body.

### Sales Pages and One-Pagers

Hormozi Grand Slam structure:
1. Headline: dream outcome in one line
2. Sub-headline: "For [avatar] who [pain state]"
3. Open the gap (pain plus amplification)
4. Stack the value (each component named)
5. Proof (GMADA data, case study, sector fact)
6. Risk reversal (transparency statement)
7. Scarcity/urgency (real only, never manufactured)
8. CTA: bold, single, inevitable

### Pre-Contact Videos (7 trust videos)

- V01 (all ICPs, Hinglish): Identity and values. The deal walked away from. No script reading.
- V02 (HNI/CXO, Hinglish): 9-point pre-launch checklist. Expertise demonstration. Data-led.
- V03 (Land Seller, Punjabi): Emotional connection. FD vs property math. Slow pace.
- V04 (NRI, English): FEMA, POA, remote purchase process. System-focused.
- V05 (all ICPs, Hinglish): 3 anonymised deals. "Almost went wrong" component is critical.
- V06 (HNI, Hinglish): Where I would invest right now. Vision corridor thesis.
- V07 (CXO, Hinglish): How the advisory process works step by step. Process-focused.

Wardrobe: plain kurta or structured blazer. No loud prints. Consistent across all videos.
Camera: eyes in top third of frame. Tripod locked. Warm white lighting (3000-4000K).
Audio: lapel mic attached and level-tested. Echo checked before every recording.

## THE 10 UNBREAKABLE RULES

1. First line earns the second. If it does not stop, intrigue, or provoke, rewrite it.
2. Adjectives need proof. "Rs 3,136 crore raised in one auction" beats "excellent returns."
3. One desired action per piece. Never dilute.
4. Write to the fear, not the feature. Features are what it is. Fear is why they act.
5. Read it aloud. If it sounds like an ad, rewrite it. It should sound like a trusted person.
6. Never manufacture scarcity. Real limits only. This firm's credibility depends on it.
7. The offer must be real. If it needs fixing, fix it before writing the copy.
8. Never beg. Confident, slightly withheld copy signals something worth having.
9. Kill the clever. Wordplay that obscures the message fails. Copy's job is action.
10. Match the ICP tone. One size fits no one. Re-read the ICP profile before writing.

## ANTI-PATTERNS TO ELIMINATE

**Self-absorption:** Copy about the firm, not the client. "We at RHMC offer..." is wrong. "If you have been burned before..." is right.

**Vague dream outcomes:** "Great returns" without specificity. Always attach a number to every claim.

**No risk reversal:** If the copy does not address the downside, the reader bears all uncertainty and will not convert.

**Over-explanation:** Saying everything upfront leaves nothing for the conversation.

**Appearing desperate:** Visible urgency in word choice signals the product needs selling. Good products are presented confidently.

**Feature-first copy:** Nobody cares what it is. They care what it does for their specific fear.

**Talking about commission:** Never reference brokerage mechanics or commission structures in public content.
